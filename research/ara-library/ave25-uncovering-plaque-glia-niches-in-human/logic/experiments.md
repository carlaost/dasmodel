# Experiments

Declarative verification/analysis plans reconstructed from the paper's Methods and Results.
Directional only — no exact numerical results (those live in `evidence/`).

## E01: Aβ-load-stratified pseudobulk differential expression analysis
- **Verifies**: C01, C02
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: Linear mixed model (LMM) on TMM-voom-normalized pseudobulk log2 CPM expression
  - Hardware: Not specified in paper (standard R/Bioconductor compute environment implied)
  - Dataset: 258,987 Visium ST spots, 78 sections, 21 ROSMAP donors (13 AD, 8 NCI), gray-matter spots only, stratified by Aβ IHC-intensity niche (no/low/high) and further split by glia stratum (glia-low/glia-high)
  - System: Per-section pseudobulk generated by summing spot UMI counts within a niche type; sections with <50 spots for a given niche type excluded
- **Procedure**:
  1. Assign each ST spot to a plaque-glia niche using adjacent-section IHC intensity thresholds (Aβ: no/low/high; glia: high/low)
  2. Sum UMI counts of all gray-matter spots in a niche type per section to build pseudobulk estimates; keep genes with CPM>1 in ≥80% of sections
  3. TMM-normalize (edgeR) and voom-transform (limma) pseudobulk estimates
  4. Fit LMM per gene contrasting low-Aβ vs. high-Aβ (combined, and separately within glia-low and glia-high strata), with age, RIN, batch, AD status as fixed effects and donor as random effect
  5. Apply FDR correction across all genes and contrasts; declare significance at FDR<0.05
- **Metrics**: Number of FDR-significant DEGs per contrast; overlap (Venn) of DEG sets across contrasts; per-gene effect size / t-statistic
- **Expected outcome**:
  - Low-Aβ vs. high-Aβ contrasts yield distinct DEG sets depending on glia stratum, with only partial overlap across combined/glia-low/glia-high contrasts
  - Low-Aβ spots show relatively higher expression of apoptosis/immune genes and lower expression of neuronal/synaptic genes than high-Aβ spots
- **Baselines**: Comparison is among the three niche contrasts themselves (combined vs. glia-low vs. glia-high); no external baseline dataset
- **Dependencies**: none

## E02: GSEA of Aβ-load contrasts against GO terms and glial gene modules
- **Verifies**: C04
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: GSEA (Subramanian et al. 2005) using per-gene t-values from E01's LMM contrasts as ranking scores
  - Dataset: Same pseudobulk contrasts as E01
  - Genesets: GO terms/canonical pathways (via gprofiler); mouse-derived PIG, OLIG, DAM, DAA gene modules; 12 human AD microglial state genesets (MG0-MG12, from Sun et al. 2023); in-house iMGL/astrocyte/neuron Aβ-response marker genesets (from the co-culture scRNA-seq, see E10)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Rank genes within each Aβ contrast by LMM t-statistic
  2. Run GSEA against each geneset collection, computing normalized enrichment scores and BH-FDR-corrected p-values
  3. Compare enrichment patterns across combined/glia-low/glia-high Aβ contrasts
- **Metrics**: Normalized enrichment score (NES); signed −log10(adjusted p) per geneset per contrast
- **Expected outcome**:
  - Reduced synaptic/ion-transport pathway enrichment and elevated apoptosis/immune/gliogenesis pathway enrichment in low-Aβ contrasts, more pronounced under glia-high
  - Positive enrichment of PIG, OLIG, DAM, and DAA modules under both glia strata
  - Enrichment of most human MG states (except homeostatic/surveillance states), with the DAM-like state showing the strongest, most consistent enrichment
- **Baselines**: Cross-comparison among the three Aβ-contrast conditions; cross-referencing against independently derived mouse and human gene-module definitions
- **Dependencies**: E01

## E03: IHC validation of apoptosis near low- vs. high-Aβ plaques
- **Verifies**: C02
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: Negative binomial mixed model (NEBULA) with cleaved caspase-3 (CASP3) puncta count as response
  - Dataset: FFPE DLPFC sections from 10 additional AD individuals (distinct from the 21-donor ST cohort), stained for DAPI, Aβ (4G8), NEUN, and cleaved caspase-3
  - System: Region of interest (ROI) defined per 4G8-positive plaque object as nuclei+/NeuN+ areas within the object plus a 25 µm surrounding ring; top-25%/bottom-75% scaled 4G8 intensity split defines high-/low-Aβ objects (consistent with the ST stratification criteria)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Identify and classify 4G8-positive plaque objects as high- or low-Aβ by scaled intensity
  2. Count cleaved-caspase-3 puncta within NeuN+ nuclei and within total nuclei per ROI
  3. Fit NEBULA model with caspase-3 count as response, NeuN+ (or total DAPI+) nuclei count as offset, age at death and sex as fixed effects, subject as random effect
  4. Compare low-Aβ vs. high-Aβ objects with a paired t-test on per-individual average counts
- **Metrics**: Average CASPASE3+ puncta count per ROI, normalized by NeuN+ or total nuclei count
- **Expected outcome**: Higher caspase-3-positive puncta density near low-Aβ plaques than high-Aβ plaques, corroborating the ST-derived apoptosis-pathway enrichment (E01/E02)
- **Baselines**: Low-Aβ vs. high-Aβ plaque objects within the same 10 individuals (paired design)
- **Dependencies**: none

## E04: Ligand-receptor (NICHES) analysis of Aβ-load contrasts
- **Verifies**: C06
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: NICHES niche-matrix construction (Omnipath LR reference, k=4 nearest-neighbor imputation) followed by mixed linear models with duplicateCorrelation for repeated donors
  - Dataset: Gray-matter ST spots from sections with ≥50 spots per stratification group
  - System: Section-averaged, stratification-averaged LR matrices, quantile-voom normalized/transformed
- **Procedure**:
  1. Construct per-spot NICHES niche matrices summing LR co-expression signal from spatial neighbors
  2. Average LR values by section and Aβ stratification group (no/low/high)
  3. Fit mixed linear models contrasting low-Aβ vs. no-Aβ and low-Aβ vs. high-Aβ, with BH-FDR correction
  4. Correlate the two contrasts' t-statistics; run GSEA/GO enrichment on differentially expressed LR genes (ligand and receptor sides)
- **Metrics**: t-statistic per LR pair; correlation coefficient (R) between contrasts; GO-term enrichment −log10(adjusted p)
- **Expected outcome**: Concordant differential LR signal between the low-vs-no and low-vs-high Aβ contrasts, enriched for synapse/neuron-differentiation/ECM pathways, plus a low-Aβ-specific signal for vesicle-transport and immune/MAPK-pathway LR pairs
- **Baselines**: No-Aβ and high-Aβ spots serve as the reference groups for the two respective contrasts
- **Dependencies**: E01

## E05: Glia-abundance-stratified pseudobulk differential expression analysis
- **Verifies**: C03
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: Same LMM/pseudobulk framework as E01, applied to the glia-high vs. glia-low contrast
  - Dataset: Gray-matter ST spots, contrasted combined and within low-Aβ / high-Aβ strata; additionally a finer 6-way GFAP/IBA1-intensity stratification (e.g., HHL, HLH, HLL, LHL, LLH, LLL) was attempted
  - System: Pseudobulk per section per niche type, TMM-voom normalized
- **Procedure**:
  1. Assign spots to glia-high/glia-low using IBA1/GFAP intensity thresholds, combined and within each Aβ stratum
  2. Build and normalize pseudobulk estimates per section/niche
  3. Fit LMM contrasting glia-high vs. glia-low (combined, low-Aβ, high-Aβ), with FDR correction
  4. Separately attempt finer 6-way Aβ×GFAP×IBA1 stratification contrasts, retaining only sections with ≥50 spots per group
- **Metrics**: Number of FDR-significant DEGs per contrast; per-gene effect size/t-statistic
- **Expected outcome**: Glia-high spots show higher glial-marker and lower neuronal/synaptic-marker expression than glia-low spots across Aβ strata; the finer 6-way stratification is expected to be underpowered for most group comparisons given limited per-group donor counts
- **Baselines**: Glia-low spots as reference within each Aβ stratum
- **Dependencies**: none

## E06: GSEA of glia-abundance contrasts against GO terms and glial gene modules
- **Verifies**: C03, C04
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: GSEA using per-gene t-values from E05's LMM contrasts
  - Genesets: Same GO/canonical-pathway, PIG/OLIG/DAM/DAA, and human MG0-MG12 genesets as E02
- **Procedure**:
  1. Rank genes within each glia contrast by LMM t-statistic
  2. Run GSEA against each geneset collection
  3. Compare enrichment across combined/low-Aβ/high-Aβ glia contrasts
- **Metrics**: NES; signed −log10(adjusted p)
- **Expected outcome**: Negative enrichment for synapse/exocytic-vesicle/ion-channel pathways and positive enrichment for immune/inflammatory/complement/cytokine pathways in glia-high spots; positive enrichment of PIG/OLIG/DAM/DAA and most human MG states, with the DAM-like state most strongly enriched
- **Baselines**: Cross-comparison among Aβ-stratified glia contrasts
- **Dependencies**: E05

## E07: IHC validation of synaptic/neuronal loss and neuroinflammation near glia-high vs. glia-low plaques
- **Verifies**: C03
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: NEBULA (NeuN count as response) and linear mixed models (synaptophysin/CD68 intensity as response)
  - Dataset: FFPE DLPFC sections from 10 AD individuals, stained for Aβ/GFAP/IBA1 plus synaptophysin (SYP) or NeuN (separate panel), and a further panel with CD68
  - System: 25 µm ring ROI around each 4G8-positive plaque object; glia-high/glia-low classification by top-25%/remainder scaled GFAP/IBA1 intensity (consistent with ST criteria)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Classify plaque objects as glia-high or glia-low by scaled GFAP/IBA1 intensity
  2. Quantify SYP intensity, NeuN+ proportion/count, and CD68 abundance within the surrounding ROI
  3. Fit NEBULA (NeuN count response, nuclei/area offset) and linear mixed models (SYP, CD68 intensity response) with age/sex fixed effects and subject random effect
  4. Compare glia-high vs. glia-low with paired t-tests on per-individual averages
- **Metrics**: SYP average intensity; NeuN+ proportion and count; CD68 average intensity
- **Expected outcome**: Reduced SYP and NeuN staining and increased CD68 abundance surrounding glia-high plaques relative to glia-low plaques, especially for low-Aβ plaques
- **Baselines**: Glia-low plaque objects within the same 10 individuals (paired design)
- **Dependencies**: none

## E08: Ligand-receptor (NICHES) analysis of glia-abundance contrasts
- **Verifies**: C07
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: Same NICHES + mixed linear model framework as E04, applied to glia-high vs. glia-low contrasts
  - Dataset: Gray-matter ST spots from sections with ≥50 spots per stratification group, under low-Aβ, high-Aβ, and combined conditions
- **Procedure**:
  1. Construct per-spot NICHES niche matrices as in E04
  2. Average LR values by section and glia stratification group within each Aβ condition
  3. Fit mixed linear models contrasting glia-high vs. glia-low under low-Aβ and high-Aβ conditions, with BH-FDR correction
  4. Correlate the two conditions' t-statistics; run GO enrichment on differentially expressed LR genes
- **Metrics**: t-statistic per LR pair; correlation coefficient (R); GO-term enrichment −log10(adjusted p)
- **Expected outcome**: Concordant downregulation of synaptic-pathway LR pairs in glia-high spots across both Aβ conditions, with additional immune/TREM2 and ECM LR upregulation and TGF-β1 LR downregulation specific to the high-Aβ condition
- **Baselines**: Glia-low spots as reference within each Aβ condition
- **Dependencies**: E05

## E09: Plaque-type validation of Aβ-intensity stratification across two independent cohorts
- **Verifies**: C05
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: Fisher's exact test (odds ratio, p-value) on manually annotated plaque-type proportions
  - Dataset 1 (primary): 781 spots from ST-adjacent frozen sections, manually annotated by plaque type
  - Dataset 2 (replication): 722 plaques manually annotated (by an investigator blinded to HCA measurements) from FFPE DLPFC sections of 9 AD ROSMAP participants (distinct from the 21-donor ST cohort)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Manually classify plaques/objects as diffuse, compact, or dense-core (excluding staining artifacts/vessel Aβ)
  2. Stratify plaques into low-/high-Aβ groups by scaled intensity (781-spot cohort: no/low/high tertile scheme; 722-plaque FFPE cohort: bottom-75%/top-25% split)
  3. Compute the proportion of plaque types within each Aβ stratum and test the diffuse-vs-compact/dense-core association with Aβ stratum via Fisher's exact test
- **Metrics**: Odds ratio (OR) and p-value for the plaque-type-by-Aβ-stratum association
- **Expected outcome**: Low-Aβ spots/objects enriched for diffuse plaques and high-Aβ spots/objects enriched for compact/dense-core plaques, in both the frozen-section and independent FFPE cohorts
- **Baselines**: Cross-cohort replication (frozen ST-adjacent sections vs. independent FFPE sections) serves as the validation baseline
- **Dependencies**: none

## E10: iPSC-derived multicellular co-culture Aβ-oligomer treatment with scRNA-seq cross-validation against the ST glial signature
- **Verifies**: C08, C09
- **Setup**:
  - Model/analysis: Wilcoxon rank-sum test (Bonferroni-corrected) for marker/DEG identification; GSEA for cross-validation against ST-derived signatures
  - Dataset: iPSC-derived microglia-like cells (iMGL, line AICS-0090-391) co-cultured with iPSC-derived astrocyte/neuron cultures (from NPCs derived from line BR28) for 72 h, then treated with 50 µg/mL oligomeric Aβ1-42 (or vehicle) for 24 h; scRNA-seq (10x Genomics 3' v3, NovaSeq 6000, ~50k reads/cell) on 13,740 cells after QC/doublet removal
  - System: Unsupervised Louvain clustering (SNN graph, neighbors=20, resolution=0.3) on top 15 PCs, for both whole-co-culture cell typing and Aβ-treated-iMGL-only subclustering (3,875 cells)
- **Procedure**:
  1. Cluster co-culture cells and annotate by canonical markers (iMGL, astrocyte, neuron, cycling progenitor, other progenitor)
  2. Identify Aβ-treated-vs-control DEGs within each annotated cell type (neurons, astrocytes, iMGLs) via Wilcoxon rank-sum test
  3. Run GSEA on the ST glia-high-vs-glia-low signature (from E05) using each cell type's Aβ-upregulated DEGs as the geneset; compare enrichment across cell types
  4. Subcluster Aβ-treated iMGLs (iMGL1-4); identify per-subcluster marker genes; run GSEA of the ST glial signature using each subcluster's markers as genesets, and cross-reference each subcluster's Aβ-vs-control DEGs against the 12 human MG-state genesets
- **Metrics**: Number of Aβ-response DEGs per cell type/subcluster; GSEA normalized enrichment score / signed −log10(adjusted p) for the ST signature and for each MG state, per cell type/subcluster
- **Expected outcome**: The ST-derived glial response signature is enriched specifically in iMGL (not astrocyte or neuron) Aβ-response genes; among iMGL subclusters, a subset (particularly one resembling the DAM-like human MG state) shows the strongest correspondence to the in vivo glial signature
- **Baselines**: Vehicle-treated cells of the same type/subcluster as the internal control; astrocyte and neuron Aβ-response genesets as negative-control comparators for the iMGL-specific enrichment
- **Dependencies**: E05, E06
