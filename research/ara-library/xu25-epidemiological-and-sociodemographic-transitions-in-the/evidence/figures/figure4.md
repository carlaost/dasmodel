## Cross-check against in-text values (grounding the transcription)
The Results text states: "the ADOD burden attributed to high FPG were highest in High-income North America, with ASDRs of 5.92 per100,000 in females and 5.34 per100,000 in males, respectively" (p.11). The heatmap's "High-income North America" column at row "2021, High FPG, Female" = **5.9** and row "2021, High FPG, Male" = **5.3** — matching the in-text values at the heatmap's printed 1-decimal precision. This cross-validates both the column alignment (27-column header order) and the row-value transcription above.

## Trend summary
The heatmap confirms, with exact values, the directional findings elaborated in Figure 3: every "Smoking" row's values are lower in 2021 than the corresponding 1990 row for most areas (e.g., Global Both-sexes: 1.1 → 0.8), while every "High BMI" and "High FPG" row rises from 1990 to 2021 for nearly every area (e.g., Global High FPG Both sexes: 2.6 → 3.7; Global High BMI Both sexes: 1.2 → 1.8). High FPG is consistently the largest-magnitude risk factor in both years (values up to 5.9 in 2021 vs. a maximum of 4.1 for High BMI/1990-FPG and 2.9 for Smoking). Female values exceed male values in almost every High BMI and High FPG cell (e.g., 2021 Global High FPG: Female 4.0 vs. Male 3.3), while male values exceed female values in almost every Smoking cell (e.g., 2021 Global Smoking: Male 1.4 vs. Female 0.4) — both patterns match the paper's stated text ("the ASDR caused by smoking was higher in males than in females, the ASDRs due to high FPG and BMI were much higher in females").
