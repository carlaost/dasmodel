## Plot description
Three side-by-side line-chart panels ("Both sexes", "Male", "Female"), each plotting ASIR (per 100,000; Y axis, linear scale, panel-specific range) against calendar year 1989ŌĆō2022 (X axis, linear scale) for six series: Global, High SDI, High-middle SDI, Middle SDI, Low-middle SDI, Low SDI. Each series is a joinpoint-segmented line (dots = observed annual ASIR, colored line = fitted joinpoint segments). Below each panel is a legend giving the fitted APC (annual percentage change) with `*` marking P<0.05, and the year range of each fitted segment, per series.

- **Axes**: X = year, no units, scale: linear, range 1989ŌĆō2022. Y = ASIR (per 100,000), scale: linear; range differs by panel (Both sexes 85ŌĆō135; Male 74ŌĆō114; Female 98ŌĆō143).

## Trend summary
Global "both sexes" ASIR rises slightly overall (segments alternate up/down, sharp late uptick 2019-2021). High SDI is the only series with a sustained decline across nearly every segment (culminating in a ŌłÆ0.38* drop 2019-2021). High-middle and Middle SDI are the only two series with a clearly positive overall trajectory, each showing an accelerating rise into 2011-2021 (up to +1.57* and +1.99* in the final 2019-2021 segment). Low-middle and Low SDI decline steadily through most of the period but then show a sharp late-segment increase in 2019-2021 (+1.08*, +0.56*) that does not reverse their overall 1990-2021 decline (Table 2: ŌłÆ0.10* and ŌłÆ0.14* respectively). This pattern ŌĆö high SDI declining, high-middle/middle SDI rising, low/low-middle declining overall but upticking at the very end ŌĆö holds directionally across all three sex panels, with the female panel's absolute ASIR values highest overall and the male panel's lowest, consistent with Table 1.
