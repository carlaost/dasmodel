## Plot description
A 3×3 grid of line charts. Rows = Smoking-ASDR, High BMI-ASDR, High FPG-ASDR (Y axis, per 100,000, linear scale, panel-specific range). Columns = Both, Female, Male. X axis in every panel = year, 1990-2021, linear. Each panel has six lines (one per area: Global, High SDI, High-middle SDI, Middle SDI, Low-middle SDI, Low SDI), colored per the shared legend at right of each row.

- **Axes**: X = year, linear, 1990-2021. Y = ASDR (per 100,000), linear; ranges: Smoking-ASDR ≈0-3 (Both/Female) and ≈0-2 (Male, larger scale ~0-2.1); High BMI-ASDR ≈0-3 (Both/Female) and ≈0-1.8 (Male); High FPG-ASDR ≈1-5.5 (Both/Female) and ≈1.5-4 (Male).

## Trend summary
Across all three risk factors, the "High SDI" series is the highest line in almost every panel and the "Low SDI" series the lowest, consistent with the heatmap detail in Figure 4. Smoking-attributable ASDR declines steadily 1990→2021 in every area/sex combination; High BMI- and High FPG-attributable ASDR rise steadily (with FPG showing a mid-period plateau in several series) — this directional pattern (smoking down, BMI/FPG up) is the same qualitative finding elaborated with exact 1990 vs. 2021 values in Figure 4. Because no data labels are printed on the line curves, per-year exact values cannot be recovered from this figure; readings above are directional estimates only (reading confidence: medium).
