## Trend summary
Neurotransmitter receptors (39 agent-slots) is the single largest CADRO class, followed by
inflammation/immune (29) and amyloid-beta (26). Amyloid activity is spread across all three phases
(and is the only class with a large Phase 3 presence besides transmitter receptors), inflammation is
concentrated in Phases 1–2, and tau is entirely in Phases 1–2 (no Phase 3). Note: these are
**per-phase agent counts** and an agent assessed in more than one phase contributes to multiple
segments, so row totals run ~1 higher than the once-per-pipeline totals in §3.1 (e.g., transmitter
receptors 39 here vs 38 drugs (24%); amyloid 26 vs 25 (16%); inflammation 29 vs 28 (18%); tau 16 vs
15 (9%)).
