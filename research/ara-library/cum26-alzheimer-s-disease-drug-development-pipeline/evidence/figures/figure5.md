## Trend summary
Panel A: both trials and agents rise over the decade — trials from ≈129 (2017) to 192 (2026,
~+35% since 2017 per §3.5) and agents from ≈94 to 158 (~+40%), with a dip in 2020 and 2024 and a
strong rise into 2026. Panel B: the amyloid share falls sharply from ≈33% (2017) to ≈15% (2020),
then stabilizes around ~18–21%; inflammation/immune rises steadily from ≈6% to ≈18–20%; the
inflammation and amyloid shares have been broadly stable since 2022.

**Text-vs-figure discrepancy (flagged):** §3.5 states "tau targeted agents have increased from 6% to
approximately 20%", but the plotted **Tau** line in Panel B rises only to ≈9–10% by 2019 and then
declines to ≈6–9% through 2026 — it does NOT reach ~20% in this figure. The amyloid (33%→≈18%) and
inflammation (6%→≈18%) endpoints are consistent with the text; the tau endpoint is not. C10 quotes
the text statement verbatim; readers should note this apparent inconsistency in the source.
