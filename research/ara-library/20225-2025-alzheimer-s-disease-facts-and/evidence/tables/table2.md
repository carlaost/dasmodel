# Table 2 - Signs of Alzheimer's Dementia Compared With Typical Age-Related Changes

**Source**: Table 2, §2.4 (p.7) in 2025 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures
**Caption**: "Signs of Alzheimer's Dementia Compared With Typical Age-Related Changes.* (*For more information about the symptoms of Alzheimer's, visit alz.org.)"
**Screenshot**: table2.png
**Extraction type**: raw_table

| Sign of Alzheimer's Dementia | Typical Age-Related Change |
|------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Memory loss that disrupts daily life (forgetting recently learned information; repeating questions; increasing reliance on memory aids/family) | Sometimes forgetting names or appointments, but remembering them later |
| Challenges in planning or solving problems (trouble following a recipe or tracking bills; difficulty concentrating) | Making occasional errors when managing finances or household bills |
| Difficulty completing familiar tasks (trouble driving to a familiar location, organizing a list, remembering game rules) | Occasionally needing help to use microwave settings or record a TV show |
| Confusion with time or place (losing track of dates/seasons; trouble with non-immediate events) | Getting confused about the day of the week but figuring it out later |
| Trouble understanding visual images and spatial relationships (problems judging distance, color, contrast) | Vision changes related to cataracts |
| New problems with words in speaking or writing (trouble following/joining a conversation; wrong names) | Sometimes having trouble finding the right word |
| Misplacing things and losing the ability to retrace steps (putting things in unusual places; accusing others of stealing) | Misplacing things from time to time and retracing steps to find them |
| Decreased or poor judgment (poor judgment with money; less attention to grooming) | Making a bad decision or mistake once in a while |
| Withdrawal from work or social activities | Sometimes feeling uninterested in family and social obligations |
| Changes in mood, personality and behavior (confused, suspicious, depressed, fearful, anxious) | Developing very specific ways of doing things and becoming irritable when a routine is disrupted |
