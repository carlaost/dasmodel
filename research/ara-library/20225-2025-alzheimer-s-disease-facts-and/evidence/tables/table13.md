# Table 13 - Type and Focus of Caregiver Interventions

**Source**: Table 13, §5.1.7 (p.45) in 2025 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures
**Caption**: "Type and Focus of Caregiver Interventions. Created from data from Sorensen et al.,596 Gaugler et al.680 and Walter and Pinquart.692"
**Screenshot**: table13.png
**Extraction type**: raw_table

| Type | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Case management | Assessment, information, planning, referral, care coordination and/or advocacy for family caregivers. |
| Psychoeducational approaches | Structured programs providing information about the disease, resources/services, and skills to respond to symptoms; lectures, discussions, written materials led by trained professionals. |
| Counseling | Resolves preexisting personal problems that complicate caregiving; reduces caregiver-recipient conflict and improves family functioning. |
| Psychotherapeutic approaches | Therapeutic relationship with a professional therapist (e.g., CBT to modify distress-related beliefs, develop coping behaviors, foster caregiver well-being). |
| Respite | Planned, temporary relief via substitute care; e.g., adult day services, in-home or institutional respite for a set number of weekly hours. |
| Support groups | Less structured; provide opportunity to share feelings/concerns and overcome isolation. |
| Multicomponent approaches | Intensive strategies combining multiple intervention forms (education, support, respite) into a single long-term service (often ≥12 months). |
