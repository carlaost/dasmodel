# Table 9 - Who Are the Caregivers?

**Source**: Table 9, §5.1 (p.37) in 2025 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures
**Caption**: "Who Are the Caregivers?"
**Screenshot**: table9.png
**Extraction type**: raw_table

| Dimension | Findings |
|-----------|----------|
| Sex/gender | Approximately two-thirds of dementia caregivers are women. |
| Race/ethnicity | Two-thirds of caregivers are White; 10% Black; 8% Hispanic; 5% Asian American; remaining 10% other populations. |
| Living status | 66% live with the person with dementia in the community; over 60% are married/partnered/in a long-term relationship; ~one-quarter are "sandwich generation" (also caring for ≥1 child). |
| Caring for parents | Over half of caregivers assist a parent or in-law with dementia; among primary caregivers, over half care for their parents. |
| Income | 41% of caregivers have a household income of $50,000 or less. |
| Education | ~40% of dementia caregivers have a college degree or more. |
| Age | ~30% of caregivers are age 65 or older; 23% of caregivers ages 18-49 help someone with dementia (a 7% increase between 2015 and 2021). |
| Caring for spouse | ~10% of caregivers provide help to a spouse with Alzheimer's or another dementia. |
