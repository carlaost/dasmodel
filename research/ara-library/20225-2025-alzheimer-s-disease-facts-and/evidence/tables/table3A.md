# Table 3A - Clinical Staging for Individuals on the Alzheimer's Disease Continuum

**Source**: Table 3A, §2.5 (p.8) in 2025 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures
**Caption**: "Clinical Staging for Individuals on the Alzheimer's Disease Continuum. Created from data from Jack et al.53"
**Screenshot**: table3A.png
**Extraction type**: raw_table

| Stage | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| Stage 0 | Asymptomatic, deterministic gene* | No evidence of clinical change. Biomarkers in normal range. |
| Stage 1 | Asymptomatic, biomarker evidence only | Performance within expected range on objective cognitive tests. No evidence of recent cognitive decline or new symptoms. |
| Stage 2 | Transitional decline: mild detectable change, minimal impact on daily function | Normal performance within expected range; decline from previous level over past 1-3 years persistent ≥6 months; may involve subtle decline, subjective report, or recent-onset mood/anxiety/motivation change; remains fully independent with no/minimal ADL impact. |
| Stage 3 | Cognitive impairment with early functional impact | Impaired/abnormal range on objective cognitive tests; documented decline from baseline; performs daily activities independently but complex ADLs may take more time/be less efficient. |
| Stage 4 | Dementia with mild functional impairment | Progressive cognitive and mild functional impairment on instrumental ADLs, with independence in basic ADLs. |
| Stage 5 | Dementia with moderate functional impairment | Progressive cognitive and moderate functional impairment, with assistance required on basic ADLs. |
| Stage 6 | Dementia with severe functional impairment | Progressive cognitive and functional impairment, and complete dependence for basic ADLs. |

*Individuals with Down syndrome may not be fully independent even in stage 0 because of underlying intellectual disability; decline in functional independence from baseline may be a more appropriate indicator of stage.
