# Table 8 - Dementia Caregiving Tasks

**Source**: Table 8, §5.1 (p.37) in 2025 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures
**Caption**: "Dementia Caregiving Tasks."
**Screenshot**: table8.png
**Extraction type**: raw_table

| Caregiving task |
|-----------------|
| Helping with instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs): chores, shopping, meals, transportation, arranging doctor's appointments, managing finances and legal affairs, answering the telephone |
| Helping the person take medications correctly (reminders or direct administration) |
| Helping the person adhere to treatment recommendations for dementia or other medical conditions |
| Assisting with personal activities of daily living (ADLs): bathing, dressing, grooming, feeding; helping the person walk, transfer, use the toilet, manage incontinence |
| Managing behavioral symptoms: wandering, depressive mood, agitation, anxiety, repetitive activity, nighttime disturbances |
| Finding and using support services such as support groups and adult day service programs |
| Making arrangements for paid in-home, nursing home or assisted living care |
| Hiring and supervising others who provide care |
| Assuming additional responsibilities: overall management of getting through the day; addressing family issues/communication about care plans and respite; managing comorbidities (arthritis, diabetes, cancer); providing emotional support and a sense of security |
