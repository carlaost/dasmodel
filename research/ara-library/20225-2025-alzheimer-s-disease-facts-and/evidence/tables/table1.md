# Table 1 - Common Causes of Dementia

**Source**: Table 1, §2 Overview (pp.3-4) in 2025 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures
**Caption**: "Common Causes of Dementia."
**Screenshot**: table1.png
**Extraction type**: raw_table

| Cause | Brain changes (summary) | Percentage of dementia cases | Symptoms (summary) |
|-------|-------------------------|------------------------------|--------------------|
| Alzheimer's disease | Beta-amyloid outside neurons + tau tangles inside neurons; neuron death, tissue damage, inflammation, atrophy | Most common cause; estimated 60% to 80% of cases; most also have ≥1 other cause (mixed pathologies) | Difficulty remembering recent conversations/names/events; apathy; depression; later communication problems, confusion, poor judgment; late-stage difficulty walking/speaking/swallowing |
| Cerebrovascular disease (vascular dementia) | Blood vessels damaged / tissue injured from insufficient blood, oxygen, nutrients | ~5% to 10% show vascular dementia alone; more common as mixed pathology | Slowed thoughts, impaired decision/planning; memory may be affected; less emotional; slow gait, poor balance |
| Frontotemporal degeneration (FTD) | Nerve cells in frontal/temporal lobes die; abnormal tau or TDP-43 | ~60% of people with FTD are ages 45-60; ~3% of dementia cases in studies incl. 65+, ~10% in studies restricted to <65 | Marked personality/behavior changes and/or language difficulty; memory typically spared early |
| Hippocampal sclerosis (HS) | Shrinkage/hardening of hippocampal tissue; often with misfolded TDP-43 | Present in ~3% to 13% of people with dementia; ~0.4% to 2% due to HS alone | Pronounced memory loss; often misdiagnosed as AD; common at age 85+ |
| Lewy body disease (DLB) | Abnormal alpha-synuclein clumps (Lewy bodies) in neurons/cortex | ~5% of older individuals with dementia show DLB alone; most also have AD changes | Sleep disturbances, visual hallucinations, visuospatial impairment; fluctuating symptoms; Parkinson-like motor problems |
| Limbic-predominant age-related TDP-43 encephalopathy (LATE) | TDP-43 accumulates in limbic system | Percentage unknown; autopsy of >6,000 found 40% had TDP-43 deposits; LATE associated with memory/thinking deficits in ~25% | Similar to AD but begin at older age (75+), milder, slower |
| Mixed pathologies | Brain changes of more than one cause | >50% of those diagnosed with AD dementia at Research Centers had mixed dementia; higher in community studies; most common at 85+ | Vary by combination present |
| Parkinson's disease (PD) | Alpha-synuclein clumps in substantia nigra; degeneration of dopamine neurons | Systematic review: 3.6% of dementia cases due to PD; 24.5% of people with PD developed dementia | Movement problems (slowness, rigidity, tremor, gait); cognitive symptoms typically develop years later |
