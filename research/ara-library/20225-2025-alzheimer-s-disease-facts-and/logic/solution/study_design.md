# Study / Report Design

The report is a recurring annual surveillance synthesis, not a primary study. Its "method" is the assembly, harmonization, and re-expression of multiple public data sources into national and state estimates across six domains plus a commissioned Special Report.

## Structure of the report (sections)
1. **Overview** — brain changes, biomarkers, staging (Tables 1, 2, 3A/3B/3C), treatments (Figure 1), risk factors (Figure 2, Table 4), lowering risk.
2. **Prevalence** — national/state counts and projections (Figures 3-7; Table 5).
3. **Mortality and Morbidity** — death counts, rates, trends, COVID-19 effect, burden/DALYs (Figures 8-10, 17; Tables 6-7).
4. **Caregiving** — unpaid caregivers, hours, value, health/employment impacts, interventions, COVID-19 (Figures 11-14; Tables 8-13).
5. **Workforce** — PCPs, specialists, direct care, community-based workforce, collaborative models (Tables 14-15).
6. **Use and Costs of Care** — total/per-person costs, utilization, long-term care, end-of-life, projections (Figures 15-18; Tables 16-24).
7. **Special Report** — American perspectives on early detection in the era of treatment (survey + focus groups; Figures 19-27).

## Estimation approach by domain
- **Prevalence (E01, E08)**: Apply CHAP-derived, demographically stratified Alzheimer's dementia prevalence rates to U.S. Census population counts and 2024 projections; state/county detail from Dhana et al.; incidence and lifetime risk from CHAP and Framingham. HRS-HCAP provides an all-cause dementia comparison.
- **Mortality (E02, E03)**: Tabulate CDC/NCHS death-certificate underlying-cause data (national/state, age-specific rates, cause-of-death trends); supplement with attributable-mortality estimates (Rush MAP/ROS; HRS) to characterize undercounting.
- **Caregiving (E04, E05)**: Derive caregiver counts and hours from the BRFSS caregiver module (all states since 2016), scaled with Census data and NAC/AARP inputs; impute economic value using a conservative wage benchmark (average of state minimum wage and median home-health-aide cost); compare caregiver health outcomes to non-dementia caregivers/non-caregivers.
- **Workforce (E07)**: Model geriatrician need (30% of 65+ with complex needs; 700 patients per geriatrician) against certified supply, by state and year; project direct-care demand from long-term occupational projections.
- **Costs (E06)**: Estimate total national payments and payer mix via the Lewin Model; per-person and per-service payments from MCBS 2018 and the National 100% Medicare FFS 2019; project to 2050; benchmark long-term-care prices (Genworth).
- **Special Report (E09)**: Commissioned probability-panel survey (NORC AmeriSpeak; Versta Research) of 1,702 U.S. adults age 45+, fielded Nov 7-18, 2024, in English/Spanish, weighted to Census totals, with racial/ethnic oversampling; triangulated with 11 focus groups (69 participants; L&M Policy Research).

## Diagnostic/staging framework (adopted from Jack et al. 2024)
- **Clinical stages 0-6** (Table 3A): from asymptomatic-with-deterministic-gene (0) through severe functional impairment (6).
- **Biological staging A-D by PET** (Table 3B): amyloid PET positivity plus tau-PET uptake pattern (medial temporal / moderate / high neocortical), yielding AT2 notation.
- **Integrated biological + clinical staging** (Table 3C): cells 1A through 4-6D; typical progression along the diagonal.
- Anti-amyloid disease-modifying treatment (lecanemab, donanemab) is approved only for stages 3-4 with confirmed elevated beta-amyloid.

## Documented estimation decisions (also captured in the exploration tree)
- **D1**: Use the CHAP symptom-based estimate (7.2M) as the headline prevalence rather than the more stringent HRS-HCAP estimate, because HRS-HCAP's functional-impairment requirement may miss mild cases (endnote A3).
- **D2**: Report symptom-based prevalence (7.2M) while explicitly noting that a biomarker-based definition would likely be up to 30% lower (~5M) (§3.3.1).
- **D3**: Present unadjusted (not age-adjusted) state death rates to convey absolute burden (endnote A6).
- **D4**: Value unpaid care conservatively (average of state minimum wage and median home-health-aide cost) (endnote A9).
