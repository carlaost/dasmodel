# Table 4 - Total, exposure, and vulnerability effects for the predictors

**Source**: Table 4, §3 Results (pp. 7-8; table spans two pages, "Continues" onto p. 8)
**Caption**: "Total, exposure, and vulnerability effects for the predictors presented in the form: Total effect of predictor = exposure x vulnerability."
**Screenshot**: table4.png (block on p. 7) and table4_continued.png (continuation on p. 8)
**Extraction type**: raw_table

Each predictor cell is presented as `total = exposure * vulnerability` (i.e., `f_i = E_i * V_i`, Eq. 6). For each disparity block, the first two rows are the *Observed disparity* (RR in Eq. 6, approximated by the univariable hazard ratio of the disparity indicator) and the *Unexplained disparity* (`R_r = exp(β_r)` in Eq. 2), which carry a single value (no decomposition).

Footnotes (verbatim):
- a: "Observed disparity is represented by the quantity RR in Equation (6) and approximated by the hazard ratio estimated in the univariable analysis with the predictor representing the disparity-affected group, for example, the Black/White indicator."
- b: "Unexplained part of disparity is represented by R_r in Equation (6); this is defined as R_r = exp(β_r) in Equation (2)."
- c: "The effects of predictors are determined by factors f_i in Equation (6) that are multiplicatively decomposed by the exposure and vulnerability effects: f_i = E_j V_j."

## Notes
- Reproduced source note (§ decomposition): observed disparity `r_obs = r_unex * Π_i E_i V_i`. Where `r_unex < r_unob` (unexplained < observed), predictors explain part of the disparity — stated by the paper for Black–White and Hispanic–White. For White–Native American and White–Asian the unexplained disparity **exceeds** the observed one (e.g., WN age 70: observed 1.26 vs unexplained 2.10; WA age 70: observed 1.40 vs unexplained 1.86), i.e., the predictors act to *increase* the apparent disparity.
- For arterial hypertension the decomposition is dominated by the vulnerability factor (exposure ≈ 1.00 in most cells, e.g., HW age 85 `1.27=0.99 * 1.28`). For low income the exposure factor carries the effect (e.g., BW age 70 `1.11=1.15 * 0.97`).
