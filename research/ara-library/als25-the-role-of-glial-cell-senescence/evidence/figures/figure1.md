# Figure 1: Potential causes and consequences of cellular senescence

- **Source**: Figure 1, page 2, §1 (Introduction)
- **Caption**: "Potential causes and consequences of cellular senescence. Cellular senescence is triggered by various factors, including mitochondrial dysfunction, telomere shortening, oncogene activation, DNA damage, and oxidative stress. These stressors lead to a state of permanent growth arrest, accompanied by metabolic changes, altered apoptosis sensitivity, and the secretion of pro-inflammatory molecules known as the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP). SASP not only drives chronic inflammation and tissue dysfunction but also enhances aging processes and serves as a significant contributor to neurodegenerative diseases, including Alzheimer's disease (ad)."
