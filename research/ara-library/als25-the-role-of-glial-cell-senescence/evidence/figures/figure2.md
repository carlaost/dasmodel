# Figure 2: Potential mechanisms underlying brain glial senescence contributing to Alzheimer's disease

- **Source**: Figure 2, page 10, §1.2.3 / §2 (referenced as summarizing the mechanisms)
- **Caption**: "Potential mechanisms underlying brain glial senescence contributing to or enhancing Alzheimer's disease (ad). Glial cell senescence plays a pivotal role in the pathophysiology of Alzheimer's Disease (ad), with astrocytes, microglia, and oligodendrocytes in the ad brain or ad model mice undergoing senescence in response to stressors such as mitochondrial dysfunction, telomere shortening, oncogene activation, DNA damage, and oxidative stress. These stressors induce permanent growth arrest in glial cells. Senescent glial cells secrete pro-inflammatory cytokines and molecules, collectively known as the senescence-associated secretory phenotype (SASP), which promotes a neuroinflammatory environment that exacerbates neurodegenerative processes in ad. Specifically, the SASP impairs the function of oligodendrocyte precursor cells (OPCs), hindering their differentiation into myelinating oligodendrocytes and leading to remyelination failure. Additionally, the accumulation of hyperphosphorylated tau and amyloid-β (Aβ) oligomers accelerates neurodegeneration and further drives the progression of brain cell senescence, reinforcing the senescent state through positive feedback loops. These processes contribute to cognitive decline and neuronal loss, hallmarks of ad. BBBD, blood–brain barrier dysfunction; HMGB1, high mobility group box 1; IL-10, interleukin-10; IL-1β, interleukin-1 beta; IL-4, interleukin-4; IL-6, interleukin-6; IL-8, interleukin-8; INF-γ, interferon gamma; MMPs, matrix metalloproteinases; NOS2, nitric oxide synthase 2 (inducible NOS); PAI-1, plasminogen activator inhibitor-1; RAGE, receptor for advanced glycation end products; TNF-α, tumor necrosis factor alpha."

## Visual description
- **Components**:
  - Lower-left cluster labeled "Glial senescence": a "Cellular Stressor" (red lightning bolts) acting on glial cells (a red reactive astrocyte, a green ramified microglia/glia) surrounded by many labeled secreted factors ("SASP"): PAI-1, TNFα, IL-4β, NOS2, IL-6 (multiple), IL-1β, HMGB1.
  - Top row: "Oligodendrocyte precursor cells" → (red X, blocked) → "Myelinating Oligodendrocyte" → "Remyelination failure" → "Demyelination" (green neuron with degrading myelin, top right).
  - Center box listing SASP-driven changes: up-arrow set "BBBD, IL-1b, IL-6, IL-8, TNF-α, INF-γ, MMPs, RAGE" (increased) and a separate set "BBB, IL-4, IL-10, Phagocytosis" with a down-arrow (decreased).
  - Right/center: "Neuroinflammation" arrow leading to "Neurodegeneration" — a purple degenerating neuron with "Tangled Tau proteins," "Microtubules disintegrating," and, at far right, "Alzheimer's Pathological features": "Aβ plaques," "Aβ & Tau Aggregation."
- **Connections**:
  - Cellular stressor → glial senescence → SASP secretion.
  - SASP → (top path) blocks OPC differentiation into myelinating oligodendrocytes → remyelination failure → demyelination.
  - SASP → "Neuroinflammation" → "Neurodegeneration" (tau tangles, microtubule disintegration) and Aβ/tau aggregation → Alzheimer's pathological features.
  - A curved arrow loops from the Alzheimer's pathological features / neurodegeneration back to "Glial senescence" (bottom), depicting the positive feedback loop in which Aβ/tau accumulation further drives glial senescence.
- **Annotations**: Red up-arrow = increased mediators; red down-arrow = decreased BBB integrity/anti-inflammatory factors/phagocytosis; red X = blocked OPC→oligodendrocyte differentiation; curved return arrow = positive feedback.
- **What it conveys**: The AD-specific mechanistic model — stressor-induced glial senescence → SASP → (a) remyelination failure via OPC differentiation block and (b) neuroinflammation → neurodegeneration and Aβ/tau aggregation → feedback to more glial senescence → cognitive decline and neuronal loss.
