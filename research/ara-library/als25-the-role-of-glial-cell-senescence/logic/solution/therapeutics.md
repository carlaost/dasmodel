# Therapeutic Approaches Targeting Glial Cell Senescence in AD

*Grounding: full-text §1.3 and §2. Two classes are surveyed: senolytics (eliminate senescent cells)
and senomorphics (suppress SASP). Agents, mechanisms, models, and reported effects below are copied
from the review; exact numeric outcomes belong in the underlying primary studies (not reproduced as
review-authored numbers).*

## Senolytics (eliminate senescent cells via apoptosis)

### Dasatinib + Quercetin (D+Q)
- **Mechanism**: Combination senolytic; reduces burden of senescent cells and alleviates neuroinflammation.
- **Preclinical**: TBI model — intermittent D+Q reduced p16INK4A+ / p21CIP1/WAF1+ cells, decreased IL-1β and IL-6, attenuated neurodegeneration, improved spatial/recognition memory (Wang 2023). APP/PS1 — weekly D+Q for 11 weeks reduced Aβ-plaque load and plaque-associated SA-β-Gal, decreased hippocampal IL-1β and TNF-α, improved Y-maze/water-maze performance (Zhang 2019). PS19 tauopathy — reduced p16INK4A+ microglia, decreased tau hyperphosphorylation, rescued cerebral oxygen hypometabolism, preserved BBB integrity, improved cognition (Yao 2024). Very-old tauopathy mice — reduced NFTs, improved cerebral blood flow, decreased ventricular pathology and brain atrophy (Musi 2018).
- **Clinical**: Phase I trial — oral D+Q decreased SA-β-Gal activity, reduced p16INK4A, decreased circulating SASP factor MMP-12 (Hickson 2019). Symptomatic early-AD older adults — decreased SASP cytokines/chemokines (IL-17, IL-21, IL-10, IL-6, MMP-9); dasatinib and quercetin increased in plasma, only dasatinib detected in CSF (BBB crossing); **no effect on cognition or structural MRI** in the short study period (Gonzales 2023; Longo & Massa 2023).
- **Claims supported**: C07, C08.

### AP20187 (genetic, INK-ATTAC system)
- **Mechanism**: Induces apoptosis of highly p16INK4A-expressing cells.
- **Preclinical**: Aged INK-ATTAC mice — reduced microglial activation, alleviated SASP cytokines, improved cognition (Ogrodnik 2021). INK-ATTAC×PS19 — cleared p16INK4A-/p19Arf-/p21CIP1/WAF1-positive cells in cortex and hippocampus, reduced IL-6 and IL-1β, decreased soluble and insoluble tau hyperphosphorylation, alleviated NFT deposition in dentate gyrus (Bussian 2018).
- **Claims supported**: C07.

### Navitoclax (ABT-263)
- **Mechanism**: Inhibits anti-apoptotic BCL-2 and BCL-xL → selective apoptosis of senescent cells (including glia).
- **Preclinical**: PS19 mice — repeated treatment prevented upregulation of senescence-associated genes (p16, p19, p21, IL-6, IL-1β) and reduced tau hyperphosphorylation in cortex and hippocampus (Bussian 2018).
- **Claims supported**: C07.

### Fisetin
- **Mechanism**: Naturally occurring flavonoid senolytic.
- **Preclinical**: Reduced p16INK4A, p21CIP1/WAF1, and SASP markers in several tissues; reduced age-related pathology; improved lifespan in aged mice (Yousefzadeh 2018).
- **Claims supported**: C07.

## Senomorphics (suppress SASP / senescent-phenotype maintenance)

*Distinguishing property (§1.3): unlike senolytics, senomorphics require **continuous treatment** to
maintain suppression of SASP/inflammatory processes.*

### Rapamycin / PQR530 (mTOR inhibitors)
- Rapamycin reduced amyloid plaques and improved vascular and cognitive function in J20 (PDGF-APPSw,Ind) AD mice (Van Skike 2021; Lin 2013). Long-term rapamycin from a young age prevented tau hyperphosphorylation/aggregation in cortex of P301S mice; PQR530 (improved pharmacokinetics) reduced tau pathology even at later time points (Morawe 2022).

### Resveratrol (NF-κB inhibitor, polyphenol)
- Prevented IL-6 and TNF-α secretion in microglia exposed to fibrillar Aβ; reduced Aβ deposition and microglial activation in APP/PS1 mice (Capiralla 2012). A meta-analysis of RCTs gave preliminary evidence for reducing Aβ load, inflammation, oxidative stress, and delaying cognitive impairment in AD patients (Tosatti 2022).

### Apigenin (polyphenol)
- Downregulated NO and pro-inflammatory cytokines in microglial lines/primary microglia; protected iPSC-derived familial-AD neurons against inflammation-induced death and neurite shortening; in Aβ-injected Wistar rats, improved spatial working memory and protected against hippocampal neuronal loss (Balez 2016).

### Adalimumab (anti-TNF-α monoclonal antibody)
- Attenuated neuroinflammation by preventing astrocyte/microglia activation in Aβ1-40-injected mice; prevented hippocampal neuronal loss, reduced amyloidosis, rescued memory (Park 2019).

### Ginsenoside F1 / SGB121 (NF-κB suppressor)
- Ginsenoside F1-enhanced mixture (SGB121) mitigated oxidative stress, reduced p16INK4A and SA-β-gal, rescued telomere shortening in high-fat-diet mice; reduced IL-6, TNF-α, CXCL-11, MMP-2; prevented astrocyte/microglia activation; reduced tau hyperphosphorylation (Hou 2022).

## Cross-cutting notes
- **Claims supported (all senotherapeutics)**: C07 (preclinical efficacy), C08 (human target engagement without cognitive benefit yet).
- **Limitations** (detailed in `constraints.md`): non-universal efficacy across cell types/models, systemic administration and off-target risk, disruption of beneficial senescence, unexplored long-term efficacy, senescent-cell heterogeneity necessitating subpopulation-specific or personalized combination therapy, and dietary/microenvironment modulation of efficacy.
