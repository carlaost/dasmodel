## Trend summary
- In every panel and every country the Female (red) bar exceeds the Male (blue) bar, visually
  supporting the "females > males" ordering (paper notes this sex difference is NOT statistically
  significant — the 95% UI whiskers are wide and overlap). Supports C05.
- Prevalence (a): bars cluster tightly across countries (~650–830 per 100,000; exact values in
  Table 1). Lebanon and Turkey extend furthest right; UAE shortest — consistent with C03.
- Death rate (b): much smaller magnitude (~23–33 per 100,000) with very wide upper UIs (whiskers
  reaching 60–110+), reflecting high uncertainty in fatal estimates.
- DALY rate (c): ~427–578 per 100,000 with wide upper UIs (whiskers to ~1,000–1,600).
- Exact per-country ASR values (both sexes combined) are transcribed in
  [tables/table1.md](../tables/table1.md); this figure's incremental content is the sex split and
  the visual UI whiskers, which carry no printed numeric labels.
