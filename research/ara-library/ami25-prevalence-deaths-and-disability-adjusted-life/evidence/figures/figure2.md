## Trend summary
- (a) Prevalent-case COUNTS rise with age to a peak at the 80–84 band, then decline through 90–94 and
  95+. The prevalence RATE line rises monotonically with age for both sexes. Supports C06.
- (b) Death COUNTS rise with age to a peak at 85–89, then decline at 90–94/95+; death RATE line rises
  monotonically with age. Supports C06.
- (c) DALY COUNTS rise with age to a peak at 80–84, then decline; DALY RATE line rises monotonically
  with age. Supports C06.
- In all three panels Female (red) bars/lines sit above Male (blue) at essentially every age band,
  supporting C05 (females > males), with wide overlapping UIs.
- The divergence between the count peak (mid-80s) and the still-rising rate at 95+ reflects the small
  and shrinking population denominator at the oldest ages.
