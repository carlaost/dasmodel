# Constraints, Assumptions, and Limitations

## Boundary conditions
- Analyses cover the EU-eligible subpopulation of TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 (NCT04437511): APOE ε4 non-carriers/heterozygotes without baseline superficial siderosis, anticoagulant use, or uncontrolled hypertension (seated systolic BP ≥140 and diastolic BP ≥90 mmHg). APOE ε4 homozygotes are excluded by design.
- The placebo-controlled analyses cover 76 weeks; the LTE covers a 78-week extension (154 weeks total follow-up).
- Efficacy on clinical scales uses the EMA-required conservative hybrid J2R/CIR imputation; results are also reported without imputation (original methodology) for comparison.
- Amyloid clearance defined as amyloid plaque level <24.1 CL.
- Results reflect the TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 donanemab dosing regimen (not the more gradual titration to be used in clinical practice).

## Assumptions
- A1: The retrospectively defined EU-eligible subset represents patients expected to be treated in European clinical practice.
- A2: J2R appropriately handles missingness due to serious/severe/symptomatic ARIA or death; CIR handles all other missingness; both being reference-derived makes them conservative relative to no imputation.
- A3: The propensity-weighted ADNI cohort is an acceptable untreated external comparator for the LTE.
- A4: Any increase in CDR-G at two consecutive visits reflects a clinically meaningful stage shift.

## Known limitations (stated in the paper)
- L1: All analyses are post-hoc, exploratory, and not controlled for multiplicity.
- L2: Excluding APOE ε4 homozygotes and contraindicated patients yields a smaller subpopulation than the primary study, reducing precision.
- L3: The LTE was not designed with an internal placebo comparator; the ADNI external control may differ due to variations in study conduct, assessments, time periods, geographic regions, and other unmeasured confounders.
- L4: Differences in disease severity between early- and delayed-start groups at donanemab initiation make between-group comparisons challenging (potential bias); rate of clinical decline and treatment benefit vary by disease stage.
- L5: The study design switched participants to placebo after treatment-course completion, so most (74.8%) early-start participants received placebo for the entirety of the LTE — unequal treatment exposure complicates interpretation.
- L6: An EU-eligible propensity-weighted ADNI control could not be constructed (superficial siderosis, anticoagulant use, and blood pressure were not collected in ADNI); therefore LTE analyses used the EU-indicated/non-homozygote population, not the EU-eligible definition. Simultaneous propensity reweighting and J2R/CIR multiple imputation is not methodologically possible, so hybrid imputation was not applied to any LTE analysis.
- L7: Results reflect the trial dosing regimen; clinical practice will use a more gradual titration (associated with lower ARIA-E in TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 6).
- L8: Wide clinical-scale ranges (e.g., iADRS 0–144) can understate treatment effects; for most EU-eligible participants baseline iADRS clustered within a ~36-point band (85–121), which should frame interpretation.
