# Claims

All claims are post-hoc, exploratory, and not controlled for multiplicity (per §2.2, §4). Numbers
are copied exactly from `paper.pdf`; source quotes appear under each claim's `**Sources**`.

## C01: Donanemab slows iADRS decline at 76 weeks in the EU-eligible population (conservative imputation)
- **Statement**: At 76 weeks, using the conservative hybrid imputation method, the adjusted mean change from baseline in iADRS was −13.0 (SE 0.6) with placebo and −10.6 (SE 0.6) with donanemab (difference 2.3 [95% CI: 0.7, 3.9]; p=0.005), an 18% (95% CI: 5.2, 30.6) slowing of disease progression.
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: The 95% CI for the placebo–donanemab iADRS difference under hybrid imputation includes 0, or the point difference is ≤0.
- **Proof**: [E01]
- **Evidence basis**: Fig. 1A; Table 2 (conservative hybrid imputation column: adjusted mean difference 2.32 [0.69, 3.95], p=0.005, 17.9% [5.2, 30.6] slowing).
- **Interpretation**: The narrow relevant band of the iADRS scale (see C10) means an 18% relative slowing corresponds to a clinically meaningful effect within the range where mild AD patients actually score.
- **Dependencies**: none
- **Tags**: iADRS, efficacy, imputation, EU-eligible
- **Sources**:
  - −13.0 (0.6) / −10.6 (0.6); difference 2.3 [0.7, 3.9]; p=0.005; 18% [5.2, 30.6] ← `paper.pdf` p3 §3.1.2.1 «the adjusted mean (SE) change from baseline in the iADRS score in the EU-eligible population using the hybrid imputation method was −13.0 (0.6) and −10.6 (0.6) in the placebo and donanemab arms, respectively (difference, 2.3 [95% CI: 0.7, 3.9]; p=0.005), representing an 18% (95% CI: 5.2, 30.6) slowing of disease progression» [result]
  - 2.32 (0.69, 3.95); 0.005; 17.9% (5.2, 30.6) ← `paper.pdf` p6 Table 2, iADRS Conservative hybrid imputation row «2.32 (0.69, 3.95) … 0.005 … 17.9% (5.2, 30.6)» [result]

- **Statement**: At 76 weeks, the adjusted mean change-from-baseline CDR-SB difference between donanemab and placebo was −0.7 (95% CI: −1.0, −0.4; p<0.001), a 28% (95% CI: 15.2, 41.3) slowing of disease progression (conservative hybrid imputation).
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: The 95% CI for the CDR-SB difference includes 0, or point estimate ≥0 (higher CDR-SB = worse).

- **Evidence basis**: Fig. 1B; abstract; Table 2 (CDR-SB conservative hybrid: −0.67 [−0.97, −0.37], p<0.001, 28.2% [15.2, 41.3]).
- **Interpretation**: CDR-SB is the anchor clinical endpoint; the effect is consistent with the overall trial (28.9% without imputation, C10).
- **Dependencies**: none
- **Tags**: CDR-SB, efficacy, imputation, EU-eligible

## C03: Donanemab lowers the risk of clinical progression by CDR-G
- **Statement**: Over the 76-week trial in the EU-eligible population, the risk of disease progression to the next clinical stage (per CDR-G) was 40.3% lower with donanemab (N=581, 174 events) than placebo (N=581, 247 events) (HR=0.597 [95% CI: 0.5, 0.8]; p<0.001); the risk of progression to moderate dementia (CDR-G ≥2) was reduced by 47.2% (donanemab N=565, 41 events; placebo N=567, 65 events; HR=0.528 [95% CI: 0.3, 0.9]; p=0.024).

## C08: The EU-eligible population has a reduced but present ARIA burden and no ARIA-related deaths
- **Statement**: In the EU-eligible donanemab arm, ARIA-E occurred in 19.5% (119/609), symptomatic ARIA-E in 4.9% (30/609), serious ARIA-E in 1.0% (6/609), and ARIA-H in 26.4% (161/609); infusion-related reactions in 8.0% (49). Any ARIA was 32.0% with donanemab vs 12.4% placebo, ~5% lower than the overall TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 population (32.0% vs 36.8%). There were no ARIA-related deaths; deaths were 1.3% (donanemab) vs 1.2% (placebo). Most ARIA-E was asymptomatic (74.8%) and mild-to-moderate radiographically (94.9%).
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: EU-eligible any-ARIA rate ≥ the overall-population rate, or any ARIA-related death is reported in the EU-eligible population.
- **Proof**: [E06]
- **Evidence basis**: §3.1.3; §4; Table 4 (ARIA-E 119 [19.5]; symptomatic 30 [4.9]; serious 6 [1.0]; ARIA-H 161 [26.4]; IRR 49 [8.0]; any ARIA 195 [32.0] vs 75 [12.4]).
- **Interpretation**: Excluding homozygotes and contraindicated patients yields a more favourable safety signature while preserving efficacy (see C10).
- **Dependencies**: none
- **Tags**: safety, ARIA, infusion-reaction, APOE, EU-eligible
- **Sources**:
  - ARIA-E 19.5% (119/609); symptomatic 4.9% (30/609); serious 1.0% (6/609); no ARIA deaths ← `paper.pdf` p4 §3.1.3 «incidence of ARIA-E … was 2.0% (12/603) and 19.5% (119/609) … symptomatic and serious ARIA-E in donanemab-treated participants was 4.9% (30/609) and 1.0% (6/609) … no ARIA-related deaths» [result]

## C09: Treatment benefit increases over 154 weeks and earlier treatment yields better outcomes (LTE)
- **Statement**: In the LTE (non-carriers and heterozygotes vs propensity-weighted ADNI external control), the treatment effect increased over 154 weeks, including for early-start participants who met treatment-course completion criteria by 52 or 76 weeks; at 154 weeks early-start participants had a 29% lower risk of progressing to the next disease stage (by CDR-G) than delayed-start participants, and amyloid clearance was maintained after treatment completion.
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: The LTE shows no increasing treatment effect over 154 weeks, or early-start does not have lower progression risk than delayed-start.
- **Proof**: [E07]
- **Evidence basis**: Abstract; §4 (Discussion of LTE results; full LTE data reported in Supplement and Zimmer et al. [7]).
- **Interpretation**: Supports durability of a limited-duration dosing course and the value of earlier treatment; the paper flags that early-vs-delayed comparisons are challenging due to baseline severity differences and unequal exposure (74.8% of early-start received placebo for the entire LTE).
- **Dependencies**: none
- **Tags**: LTE, long-term, early-start, delayed-start, durability
- **Sources**:
  - increasing effect over 154 weeks; early-start met criteria by 52/76 weeks ← `paper.pdf` p6 §4 «An increasing treatment effect was demonstrated over 154 weeks for donanemab-treated participants compared to the untreated propensity-weighted ADNI cohort, including for early-start participants who met treatment completion criteria by 52 weeks and 76 weeks» [result]
  - 29% lower risk early-start vs delayed-start at 154 weeks ← `paper.pdf` p6 §4 «At 154 weeks, participants who were treated earlier with donanemab (early-start group) demonstrated a 29% lower risk of progressing to the next stage of disease than participants who started donanemab treatment later (delayed-start group)» [result]

## C10: Efficacy is consistent across imputation methods and with the overall trial population
- **Statement**: Donanemab benefit was consistent whether analysed with the conservative hybrid imputation or the original methodology (e.g., CDR-SB slowing 28.2% with vs 30.8% without imputation), and consistent with the overall TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 population (28.9% CDR-SB slowing without imputation); across scales, slowing ranged from 18% to 28% at week 76 under conservative imputation.
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: The hybrid-imputation and original estimates diverge in direction or significance, or the EU-eligible CDR-SB slowing differs materially from the overall population.
- **Proof**: [E08]
- **Evidence basis**: §4; Table 2 (original vs conservative columns for iADRS, CDR-SB, ADAS-Cog13, ADCS-iADL, MMSE).
- **Interpretation**: Robustness of benefit across estimands strengthens the regulatory and clinical interpretation.
- **Dependencies**: C01, C02
- **Tags**: robustness, imputation, consistency, EU-eligible
- **Sources**:
  - 28.2% (with) / 30.8% (without) hybrid imputation vs 28.9% overall population ← `paper.pdf` p5 §4 «donanemab-mediated slowing of disease progression, as measured by CDR-SB, was 28.2% and 30.8% in the EU-eligible population with and without the hybrid imputation method respectively, compared to 28.9% in the overall TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 population (without imputation)» [result]
  - 18% to 28% slowing at week 76 ← `paper.pdf` p4 §4 «with an 18% to 28% slowing of disease progression compared to placebo at week 76» [result]
  - Original vs conservative full row set (iADRS 2.52 vs 2.32; CDR-SB −0.73 vs −0.67; ADAS-Cog13 −1.64 vs −1.37; ADCS-iADL 1.25 vs 1.24; MMSE 0.73 vs 0.61) ← `paper.pdf` p6 Table 2 «iADRS 2.52 (0.89, 4.14) … 2.32 (0.69, 3.95) … CDR-SB −0.73 (−1.03, −0.43) … −0.67 (−0.97, −0.37) …» [result]
