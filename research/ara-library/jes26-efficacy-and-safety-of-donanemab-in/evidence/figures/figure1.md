## Panels A–E: adjusted mean change from baseline over 76 weeks (donanemab vs placebo)
- **Plot kind**: line (with SE error bars); X = Week (0, 12, 24, 36, 52, 64, 76; linear), Y = Adjusted Mean Change (SE) in each scale (linear). Placebo = grey, donanemab = red. On-figure "Δ" annotations mark the donanemab–placebo difference at weeks 24, 52, and 76; the boxed % is the 76-week slowing (95% CI).

| Panel | Scale | Δ at wk 24 | Δ at wk 52 | Δ at wk 76 | 76-wk slowing (95% CI) |

| A | iADRS | 1.2 | 1.6 | 2.3 | 18% (5.2, 30.6) |
| B | CDR-SB | 0.3 | 0.6 | 0.7 | 28% (15.2, 41.3) |
| C | ADAS-Cog13 | 0.9 | 1.4 | 1.4 | 20% (6.4, 33.1) |

## Trend summary
Across all five scales the donanemab (red) curve stays above/below placebo (grey) in the direction of less impairment, with the gap widening monotonically from week 24 to week 76 (Δ increasing). All five 76-week slowing CIs exclude 0. In Panel F, five of six CDR-SB domains show statistically significant benefit favouring donanemab; community affairs trends favourably but crosses significance (p=0.052).
