# Concepts

*Grounding: full-text. Definitions are the review's usage of each term, with source sections.*

## APOE4 ("molten globule" isoform)
- **Notation**: APOE4 (Arg112/Arg158)
- **Definition**: One of three human APOE isoforms (APOE2: Cys112/Cys158; APOE3: Cys112/Arg158; APOE4: Arg112/Arg158), encoded by a gene on chromosome 19 as a 299-amino-acid, ~34 kDa secreted glycoprotein. APOE4 is the least structurally stable isoform, adopting a "molten globule" folded-intermediate state (core α-helical structure, increased β-lamellar structure, enlarged hydrodynamic radius) that enhances interaction with larger lipid-rich particles and Aβ deposits while promoting aggregation of lipid-deficient APOE4 and impairing lipid transport.
- **Boundary conditions**: The strongest genetic risk factor for late-onset AD; carrier rate ~23.9% in the general population; dose-dependent risk (3-4× heterozygous, 9-15× homozygous).
- **Related concepts**: APOE2/APOE3, LDLR, LRP1, ABCA1, molten globule

## Lipid-droplet-accumulating microglia (LDAM)
- **Notation**: LDAM
- **Definition**: The canonical APOE4-driven pathological microglial subtype, characterized by dysregulated lipid metabolism (enhanced synthesis, suppressed degradation of triglyceride-rich lipid droplets), a proinflammatory state, and impaired phagocytic function. Produced via APOE4-driven upregulation of fatty acid synthesis (FAS) and lipid droplet accumulation combined with suppressed autophagy/lipophagy and impaired fatty acid oxidation (FAO).
- **Boundary conditions**: Accumulates near Aβ plaques; releases proinflammatory cytokines and ROS that promote neuronal lipogenesis, forming a "vicious cycle"; associated with tau pathology and neurotoxicity (Figure 2C).
- **Related concepts**: FAS, FAO, PU.1, NF-κB, DAM

## Disease-associated microglia (DAM)
- **Notation**: DAM
- **Definition**: A microglial activation state showing high expression of genes involved in lipid metabolism and phagocytosis; APOE4-expressing microglia show DAM-consistent metabolic features (increased aerobic glycolysis and Hif1a expression) but with impaired Aβ uptake.
- **Boundary conditions**: DAM-like transcriptional identity does not guarantee preserved function under APOE4 — glycolytic/Hif1a features co-occur with reduced Aβ clearance.
- **Related concepts**: LDAM, HIF1α, aerobic glycolysis, Aβ uptake

## Neurodegenerative microglia (MGnD)
- **Notation**: MGnD
- **Definition**: A microglial state induced via the TREM2-APOE signaling pathway that exerts a neuroprotective effect by eliminating apoptotic neurons.
- **Boundary conditions**: APOE4 exacerbates neurodegeneration by activating ITGB8-TGFβ signaling, upregulating homeostatic checkpoint molecules (e.g., Inpp5d), and inhibiting MGnD function — i.e., APOE4 suppresses what is otherwise a protective microglial program.
- **Related concepts**: TREM2, APOE, ITGB8, TGFβ, Inpp5d

## Terminally inflammatory microglia (TIM)
- **Notation**: TIM
- **Definition**: An APOE4-associated exhausted microglial subtype displaying profound Aβ-clearance deficits, observed in both AD patients and mouse models.
- **Boundary conditions**: Represents a terminal, dysfunctional endpoint of chronic APOE4-driven microglial activation distinct from the metabolically active LDAM/DAM states.
- **Related concepts**: LDAM, Aβ clearance, microglial exhaustion

## TREM2 (triggering receptor expressed on myeloid cells 2)
- **Notation**: TREM2
- **Definition**: A microglial surface receptor whose TREM2-dependent lipid metabolic network coordinates cholesterol efflux (via LXR/PPARγ), maintains phago-lysosomal cycling, and activates microglia to phagocytose diffuse Aβ, compacting it into denser, less-toxic plaques. Downregulated in APOE4 microglia, impairing phagocytic capacity.
- **Boundary conditions**: The TREM2-R47H risk variant shows context-dependent effects — reduced microglial lipid-droplet accumulation and plaque reactivity in vivo, but exacerbated lipid-droplet accumulation in vitro.
- **Related concepts**: TYROBP, MGnD, DAM, ITGB8-TGFβ, TREM2-R47H

## LilrB3 (leukocyte immunoglobulin-like receptor B3)
- **Notation**: LilrB3
- **Definition**: A microglial cell-surface receptor identified as a binding partner of APOE4; APOE4-LilrB3 binding upregulates type-I-interferon-stimulated genes (IFITM3, BST2, MX1, ISG15, STAT1), driving microglia into a proinflammatory state that hinders phagocytic function and contributes to Aβ deposition.
- **Boundary conditions**: A receptor-mediated, direct inflammatory-activation pathway distinct from APOE4's lipid-metabolic effects.
- **Related concepts**: Interferon-stimulating genes (ISGs), Cxorf56, NLRP3

## SREBP2 (sterol regulatory element-binding protein 2)
- **Notation**: SREBP2
- **Definition**: A transcription factor activated in APOE4 astrocytes and microglia via ER stress (with ER Ca2+ depletion in microglia), driving transcription of cholesterol-biosynthesis genes (e.g., HMGCR, SQLE in microglia) and increasing de novo cholesterol synthesis despite lysosomal-dysfunction-induced accumulation.
- **Boundary conditions**: Operates alongside suppressed PPARγ expression in astrocytes; disrupts lysosome-dependent mitophagy when chronically active.
- **Related concepts**: ER stress, HMGCR, SQLE, PPARγ, cholesterol overload

## ABCA1 (ATP-binding cassette transporter A1)
- **Notation**: ABCA1
- **Definition**: A membrane transporter mediating extracellular lipidation of APOE-containing lipoprotein particles and cholesterol efflux; expressed in CNS glial cells and neurons (and peripheral tissues) to maintain lipid homeostasis, regulated by the RXR/LXR nuclear receptor system.
- **Boundary conditions**: APOE4 impairs ABCA1 recycling and promotes its degradation, reducing cholesterol efflux in both microglia and neurons — a shared downstream lesion across cell types.
- **Related concepts**: LXR, RXR, cholesterol efflux, SREBP2, bexarotene

## GPC-4 (glypican-4) / LRP1 axis
- **Notation**: GPC-4, LRP1
- **Definition**: A signaling axis upregulated specifically in APOE4 astrocytes: GPC-4 upregulation increases LRP1 membrane trafficking, promoting tau propagation and hyperphosphorylation in neurons, and impairing APOE4-mediated miRNA transfer to neurons.
- **Boundary conditions**: An astrocyte-specific mechanism (contrast with microglial TREM2/ITGB8-TGFβ pathways).
- **Related concepts**: LDLR, tau propagation, astrocytes

## ITGB8–TGFβ signaling
- **Notation**: ITGB8-TGFβ
- **Definition**: A microglial signaling pathway that APOE4 activates to specifically suppress Aβ42 clearance and inhibit MGnD neuroprotective function; selective ablation of microglial APOE4 restores phagocytic function and reduces plaque burden by relieving this suppression.
- **Boundary conditions**: Distinct from the TREM2/LilrB3 axes; specifically implicated in Aβ42 (not general Aβ) clearance suppression and MGnD-function inhibition.
- **Related concepts**: TREM2, MGnD, Inpp5d, Aβ42

## NLRP3 inflammasome
- **Notation**: NLRP3
- **Definition**: An intracellular multiprotein complex whose activation is increased in APOE4 microglia in the basal state and further amplified by LPS/IFN-γ stimulation, driving reactive oxygen species (ROS) production and proinflammatory cytokine release (TNF-α, IL-1β, NOS2, MCP1).
- **Boundary conditions**: A therapeutic target (JC-124, dihydromyricetin, DAPPD, dapansutrile/OLT1177 are NLRP3 inhibitors surveyed in Table 1).
- **Related concepts**: ROS, cytokines, LilrB3, sex-differences

## Lipofuscin
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: An intracellular pigment/aggregate that accumulates in APOE4 neurons downstream of excessive APOE4-LDLR binding, increased lipid uptake, and lysosomal dysfunction; its accumulation is linked to impaired autophagy and subsequent tau protein aggregation.
- **Boundary conditions**: A marker of neuronal lysosomal/autophagic failure specific to the neuronal lipid-handling lesion described in the review (distinct from microglial LD/cholesterol pathology).
- **Related concepts**: LDLR, autophagy, lysosomal dysfunction, tau aggregation

## GIRK3 (G protein-gated inwardly rectifying potassium channel 3)
- **Notation**: GIRK3
- **Definition**: A neuronal potassium channel upregulated in response to extracellular cholesterol accumulated from impaired microglial lipid reuptake; its upregulation hyperpolarizes neuronal resting membrane potential, collectively reducing neuronal excitability.
- **Boundary conditions**: A specific downstream neuronal functional consequence of microglial (not neuronal) APOE4-driven cholesterol/lipid dysregulation — illustrates microglia-to-neuron lipid-mediated signaling.
- **Related concepts**: Neuronal excitability, cholesterol, microglia-neuron communication

## Hexokinase 2 (HK2) — lipid metabolic coupling
- **Notation**: HK2
- **Definition**: A glycolytic enzyme upregulated in AD microglia; pharmacological inhibition of HK2 activates lipoprotein lipase, increasing lipid metabolism and sustaining ATP production, thereby promoting Aβ clearance — a compensatory mechanism reported as microglia-specific (not observed in other brain cells).
- **Boundary conditions**: Distinguishes a potentially protective glycolysis-lipid coupling from the pathological glycolytic shift described elsewhere in APOE4 microglia (C04) — context (pharmacological HK2 inhibition vs uncontrolled glycolytic reprogramming) determines whether the outcome is adaptive or pathological.
- **Related concepts**: Glycolysis, lipoprotein lipase, ATP, Aβ clearance

## "Enhanced synthesis–suppressed degradation" imbalance
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The review's summary phrase for the core microglial lipid lesion: APOE4 simultaneously upregulates lipid/triglyceride synthesis (FAS) and lipid-droplet formation while suppressing degradative pathways (lipophagy, fatty acid oxidation), yielding net pathological lipid-droplet accumulation.
- **Boundary conditions**: Applies specifically to microglia in this review; astrocytes and neurons show related but distinct imbalances (SREBP2/PPARγ in astrocytes; LDLR-driven uptake/lipofuscin in neurons).
- **Related concepts**: LDAM, FAS, FAO, lipophagy
