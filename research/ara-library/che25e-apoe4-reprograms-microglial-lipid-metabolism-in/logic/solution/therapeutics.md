# Therapeutic Strategies Targeting Microglial APOE4 in AD

*Grounding: full-text §5, Table 1. Fourteen agents are surveyed across four target-pathway groups.
Mechanisms, models, and reported effects below are copied from the review; exact tabulated content
(agent/mechanism/effect/note/reference per row) lives in `evidence/tables/table1.md` — this file
adds the narrative context and body-text detail (e.g., DGAT2/PLIN2) not captured as separate table
rows.*

## Target: Lipid Metabolism

- **TRPV1/capsaicin**: Inhibits SREBP-2, enhances autophagy activity. Alleviates cholesterol
  biosynthesis in APOE4 microglia; reduces APOE4 microglial phagocytosis of synapses. Specific to
  APOE4 microglia. (refs 96,100)
- **LXR agonist (GW3965), CS-6253**: Increases cholesterol efflux in glial cells. Reduces lipid
  accumulation in glial cells; attenuates tau pathology. Specific to APOE4 microglia. (refs
  51,129,131)
- **Bexarotene** (RXR agonist): Increases APOE4 lipidation. Reduces Aβ and p-tau accumulation;
  reverses cognitive and neuronal impairments. Specific to APOE4; clinical trial NCT01782742.
  (ref 130)
- **Body-text-only additions (not separate Table 1 rows)**: Pharmacological inhibition of
  diacylglycerol O-acyltransferase 2 (**DGAT2**) in microglia suppresses triglyceride biosynthesis
  and subsequent LD accumulation; genetic ablation of **perilipin 2 (PLIN2)** promotes LD
  degradation and attenuates neuroinflammation (§5, refs 132,133).

## Target: Neuro-inflammation

- **JC-124, dihydromyricetin, DAPPD, dapansutrile (OLT1177)**: NLRP3 inflammasome inhibitors.
  Suppresses neuroinflammation; promotes Aβ clearance. Targeting the NLRP3 inflammasome.
  (refs 135-138)
- **Ubiquitin ligase COP1**: Modulates C/EBP levels and inhibits pro-inflammatory gene expression in
  microglia. Reduces microglial activation and neurotoxicity; suppresses neuroinflammation.
  (ref 139)
- **Resveratrol, curcumin** (traditional Chinese herbal medicine): Reduces neuroinflammation;
  neuroprotective. (refs 140-142)
- **Dimethyl malonate**: Inhibits succinate dehydrogenase (SDH) and HIF-1α expression in microglia.
  Enhances mitochondrial function; suppresses neuroinflammation. (ref 134)

## Target: Phagocytosis

- **Sodium rutin (NaR), desloratadine**: Increase expression of phagocytic receptors in microglia.
  Enhances microglial phagocytosis of Aβ. (refs 143,144)
- **DW14006** (AMPKα1 activator): Upregulates CD36 and modulates AMPKα1/IκB/NFκB signaling in
  microglia. Enhances microglial phagocytosis of Aβ; suppresses neuroinflammation. (ref 145)
- **AL002c** (TREM2 activator): Reduces Aβ plaques and neurite dystrophy; tempers microglial
  inflammatory response. Phase I clinical trial NCT03635047; phase II clinical trial NCT04592874.
  (refs 146-148)

## Target: APOE4

- **Aβ12-28p, CN-105** (small-molecule mimetics): Disrupt the interaction between APOE and Aβ.
  Reduces Aβ pathology. Specific to APOE4. (refs 149-151)
- **HAE-4** (anti-human APOE4 antibody): Reduces Aβ plaques; inhibits tau propagation and neuritic
  dystrophy. Specific to APOE4. (refs 152,153)
- **LX1001** (AAV-delivered human APOEε2 gene therapy, intracisternal): Increases APOE2 levels in
  the brain of APOE4-homozygous patients. Ameliorates tau pathology. Clinical trial NCT03634007
  (with long-term follow-up NCT05400330). (refs 156,157; also 154,155 for the underlying AAV-APOE2
  preclinical rationale)
- **CRISPR-Cas9** (gene editing): Potential to convert APOE4 to other isoforms. Technical, ethical,
  and safety challenges. (ref 158)

## Cross-cutting notes

- **Claims supported**: C13 (overall therapeutic-landscape claim); individual mechanisms also
  support C01/C02 (lipid-metabolism agents), C03/C04 (neuro-inflammation agents), C05/C06
  (phagocytosis agents).
- **Structure of the table**: 14 rows total across 4 target-pathway groups (Lipid Metabolism: 3;
  Neuro-inflammation: 4; Phagocytosis: 3; APOE4: 4) — see `evidence/tables/table1.md` for the
  verbatim table.
- **Clinical-trial identifiers appearing in the paper**: NCT01782742 (bexarotene), NCT03635047 and
  NCT04592874 (AL002c, phase I and phase II respectively), NCT03634007 and NCT05400330 (LX1001,
  initial and long-term follow-up).
- **Limitations** (detailed in `constraints.md`): no agent yet specifically corrects the microglial-
  lipid-metabolism axis (§5); translating animal/cellular findings to human efficacy remains
  challenging; full-scale APOE4 interventions risk toxic side effects, motivating cell-type-specific
  precision approaches; CRISPR-based APOE editing carries acknowledged technical/ethical/safety
  challenges.
