# Figure 4: Microglial APOE4 promotes neuroinflammation and inhibits the clearance of pathological proteins in AD

- **Source**: Figure 4, p.9, Chen et al. (2025), *BioScience Trends*, Advance Publication, doi:10.5582/bst.2025.01148, §4.2-§4.3
- **Caption**: "Figure 4. Microglial APOE4 promotes neuroinflammation and inhibits the clearance of
  pathological proteins in AD. (A). Microglial APOE4 promotes neuroinflammation by interacting with
  LilrB3 to upregulate ISGs, or inhibiting Cxorf56 to enhance TNF-α secretion in AD. In addition,
  NLRP3 inflammasomes and ROS were increased in APOE4 microglia, and more inflammatory factors were
  secreted in response to LPS and IFN-γ stimulation, further confirming the involvement of APOE4 in
  microglia-mediated neuroinflammation. (B). Upper part: APOE accumulates around Aβ plaques,
  compacting diffuse Aβ into less toxic core plaques and restricting their dissemination within the
  brain. Lower part: microglial APOE4 inhibits Aβ clearance, promotes Aβ plaque formation, reduces
  tau degradation, and facilitates tau spread via exosomes. Meanwhile, APOE4 microglia enhance
  synaptic phagocytosis near Aβ plaques, correlating with cognitive decline. ISGs,
  interferon-stimulating genes; LPS, lipopolysaccharide; IFN-γ, interferon-gamma."

## Visual description

**Panel A — direct/receptor-mediated and cell-intrinsic inflammatory activation**: A yellow
branched microglia icon contains: a nucleus with a blue nucleic-acid strand looping into the
cytoplasm labeled "CXorf56" (blue tangled loop, with an inhibitory ⊣ connector from "ROS" and from
the "nAPOE4(1-151) fragment," shown as a straight blue line, both pointing at CXorf56); a
"NLRP3 inflammasome" icon (pink starburst rosette) at upper-left with small red virus-like APOE4
icons nearby; and a membrane-spanning "LilrB3" receptor (magenta zigzag) at lower-left. Outputs on
the right, each with a red up-arrow: "25-HC↑" (triggered by an "LPS" input arrow), which points to
"IL-1β↑"; a cluster of colored dot icons representing "NOS2↑," "MCP1↑," and "TNF-α↑," bracketed
together under combined "LPS / IFN-γ" stimulus inputs. At the bottom, a boxed label reads "ISGs:
IFITM3, BST2, MX1 and STAT1↑" with a red up-arrow, connected upward to the LilrB3 receptor.

**Panel B — phagocytic clearance of Aβ, tau, and synapses**: Divided by a horizontal dashed line
into an upper (physiological/APOE-mediated compaction) and lower (APOE4-impaired) half.
- Upper half: An orange microglial process extends toward two Aβ-aggregate icons (a looser
  "diffuse" plaque and a denser "compact" plaque, shown converting into one another via a circular
  arrow), with "APOE" labeled entering a "TREM2" receptor on the microglia, which signals to
  "cholesterol efflux" (small hexagon/particle icons).
- Lower half: A red/pink neuron with a synapse inset (magnified callout box showing a
  synaptic-cleft cross-section with small tau-fibril fragments inside) connects via "ITGB8" and
  "TGFβ" receptor-stem icons to the microglia, with an inhibitory ⊣ connector from ITGB8/TGFβ to
  "clearance." Inside the neuron/microglia interface, a lysosome-like vesicle containing tau
  fibrils is shown budding exosomes (small circles) that migrate outward, and an "Inpp5d↑" gene
  label (blue double-helix) with a red up-arrow sits centrally, connected upward toward the
  TREM2/cholesterol-efflux node (inhibitory bar, indicating Inpp5d↑ suppresses that pathway). At
  far right, an "APOE4 neutrophils" icon (pink round cell) connects via small green/pink Y-shaped
  receptor icons labeled "IL-17RA" and "IL-17F" to the neuron-microglia junction.

**Shared legend** (bottom, spanning both panels): APOE4 (dark red triangle), NLRP3 inflammasome
(pink rosette), nAPOE4(1-151) fragment (blue looped line), CXorf56 (blue tangle), LilrB3 (magenta
zigzag), synapse (pink oval pair), tau (dark fibril), Aβ plaque (diffuse, loose brown aggregate),
Aβ plaque (dense, compact brown aggregate), Aβ fibres (green fibril), IL-17RA (green Y-receptor),
IL-17F (pink Y-ligand).

**What it conveys**: Panel A visually separates the two neuroinflammatory routes the review
describes — direct receptor-mediated (LilrB3→ISGs; nAPOE4 fragment ⊣ Cxorf56 → TNF-α) and
basal/stimulus-amplified (NLRP3/ROS; LPS/IFN-γ→cytokines) — supporting claim C03. Panel B visually
contrasts APOE's normal Aβ-compaction/clearance role (upper) against APOE4's impairment of Aβ, tau,
and synaptic clearance via ITGB8-TGFβ/Inpp5d and the female-specific IL-17F/IL-17RA axis (lower),
supporting claims C05 and C06.
