# Figure 3: Microglial APOE4-mediated dysregulation of lipid metabolism in AD

- **Source**: Figure 3, p.7, Chen et al. (2025), *BioScience Trends*, Advance Publication, doi:10.5582/bst.2025.01148, §4.1
- **Caption**: "Figure 3. Microglial APOE4-mediated dysregulation of lipid metabolism in AD.
  Microglial APOE4-mediated upregulation of triglyceride synthesis exacerbates the accumulation of
  LDs under Aβ stimulation, whereas TREM2-R47H reduces intracellular LDs accumulation. In addition,
  microglial APOE4 promotes cholesterol synthesis via the ER stress-ER Ca2+-SREBP2 pathway and
  impairs cholesterol efflux, affecting Aβ degradation in lysosomes. The accumulation of
  extracellular cholesterol increased GIRK3 and decreased neuronal excitability. Microglial APOE4
  also decreased lipid transport efficiency and disrupted lipid metabolic coupling in neurons.
  Ultimately, phagocytosis-impaired APOE4 microglia release pro-inflammatory cytokines and ROS that
  promote neuronal lipogenesis and translocation to microglia, creating a vicious cycle. ACSL,
  acyl-CoA synthetase long-chain; ER, endoplasmic reticulum; GIRK3, G protein-gated inwardly
  rectifying potassium channel 3; ROS, reactive oxygen species; LDs, lipid droplets; TREM2,
  triggering receptor expressed on myeloid cells 2."

## Visual description

**Upper region (neuron-microglia crosstalk)**: A neuron icon at top-left shows internal potassium
(K+) ions and a membrane channel labeled "GIRK3↑" with a red up-arrow; the neuron's dendritic
region contains lipid-droplet and Aβ-fibril icons. To its right, a dashed circular arrow labeled
"neuron ⇄ microglia" with "efficiency↓" (red down-arrow) links the neuron's axon terminal
(containing an APOE4-lipoprotein icon) back down to the microglia below. A "ROS↑" label (red
starburst icon with up-arrow) sits between the neuron and the microglia, connected by an arrow
pointing up toward GIRK3, indicating ROS drives the extracellular-cholesterol-to-GIRK3 pathway.

**Central microglia cell**: A tan branched microglia icon containing a nucleus, lipid-droplet
(yellow circle) icons, and APOE4 triangle icons, with brown Aβ aggregates adjacent to it.

**Lower boxed region (intracellular signaling detail)**: Below the membrane line, a shaded box
contains the core signaling diagram:
- Left arm: "fibrillar Aβ" binds two receptor stems, "TREM2" and "TREM2-R47H" side by side. TREM2
  signals down to "ACSL1, PLIN2↑" (boxed, with a "TG↑" triglyceride icon above) via "PU.1"; a
  mitochondrial "stress" icon (red starburst) sits centrally, connected to both TREM2-R47H (via an
  inhibitory ⊣ connector, i.e., TREM2-R47H reduces the stress/LD-accumulation node) and to lipid
  droplet icons.
- Center arm: "APOE4" gene icon (double helix) transcribed via "PU.1" and "NF-κB" transcription
  factor icons; NF-κB also connects to "complement and lysosomal pathways" (inhibited, ⊣ connector).
- Right arm: "SREBP2" (double helix) transcribed into mRNA, arrow up to the ER (shown as a
  membranous organelle icon) labeled "ER stress" (red lightning bolt icons) with "Ca2+↓" (green
  circle with down arrow) — ER stress feeds forward to "HMGCR mRNA / SQLE mRNA" → "cholesterol"
  (yellow hexagon icons).
- A "reduced cholesterol efflux" label sits above two purple ATP-transporter (ABCA1-like) icons
  showing ATP-driven cholesterol transport, with an inhibitory connector down to a "degradation"
  lysosome icon (pink-ringed circle) that fails to process an Aβ-fibril icon.

**Legend** (bottom): APOE4 (dark red triangle), TG (yellow bracket icon), cholesterol (yellow
hexagon), LDs (yellow circle cluster), Aβ (brown aggregate), APOE4-containing lipoprotein (orange
particle in blue-ringed circle), proinflammatory cytokines (small blue dots).

**What it conveys**: This is the review's most mechanistically dense figure, integrating three
parallel intracellular lesions (triglyceride/LD synthesis via PU.1/NF-κB; cholesterol overload via
ER stress-SREBP2; reduced cholesterol efflux via impaired ABCA1-like transport) with the
TREM2-R47H context-dependent modulation (explicitly shown as reducing the stress/LD node,
consistent with claim C10's in vivo finding) and the downstream neuron-microglia GIRK3/ROS feedback
loop (claim C01/C02 mechanistic support).

## Notes
