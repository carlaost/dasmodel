## Visual description

A three-box synthesis diagram with a top "core" box and two downstream boxes, joined by directional
and bidirectional arrows:

- **Top box — "Abnormal lipid metabolism"** (blue header bar): contains a central "APOE4 microglia"
  cell icon flanked by two bulleted lists: left — "metabolic reprogramming" (enhanced glycolysis;
  inhibition of the TAC [TCA cycle]; increased lipid synthesis); right — "lipid abnormal" (LDs
  accumulation; decreased cholesterol efflux; decreased membrane fluidity).
- **Bottom-left box — "Neuroinflammation"** (blue header bar): fed by a bidirectional pair of arrows
  from the top box (one down into Neuroinflammation, one back up from it — i.e., the relationship
  is drawn as reciprocal, not one-directional). Inside: "lipid peroxides and cholesterol" icons →
  two parallel arms — "NF-κB" icon → "activated NF-κB signaling" → "proinflammatory cytokine
  secretion"; and an antigen-presenting-cell icon → "MHC-II antigen presentation" → "overactivated
  T cells" — both arms converging downward into "activated neuroinflammation / neuronal
  degeneration" (illustrated with a damaged/fragmented neuron network icon).
- **Bottom-right box — "Phagocytosis"** (blue header bar): fed by a one-directional arrow down from
  the top box, labeled with two pathway names beside the connecting arrow: "APOE-TREM2-TYROBP" and
  "APOE4-ITGB8-TGFβ." Inside: "disrupted cholesterol homeostasis" and "mitochondrial dysfunction"
  (with an ATP icon) both feed down into "lysosomal dysfunction," which feeds into "degradation"
  (with an inhibitory ⊣ mark on the arrow from mitochondrial dysfunction to degradation, indicating
  suppressed degradation capacity) → "Aβ plaques / tau spread / synaptic impairment" (illustrated
  with plaque, tau-fibril, and synapse-damage icons).
- A horizontal double-headed connector labeled "proinflammatory phenotype" links the bottom-left
  (Neuroinflammation) and bottom-right (Phagocytosis) boxes directly, with an inhibitory (⊣) mark
  on the Phagocytosis side — i.e., the proinflammatory phenotype arising from neuroinflammation
  further suppresses phagocytic function.

**What it conveys**: This is the review's single-page synthesis of its own central thesis — lipid
metabolic dysregulation in microglia (top) is the shared upstream driver of both neuroinflammation
and impaired phagocytosis (bottom two boxes), which are themselves linked by a
neuroinflammation-suppresses-phagocytosis relationship, together accelerating AD pathology. It
visually operationalizes the "self-perpetuating cycle" language of §6 (the "vicious cycle" node in
`logic/solution/mechanisms.md` Stage 6) and directly supports the synthesis claim C13 and the
problem statement's Key Insight (`logic/problem.md`).
