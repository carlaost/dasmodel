# Constraints, Assumptions, and Limitations

## Boundary conditions
- Analyses cover the 78-week LTE period of TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 (NCT04437511), following the 76-week placebo-controlled period, for a total of 154 weeks (~3 years) of observation.
- Early-start = randomized to donanemab in the placebo-controlled period; delayed-start = randomized to placebo, started donanemab at LTE entry. Both groups may be switched to blinded placebo mid-LTE upon meeting treatment course completion criteria (<11 CL single scan or <25 CL two consecutive scans).
- The LTE has no internal placebo control; CDR-SB efficacy comparisons use a propensity-score-weighted external control cohort from ADNI.
- Amyloid clearance is defined as <24.1 CL, distinct from (higher than) the <11/<25 CL treatment course completion thresholds.
- All statistical analyses are exploratory and not controlled for multiplicity (§2.5, §4).

## Assumptions
- A1: The propensity-score-weighted ADNI cohort (matched on age, sex, APOE ε4 status, CDR-SB, ADAS-Cog13, screening MMSE) is an acceptable proxy for the untreated disease trajectory of TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 participants.
- A2: Omitting baseline amyloid CL from the weighting covariates (missing for 43% of the eligible ADNI-derived population) does not materially bias the weighted comparison.
- A3: Any CDR-G increase confirmed at two consecutive visits represents a clinically meaningful disease-stage transition suitable as a hazard-model endpoint.
- A4: MCID thresholds developed for individual patient-level change are not appropriate for interpreting between-group CDR-SB differences at a study endpoint (§4, citing [16]); CDR-G stage transition and time-saved framings are used instead.
- A5: Combining this LTE's amyloid data with three earlier donanemab studies (phase 1b, TRAILBLAZER-ALZ, TRAILBLAZER-EXT) into a single exposure-response model yields a valid pooled reaccumulation-rate estimate.

## Known limitations (stated in the paper, §4 Discussion)
- L1: The LTE lacks a true (internal) placebo comparator; the external ADNI control cohort may differ from the trial population due to differences in study conduct, assessments, time periods, geographic regions, and other unmeasured confounding factors.
- L2: Potential survivor bias over time — participants remaining in the LTE by 3 years are a subset of those originally randomized, and this subset may differ systematically from those who discontinued.
- L3: The majority (71.5%) of early-start participants who continued into the LTE received only blinded placebo infusions for the entire LTE duration; the observed early-start LTE efficacy therefore reflects sustained benefit after treatment discontinuation, not continued active dosing, for most of this group.
- L4: Delayed-start participants who entered the LTE had more severe disease at LTE start than the overall study population had at the start of the placebo-controlled period; almost half would not have met the study's initial MMSE-based eligibility criteria. This creates a plausible bias favoring the early-start group in early- vs delayed-start comparisons (e.g., C04, C06).
- L5: Inclusion criteria may limit generalizability to broader clinical populations; however, the paper notes that TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2's comorbidities and co-medications were consistent with the Medicare population [23].
- L6: Time-saved calculations (C06) do not account for the generally higher rate of disease progression at later disease stages, which the paper states likely causes the reported 1.3-month early-vs-delayed-start gap to understate the true benefit of earlier treatment.
- L7: Established CDR-SB minimal clinically important difference (MCID) thresholds are not applicable to interpreting the LTE's between-group differences at a study endpoint (they were derived for individual-patient change) — the paper relies instead on CDR-G stage transition and time-saved framings for clinical-meaningfulness claims.
- L8: Results reflect the TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2 standard dosing/titration regimen; a more gradual titration used in TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 6 has since been shown to significantly reduce ARIA-E risk relative to this regimen [22], so this paper's ARIA rates may not generalize to that newer titration approach.
- L9: Future analyses are needed to evaluate other AD-relevant biomarkers, such as plasma P-tau217, which were not reported in this LTE analysis.
