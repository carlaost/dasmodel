# Constraints

## Boundary Conditions
- The assay's diagnostic-performance figures (sensitivity, specificity, AUC, PPV/NPV) are specific to the [18F]MK6240 tau-PET reference standard and to the visual Braak-like staging protocol described in Methods (PDF p.13–14); they are not established against post-mortem neuropathology or against other tau-PET tracers.
- PPV/NPV figures (C03) depend on externally sourced tau-PET positivity prevalence priors (10% CU, 60% CI, from Moscoso et al. 2025), not on this study's own (enriched) sample prevalence; applying these PPV/NPV figures to a population with a different true prevalence would be invalid.
- The pTau217 comparator thresholds (0.142/0.193/0.256 pg/mL) were originally calibrated for amyloid-PET status in an independent cohort (ref 19), not for tau-PET status; using them as tau-PET-prediction thresholds is itself a comparison choice made by this paper, and may understate pTau217's best-case tau-specific performance had thresholds been separately optimized for tau-PET.
- The VeraBIND Tau test result Score is a within-run, Standard-relative measure ("Score = Unknown RLU / (Standard RLU × Correction Factor)"); it is not a validated absolute concentration and is not directly comparable across different Standard lots without the stated correction factor.

## Assumptions
- Visual Braak-like [18F]MK6240 tau-PET staging (stage>0 = T+) is accepted as the in vivo ground truth for tau aggregation pathology, despite the tracer's documented limited affinity for non-AD (e.g., 4R) tauopathies (Discussion, PDF p.25, citing Gérard et al. 2025, ref 51) — a limitation the authors themselves invoke to explain apparent VeraBIND Tau "false positives" in A-T- and A+T- discordant cases (C12).
- Amyloid-PET (Centiloid≥20) and CSF Aβ42 (≤544 pg/mL) positivity are treated as interchangeable amyloid-status determinations within the same cohort (n=101 by PET, n=44 by lumbar puncture).
- The CU volunteer subsample was deliberately enriched for APOE ε4 carriers to match the patient (CI) subsample's carriership frequency (Methods, PDF p.7); this raises the CU subsample's baseline AD risk relative to a representative general-population CU sample.

## Known Limitations (stated by the authors, Discussion, PDF p.26)
- **No independent validation cohort**: All diagnostic-performance and threshold-comparison results (C01–C09) come from a single cohort; the authors explicitly state validation in independent cohorts is needed before clinical deployment.
- **Homogeneous sample**: The cohort is described as "relatively homogenous ... at the educational and racial levels," limiting generalizability across educational/racial strata.
- **APOE ε4 enrichment bias**: The CU subsample's APOE ε4 enrichment (vs. general population) means measured CU-specific accuracy/PPV figures (C03) may not directly generalize to an unselected population-screening context with a different genetic-risk composition.
- **Non-AD tauopathy specificity untested with more sensitive tracers**: The suggestion that VeraBIND Tau detects non-AD tauopathies (C12) is based on only 6 discordant cases (1 corticobasal-degeneration-suspected, 5 PART/FTLD-suspected) and needs confirmation with tau-PET tracers more sensitive to 4R tauopathy (e.g., not [18F]MK6240).
- **Longitudinal monitoring utility for anti-tau therapy not yet established**: The authors state that "further longitudinal work is required before considering the use of VeraBIND Tau for monitoring the effects of anti-tau therapies" — the rate-of-change correlations (C09) are modest in magnitude (|rs| 0.21–0.38) and are explicitly framed as preliminary.
- **Data/analysis timing note**: Recruitment and examinations spanned June 2019–April 2025 (nearly 6 years), and biomarker measurements (VeraBIND Tau, amyloid status, cognition) were not always collected on the same day as tau-PET (average delays of 0.4, 0.8, and 0.5 years respectively) — introducing some temporal misalignment into the cross-sectional comparisons.

They do not by themselves invalidate the reported statistics, but they are directly relevant to how strongly this single, non-independently-validated, industry-measured dataset should be weighted (compounding the "no independent validation cohort" limitation above) — an external, developer-independent replication is the natural next step implied by this conflict structure.
