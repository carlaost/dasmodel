# Figure 1: VeraBIND Tau assay

- **Source**: Figure 1, "Tables and Figures" section, PDF p.43
- **Caption**: "Fig. 1. VeraBIND Tau assay. The VeraBIND Tau assay uses a pool of different antibody-coated capture beads to capture hyperphosphorylated tau (pTau) in the plasma sample. The capture beads are washed to remove the plasma matrix, and buffer-exchanged/washed into a normal Tau binding buffer that mimic the pH, ionic and hydrophobic binding environment in the brain. The assay adds recombinant full-length normal Tau 1-441 with a recombinant tag at the C-terminus (recombinant normal tau) to determine if any of the pTau captured on the beads is pathologically active (PA pTau). If pTau captured from the plasma is pathologically active, it will bind the recombinant normal tau like it does in the brain with tau pathology. The final step washes the capture beads to remove unbound normal tau, adds an alkaline phosphatase conjugated anti-tag monoclonal antibody (AP Conjugate), washes the capture beads to remove unbound AP Conjugate, and adds Substrate to generate a relative luminescence signal (RLU) that is directly proportional to the amount of recombinant normal tau bound by PA pTau in the plasma sample."

## Visual description
- **Components (left, process flowchart)**: Conditioned and Cleaned Sample → First Reaction → Washing → Second Reaction → Washing → Third Reaction → Washing → Enzyme Reaction → Luminescence Measurement (nine sequential boxes connected by downward arrows).
- **Components (right, molecular schematic titled "VeraBIND Tau Assay: PA pTau-Specific Seeding Signal")**: A pTau strand (labeled N-terminus/C-terminus, with red dots depicting phosphorylation sites) representing "Pathologically Active Hyperphosphorylated Tau (PA pTau)"; a Y-shaped "Anti-pTau Solid Phase" antibody attached to an orange bead; a looped strand labeled "Recombinant Normal Tau-441" with an N-terminus, C-terminus, and an orange "Tag" square; a second Y-shaped "Anti-Tag AP Conjugate" antibody (shown with an embedded ribbon-structure icon representing the alkaline phosphatase enzyme); a final assembled complex (bead + anti-pTau antibody + PA pTau + recombinant normal tau + anti-tag AP conjugate) connected by an arrow to "Substrate" and then to a burst-icon labeled "PA pTau-Specific Signal."
- **Connections**: Sequential "+" signs join PA pTau → Anti-pTau Solid Phase → Recombinant Normal Tau-441 → Anti-Tag AP Conjugate, culminating in the assembled bead complex; an open arrow leads from the assembled complex to Substrate, then another open arrow leads to the luminescent "PA pTau-Specific Signal" burst icon. The left-hand flowchart and the right-hand molecular schematic are two representations of the same sequential process (steps 1-9 of the flowchart correspond to the capture → wash → bind → wash → detect → wash → develop → read sequence shown molecularly on the right).
- **Annotations**: Red dots on the pTau strand denote phosphorylation sites; the orange square on Recombinant Normal Tau-441 denotes its C-terminal detection tag; the bead is drawn as a solid orange circle (solid-phase support).
- **What it conveys**: The assay's core structural claim is that only *pathologically active* pTau — captured from plasma and shown to actively bind recombinant normal tau under brain-like conditions — produces a luminescent signal; passively phosphorylated (non-seeding-competent) tau captured on the same beads would not bind the recombinant normal tau substrate and would generate no signal, which is the assay's basis for functional (not just concentration-based) specificity.
