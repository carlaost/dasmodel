# Table 14 - Number of geriatricians in 2021 and projected number needed in 2050 by state
- **Source**: Table 14, §6.1.1, pp. 46-47 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Number of geriatricians in 2021 and projected number of geriatricians needed in 2050 by state." Assumes 30% of people 65+ have complex medical needs benefiting from geriatrician care; each geriatrician can care for up to 700 patients. Alzheimer's prevalence rates: 65-74 ~5.2%, 75-84 ~13.8%, 85+ ~35.8% (Figure 5; Rajan et al. ref 276). 2021 counts from American Geriatrics Society (ref 771).

**Note on internal source discrepancy (reproduced verbatim, not corrected):** The narrative text (§6.1.1, p. 46) states "there were only 7,454 certified geriatricians in 2021" and "An estimated 14,480 geriatricians will be needed to care for the approximately 10.1 million individuals age 65 and older who are projected to have Alzheimer's dementia in 2050," whereas this Table 14 gives a 2021 U.S. total of 7,066 and a 2050 Alzheimer's-need total of 14,656. Both figures are attributed to the same American Geriatrics Society source. The discrepancy is preserved here as it appears in the report.
