# Table 3A - Clinical Staging for individuals on the Alzheimer's disease continuum
- **Source**: Table 3A, §2.2, p. 7 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Clinical Staging for individuals on the Alzheimer's disease continuum." Created from data from Jack et al. (ref 38).
- **Screenshot**: table3A.png
- **Extraction type**: raw_table

| Stage | Label | Description (summary) |
|------|------|------|
| Stage 0 | Asymptomatic, deterministic gene* | No evidence of clinical change. Biomarkers (beta-amyloid, tau) in normal range. |
| Stage 1 | Asymptomatic, biomarker evidence only | Performance within expected range on cognitive tests; no recent decline/new symptoms; biomarker evidence of Alzheimer's (elevated beta-amyloid, abnormal tau). |
| Stage 2 | Transitional decline: mild detectable change, minimal impact on daily function | Normal cognitive test performance; decline from baseline within past 1-3 years, persistent ≥6 months; may show subtle decline, subjective report, or mood/anxiety/motivation change; remains fully independent in ADLs. |
| Stage 3 | Cognitive impairment with early functional impact | Impaired/abnormal range on objective tests; decline documented; performs ADLs independently but detectable impact on complex (instrumental) ADLs. |
| Stage 4 | Dementia with mild functional impairment | Progressive cognitive and mild functional impairment on instrumental ADLs; independent in basic ADLs. |
| Stage 5 | Dementia with moderate functional impairment | Progressive cognitive and moderate functional impairment on basic ADLs, requiring assistance. |
| Stage 6 | Dementia with severe functional impairment | Progressive cognitive/functional impairment; complete dependence for basic ADLs. |

*Individuals with Down syndrome may not be fully independent even in stage 0 due to underlying intellectual disability.
