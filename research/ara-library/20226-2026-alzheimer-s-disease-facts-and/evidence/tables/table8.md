# Table 8 - Dementia caregiving tasks
- **Source**: Table 8, §5 (Caregiving), p. 33 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Dementia caregiving tasks."
- **Screenshot**: table8.png
- **Extraction type**: raw_table

| Caregiving task |
|------|
| Helping with instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs): household chores, shopping, preparing meals, transportation, arranging doctor's appointments, managing finances and legal affairs, answering the telephone |
| Helping the person take medications correctly (reminders or direct administration) |
| Helping the person adhere to treatment recommendations for dementia or other medical conditions |
| Assisting with personal activities of daily living (ADLs): bathing, dressing, grooming, feeding, walking, transferring bed to chair, toileting, managing incontinence |
| Managing behavioral symptoms: wandering, depressive mood, agitation, anxiety, repetitive activity, nighttime disturbances |
| Finding and using support services such as support groups and adult day service programs |
| Making arrangements for paid in-home, nursing home or assisted living care |
| Hiring and supervising others who provide care |
| Assuming additional responsibilities: overall management of getting through the day; addressing family issues (communication, decision-making, respite); managing comorbidities (arthritis, diabetes, cancer); providing emotional support and a sense of security |
