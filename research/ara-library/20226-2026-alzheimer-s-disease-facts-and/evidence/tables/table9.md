# Table 9 - Who are dementia caregivers?
- **Source**: Table 9, §5.1, p. 34 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Who are dementia caregivers?"
- **Screenshot**: table9.png
- **Extraction type**: raw_table

| Category | Findings |
|------|------|
| Sex/gender | Approximately two-thirds of dementia caregivers are women. |
| Race/ethnicity | Two-thirds of caregivers are White; 10% Black; 8% Hispanic; 5% Asian American; remaining 10% other populations. |
| Living status | Most (66%) live with the person with dementia in the community; over 60% married/partnered/long-term relationship; ~one-quarter are "sandwich generation" (also caring for ≥1 child). |
| Caring for parents | Over half provide assistance to a parent or in-law; among primary caregivers, over half care for their parents. |
| Income | 41% have a household income of \$50,000 or less. |
| Education | ~40% have a college degree or more. |
| Age | ~30% are age 65 or older; 23% of caregivers ages 18-49 help someone with dementia (an increase of 7% between 2015 and 2021). |
| Caring for spouse | ~10% provide help to a spouse. |
