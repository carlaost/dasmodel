# Table 1 - Common types of dementia
- **Source**: Table 1, §2.1.2 (Overview), pp. 4-5 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Common types of dementia."
- **Screenshot**: table1.png (page 3; continues page 4)
- **Extraction type**: raw_table

| Type | Brain changes (summary) | Percentage of dementia cases | Symptoms (summary) |
|------|------|------|------|
| Alzheimer's disease | Beta-amyloid outside neurons + tau tangles inside neurons; neuron death, inflammation, atrophy | Most common type; est. 60% to 80% of cases; most also have ≥1 other type (mixed pathology) | Difficulty remembering recent conversations/names/events; apathy; depression early; later communication problems, confusion, poor judgment; late-stage difficulty walking, speaking, swallowing |
| Cerebrovascular disease (vascular dementia) | Brain blood vessels damaged / tissue injured from insufficient blood/oxygen/nutrients | ~5% to 10% show vascular dementia alone; more common as mixed pathology | Slowed thoughts, impaired decision-making/planning; memory may be affected; slow gait, poor balance |
| Frontotemporal degeneration (FTD) | Nerve cells in frontal/temporal lobes die; abnormal tau or TDP-43 | ~60% of people with FTD are 45-60; ~3% of dementia cases in studies incl. 65+, ~10% in studies restricted to <65 | Marked personality/behavior changes and/or language difficulty; memory typically spared early |
| Hippocampal sclerosis (HS) | Shrinkage/hardening of hippocampal tissue; often TDP-43 | Present in ~3% to 13% of people with dementia; ~0.4% to 2% due to HS alone | Pronounced memory loss; often misdiagnosed as Alzheimer's; common cause in age 85+ |
| Lewy body (DLB) | Alpha-synuclein clumps (Lewy bodies) in neurons/cortex | ~5% of older individuals with dementia show DLB alone; most also have Alzheimer's changes | Sleep disturbances, visual hallucinations, visuospatial impairment; Parkinson-like motor problems |
| Limbic-predominant age-related TDP-43 encephalopathy (LATE) | TDP-43 accumulates in limbic system | % unknown; autopsy of >6,000 people found 40% had TDP-43 deposits; LATE associated with memory/thinking deficits in ~25% | Similar to Alzheimer's but begin older (75+), milder, slower |
| Mixed pathologies | Brain changes of >1 type of dementia | >50% of people diagnosed with Alzheimer's dementia at Alzheimer's Disease Research Centers had mixed dementia; higher in community studies; most common in 85+ | Vary by combination of brain changes present |
| Parkinson's disease (PD) | Alpha-synuclein in substantia nigra; degeneration of dopamine-producing cells | Systematic review: 3.6% of dementia cases due to PD; 24.5% of people with PD develop dementia | Movement problems (slowness, rigidity, tremor, gait); cognitive symptoms may develop years later |

Created from data as cited within the report (refs 17-28).
