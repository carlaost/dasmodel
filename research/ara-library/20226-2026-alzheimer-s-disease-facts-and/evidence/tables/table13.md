# Table 13 - Type and focus of caregiver interventions
- **Source**: Table 13, §5.1.7, p. 43 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Type and focus of caregiver interventions." Created from data from Sörensen et al. (ref 588) and Gaugler et al. (ref 675).
- **Screenshot**: table13.png
- **Extraction type**: raw_table

| Type | Focus |
|------|------|
| Case management | Provides assessment, information, planning, referral, care coordination and/or advocacy for family caregivers. |
| Psychoeducational approaches | Structured programs providing information about the disease, resources/services, and skills to respond to symptoms; lectures, discussions, written materials; led by trained professionals. |
| Counseling | Aims to resolve preexisting personal problems that complicate caregiving; reduce conflicts; improve family functioning. |
| Psychotherapeutic approaches | Therapeutic relationship with a professional therapist (e.g., cognitive behavioral therapy for caregivers). |
| Respite | Planned, temporary relief through substitute care (adult day services, in-home or institutional respite). |
| Support groups | Less structured; opportunity to share feelings/concerns and overcome isolation. |
| Multicomponent approaches | Intensive support combining multiple forms of intervention (education, support, respite) into a single long-term service (often ≥12 months). |
