# Figure 11: Proportion of dementia caregivers vs caregivers of other older people helping with specific ADLs, U.S., 2015
- **Source**: Figure 11, §5.1.3, p. 36 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Proportion of caregivers of people with Alzheimer's or other dementias versus caregivers of other older people who provide help with specific activities of daily living, United States, 2015. Created from data from the National Alliance for Caregiving in partnership with the Alzheimer's Association (ref 495)."
- **Screenshot**: figure11.png
- **Figure type**: quantitative_plot
- **Extraction method**: exact_from_labels
- **Reading confidence**: high
- **Plot kind**: bar (grouped)
- **Axes**: X = activity (categorical), Y = Percentage (linear); series = caregivers of people with Alzheimer's/other dementias vs caregivers of other older people

| Activity | Dementia caregivers | Caregivers of other older people |
|------|------|------|
| Getting in and out of beds and chairs | 45% | 43% |
| Getting dressed | 38% | 30% |
| Bathing or showering | 34% | 23% |
| Feeding | 33% | 20% |
| Getting to and from the toilet | 32% | 25% |
| Dealing with incontinence | 32% | 12% |

## Trend summary
Dementia caregivers assist with each ADL at a higher rate than caregivers of other older people; the largest gap is dealing with incontinence (32% vs 12%).
