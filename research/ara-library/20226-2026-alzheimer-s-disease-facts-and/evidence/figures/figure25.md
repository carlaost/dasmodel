# Figure 25: Importance of factors in deciding to participate in a brain health lifestyle program
- **Source**: Figure 25, §8.6.6 (Special Report), p. 78 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "Importance of factors in deciding to participate in a brain health lifestyle program." (U-M National Poll on Healthy Aging.)
- **Screenshot**: figure25.png
- **Figure type**: quantitative_plot
- **Extraction method**: exact_from_labels
- **Reading confidence**: high
- **Plot kind**: stacked bar (100%; categories: Not at all important, Not very important, Somewhat important, Very important)
- **Axes**: X = Percentage (0-100, linear), Y = factor (categorical)

| Factor | Not at all | Not very | Somewhat | Very important |
|------|------|------|------|------|
| Cost | 3% | 3% | 21% | 73% |
| Location of program | 3% | 5% | 25% | 67% |
| Personal motivation | 3% | 4% | 34% | 59% |
| Insurance coverage | 5% | 10% | 27% | 58% |
| Coverage through employer | 11% | 9% | 28% | 51% |
| Length of program | 4% | 9% | 38% | 49% |
| Recommendation from health care provider | 8% | 16% | 45% | 31% |
| Recommendation from family/friends | 18% | 30% | 37% | 15% |

## Trend summary
Cost is the top "very important" decision factor (73%); recommendations from providers (31%) or family/friends (15%) carry the least weight.
