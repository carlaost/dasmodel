## Visual description
- **Components**: A matrix mapping FDA-approved treatments (rows, with year of approval) to the disease-continuum columns: Asymptomatic or Subtle Cognitive Change (Stages 0-2), Mild Cognitive Impairment (Stage 3), Mild Dementia (Stage 4), Moderate Dementia (Stage 5), Severe Dementia (Stage 6). A color legend distinguishes: treats symptoms (cognition and function); treats symptoms (behavior); treats symptoms (mood); slows disease progression (cognition, function and behavior).
- **Rows (year approved, drug)**: 1996 Donepezil (Aricept); 2000 Rivastigmine (Exelon); 2001 Galantamine (Razadyne); 2003 Memantine (Namenda); 2014 Memantine + Donepezil (Namzaric); 2020 Suvorexant* (Belsomra); 2023 Lecanemab (Leqembi); 2023 Brexpiprazole (Rexulti); 2024 Benzgalantamine (Zunveyl); 2024 Donanemab (Kisunla).
- **Connections/coverage bars**: Cholinesterase-inhibitor/symptomatic cognition drugs (donepezil, rivastigmine, galantamine, benzgalantamine) span Stages 4-6; memantine and memantine+donepezil span moderate-to-severe (Stages 5-6). Suvorexant (behavior/sleep) spans mild-moderate dementia. Brexpiprazole (mood/agitation) spans mild-severe dementia. The two disease-modifying anti-amyloid antibodies, lecanemab and donanemab, are indicated for Stage 3 (MCI) through Stage 4 (mild dementia) only.
- **What it conveys**: Only lecanemab and donanemab change the underlying biology and act at the early (Stage 3-4) end of the continuum; all other approved drugs treat symptoms across mild-to-severe dementia.
