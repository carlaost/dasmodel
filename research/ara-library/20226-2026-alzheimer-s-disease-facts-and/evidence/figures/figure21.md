# Figure 21: What good brain health means to adults age 40 and older
- **Source**: Figure 21, §8.6.1 (Special Report), p. 76 in 2026 Alzheimer's Disease Facts and Figures (Alzheimer's Association Report), Alzheimer's & Dementia 2026;22:e71345
- **Caption**: "What good brain health means to adults age 40 and older." (U-M National Poll on Healthy Aging; percent selecting each association.)
- **Screenshot**: figure21.png
- **Figure type**: quantitative_plot
- **Extraction method**: exact_from_labels
- **Reading confidence**: high
- **Plot kind**: bar
- **Axes**: X = association (categorical), Y = Percentage (linear)

| What good brain health means | Percentage |
|------|------|
| Thinking clearly to make sound decisions | 82% |
| Being able to carry out everyday activities without difficulty | 74% |
| Having a good memory | 73% |
| Not having a neurological condition (e.g., Alzheimer's) | 64% |
| Managing stress effectively | 60% |
| Not having a mental health condition (e.g., depression, anxiety) | 57% |
| Maintaining a good mood | 46% |

## Trend summary
Adults define good brain health primarily in functional/ability terms (thinking clearly 82%, everyday activities 74%, memory 73%) rather than absence of disease or mood (lowest, 46%).
