# Concepts

Genuine technical terms the report defines and relies on.

## Alzheimer's disease continuum
- **Notation**: — (Stages 0-6)
- **Definition**: The progression of Alzheimer's disease from brain changes unnoticeable to the person (asymptomatic) through severe dementia. The 2024 revised criteria assign a clinical stage 0-6 based on cognitive/functional status and biomarkers.
- **Boundary conditions**: Duration in each part varies by age, genetics and other factors; progression is typically along the diagonal from early biological/clinical to advanced but with wide individual variability.
- **Related concepts**: Clinical staging; biological staging; biomarkers; MCI.

## Clinical staging (Stages 0-6)
- **Notation**: Stage 0-6 (Table 3A)
- **Definition**: A clinical classification of the AD continuum: Stage 0 asymptomatic deterministic-gene carrier; Stage 1 asymptomatic with biomarker evidence; Stage 2 transitional decline (minimal functional impact); Stage 3 cognitive impairment with early functional impact (~MCI); Stage 4 dementia with mild functional impairment; Stage 5 moderate; Stage 6 severe (complete dependence in basic ADLs).
- **Boundary conditions**: Stages 1-3 constitute "early Alzheimer's disease"; individuals with Down syndrome may not be fully independent even at Stage 0.
- **Related concepts**: Alzheimer's disease continuum; MCI; ADLs/IADLs.

## Biological staging (AT2 / PET)
- **Notation**: A+T2−, A+T2MTL+, A+T2MOD+, A+T2HIGH+ (Table 3B)
- **Definition**: A biomarker-based staging (A-D) using amyloid PET positivity plus tau PET uptake pattern (medial temporal lobe, moderate neocortical, high neocortical) to grade underlying AD pathology independent of symptoms.
- **Boundary conditions**: Requires amyloid positivity; integrated with clinical stage in Table 3C.
- **Related concepts**: Biomarkers; clinical staging; beta-amyloid; tau.

## Biomarker
- **Notation**: e.g., Aβ42, pTau217, pTau181, NfL
- **Definition**: A measurable biological change indicating the presence/absence or risk of disease. AD biomarkers include abnormal beta-amyloid and tau (blood, CSF, PET), decreased glucose metabolism (FDG-PET), brain atrophy (sMRI), and neurofilament light chain (NfL). The first FDA-cleared blood tests (2025): Lumipulse G pTau217/β-Amyloid 1-42 Plasma Ratio (specialty care) and Elecsys pTau181 (primary care rule-out).
- **Boundary conditions**: Not all biomarkers are disease-specific (e.g., NfL indicates neurodegeneration generally); cut-points may vary by comorbidity and race/ethnicity.
- **Related concepts**: Biological staging; beta-amyloid; tau; ARIA.

## Clinical Alzheimer's dementia (vs biological Alzheimer's disease)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: "Clinical Alzheimer's dementia" is dementia believed due to Alzheimer's based on clinical symptoms only (used throughout the Prevalence section because most population studies predate biomarkers). "Alzheimer's disease" in the biological sense is defined by biomarker evidence of AD brain changes regardless of symptoms.
- **Boundary conditions**: 15%-30% of those diagnosed with clinical Alzheimer's dementia during life lack AD biomarkers/pathology at autopsy; conversely many biomarker-positive people never develop dementia.
- **Related concepts**: Biomarker; mixed dementia; prevalence.

## Mixed dementia (mixed pathology)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The presence of the brain changes of more than one type of dementia in the same individual; when these cause dementia symptoms during life it is termed mixed dementia.
- **Boundary conditions**: Most common at age 85+; one autopsy study found only 3% of presumed Alzheimer's cases had AD pathology alone.
- **Related concepts**: Alzheimer's disease; vascular dementia; Lewy body; LATE.

## Mild cognitive impairment (MCI)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: A precursor stage in which thinking/memory problems are noticeable but do not interfere with everyday activities; corresponds roughly to clinical Stage 3. "MCI due to Alzheimer's disease" adds AD biomarker evidence.
- **Boundary conditions**: ~17-22% of older adults have all-cause MCI; ~32.6% of MCI (age 70+) is biomarker-positive for AD in one study.
- **Related concepts**: Clinical staging; subjective cognitive decline; biomarker.

## Subjective cognitive decline (SCD)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: An individual's perception that their memory/thinking is worsening, in the absence (in some definitions) of objective test impairment; an early potential warning sign.
- **Boundary conditions**: Not all SCD progresses; often reflects treatable conditions (sleep apnea, depression, anxiety); ~16.6% of adults 45+ report SCD.
- **Related concepts**: MCI; biomarker; preclinical AD.

## Prevalence vs incidence
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: Prevalence = number/proportion of people who have a condition at a point in time; incidence = number/rate of new cases per year (often per 100,000).
- **Boundary conditions**: Both rise with age; age-specific incidence may be declining over decades even as total prevalence grows due to aging.
- **Related concepts**: Lifetime risk; projection.

## Lifetime risk
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The probability that a person of a given age without the condition will develop it during their remaining lifespan.
- **Boundary conditions**: Estimated from Framingham with strict DSM-IV/NINCDS-ADRDA criteria; higher for women; slightly higher at age 65 than 45.
- **Related concepts**: Incidence; sex differences.

## APOE-e4
- **Notation**: APOE ε2/ε3/ε4
- **Definition**: The e4 allele of the apolipoprotein E gene — the strongest common genetic risk factor for late-onset Alzheimer's dementia; risk increases with one and further with two copies; e2 is protective, e3 neutral.
- **Boundary conditions**: Effect varies by ancestry and is inconsistent in some Black/Hispanic populations; carriers are at higher risk of ARIA from anti-amyloid drugs; routine testing is not recommended except before anti-amyloid treatment.
- **Related concepts**: Genetics; ARIA; anti-amyloid therapy.

## ARIA (amyloid-related imaging abnormalities)
- **Notation**: ARIA-E, ARIA-H
- **Definition**: A side effect of anti-amyloid antibodies seen on MRI: ARIA-E (edema/effusion) and ARIA-H (microhemorrhages/superficial siderosis). Often asymptomatic but can be serious.
- **Boundary conditions**: Higher risk in APOE-e4 homozygotes; managed via scheduled MRI monitoring and possible treatment discontinuation.
- **Related concepts**: Anti-amyloid therapy; APOE-e4; biomarker.

## Treatment-related amyloid clearance (TRAC)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: Transition from amyloid-positive to amyloid-negative status after anti-amyloid treatment (observed in most lecanemab/donanemab trial participants after ~18 months).
- **Boundary conditions**: Documented over 18-month trials; long-term durability still under study.
- **Related concepts**: Anti-amyloid therapy; biomarker.

## Modifiable risk factors / cognitive reserve
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: Modifiable risk factors are changeable exposures/conditions (e.g., hypertension, diabetes, hearing/vision loss, physical inactivity, smoking, obesity, low education, TBI, air pollution) associated with dementia risk. Cognitive reserve is the brain's ability — built through education, stimulating work/activities — to sustain cognitive function despite brain changes.
- **Boundary conditions**: Many are risk factors for dementia generally, not proven AD-specific; midlife is an important window.
- **Related concepts**: Attributable fraction; U.S. POINTER; prevention.

## Disability-adjusted life years (DALYs)
- **Notation**: DALYs = YLLs + YLDs
- **Definition**: A disease-burden measure combining years of life lost (YLLs) due to premature mortality and years lived with disability (YLDs).
- **Boundary conditions**: AD/other dementias ranked 17th cause of DALYs overall in the U.S. (2021) and the leading cause for age ≥80; contributed 30% more DALYs in women than men.
- **Related concepts**: Morbidity; mortality; burden.

## Direct care workforce
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: Personal care aides, home health aides, and nursing assistants who deliver the majority of day-to-day hands-on care; the largest segment of the dementia care workforce.
- **Boundary conditions**: Predominantly women and people of color; low wages, high turnover (79%-99%), variable/limited dementia-specific training requirements.
- **Related concepts**: Workforce; ADLs/IADLs; geriatrician.

## Dually eligible (Medicare-Medicaid)
- **Notation**: D-SNP
- **Definition**: Individuals eligible for both Medicare and Medicaid; Medicaid uniquely covers long-term nursing-home care (>90 days) that Medicare does not. Dual-eligible Special Needs Plans (D-SNPs) coordinate the two programs.
- **Boundary conditions**: 24% of older individuals with AD/other dementias who have Medicare also have Medicaid (vs 10% without dementia); benefits vary by state.
- **Related concepts**: Medicaid; Medicare; long-term care costs.

## ADLs / IADLs
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: Activities of daily living (ADLs: bathing, dressing, eating, toileting, transferring, walking) and instrumental activities of daily living (IADLs: managing finances/medications, shopping, meal preparation, transportation). Loss of these defines functional impairment across the clinical stages.
- **Boundary conditions**: Impairment in one additional ADL adds substantially more caregiving hours for people with dementia than without.
- **Related concepts**: Clinical staging; caregiving; direct care workforce.
