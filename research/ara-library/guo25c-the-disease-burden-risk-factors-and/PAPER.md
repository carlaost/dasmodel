## Overview

This study analyzes Alzheimer's disease and other dementias (ADOD) in Asia using Global Burden of Disease Study 2021 data from 1990 to 2021. The abstract reports analyses of counts and age-standardized rates for incidence, prevalence, mortality, and DALYs, trend assessment with Joinpoint regression and AAPCs, and 30-year forecasting with an ARIMA model.

The abstract-supported findings are that ADOD deaths, DALYs, incidence, and prevalence increased substantially in Asia from 1990 to 2021; age-standardized rates rose in both sexes and were consistently higher among females; Qatar and the United Arab Emirates had the greatest changes in several burden counts; Afghanistan and China had the highest ASMR in 2021; high fasting blood glucose was the top risk factor; and the ARIMA forecast indicated continued upward burden over the next 30 years.
