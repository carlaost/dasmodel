## C01: Plasma p-tau217 is the highest-performing p-tau biomarker for biologically defined AD
- **Statement**: Plasma p-tau217 is the highest-performing p-tau biomarker for identifying biologically defined Alzheimer's disease, with pooled sensitivity 88·1% (95% CI 86·7–89·5), specificity 88·7% (87·4–89·9), AUROC 91·1% (88·9–92·4), and diagnostic odds ratio 50·7 (40·6–63·4), all at moderate certainty of evidence.
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: A comparably powered meta-analysis of biologically defined AD in which another p-tau assay yields a higher pooled AUROC/DOR than p-tau217, or in which p-tau217's pooled AUROC falls outside the reported CI.
- **Proof**: [E01, E02]
- **Evidence basis**: Abstract reports p-tau217 as "the highest-performing biomarker" with the stated pooled estimates and moderate GRADE certainty.
- **Interpretation**: Positions p-tau217 as the leading candidate for clinical implementation; the abstract's INTERPRETATION echoes this.

## C06: About 90% of included studies were high risk of bias due to threshold derivation
- **Statement**: Approximately 90% of the included studies were rated as high risk of bias for not having used predefined or externally derived thresholds.
- **Status**: supported
- **Falsification criteria**: Re-assessment under QUADAS-2 showing substantially fewer than ~90% of the 113 included studies at high risk of bias on the threshold/index-test domain.
- **Proof**: [E03]
- **Evidence basis**: Abstract, FINDINGS, states this figure directly from the QUADAS-2 assessment.
- **Interpretation**: Reported diagnostic accuracy is likely optimistic because thresholds were commonly derived within the same data used to evaluate them; the abstract's INTERPRETATION acknowledges "high risk of bias of many studies".
- **Sources**:

## C07: Prospective real-world implementation studies of p-tau217 are needed
- **Statement**: Despite p-tau217 being a highly sensitive and specific biomarker for Alzheimer's disease pathology, prospective clinical implementation studies in real-world settings are needed to characterise its effect on AD diagnosis and clinical management.
- **Status**: hypothesis
- **Falsification criteria**: Demonstration that existing retrospective/case-control evidence already establishes p-tau217's real-world diagnostic and management impact, obviating prospective implementation studies.
- **Proof**: [E04]
- **Evidence basis**: Abstract, INTERPRETATION, states this need directly.
- **Interpretation**: A forward-looking recommendation, not an empirical result; motivated by the high risk of bias documented in C06.
