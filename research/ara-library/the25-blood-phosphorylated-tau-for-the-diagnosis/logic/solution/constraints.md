# Constraints, Assumptions, and Limitations

> Grounding: abstract-only. Limitations below are those stated or directly implied by the abstract; the full text likely contains more (Not available from provided input).

## Boundary conditions
- Scope is plasma (blood) p-tau biomarkers only; CSF and other-fluid p-tau are used only as reference standards, not as index tests.
- Target condition is *biologically defined* Alzheimer's disease (confirmed by amyloid-PET, tau-PET, CSF, or neuropathology), not clinically defined dementia.
- Five individual assays are quantified: p-tau217, p-tau181, p-tau205, p-tau212, p-tau231. Any other p-tau site is out of the reported scope.
- Search limited to five databases and to the window July 1, 1984 – Dec 9, 2024.

## Assumptions
- Paired sensitivity/specificity data from heterogeneous primary studies are comparable enough for bivariate pooling.
- The four biological reference standards are acceptable and roughly interchangeable proxies for AD pathology for pooling purposes (per-standard handling: Not available from provided input).
- Excluded overlapping/duplicate datasets were correctly identified, so individuals are counted once (29,625 unique individuals).

## Known limitations
- **High risk of bias**: approximately 90% of included studies were high risk of bias for not using predefined or externally derived thresholds (C06), so pooled accuracy — especially specificity/sensitivity at "optimal" cut-points — is likely optimistic.
- **Certainty of evidence is capped at moderate**: no assay achieved high GRADE certainty; p-tau181 evidence was low certainty.
- **No prospective real-world evidence**: the abstract explicitly notes prospective clinical implementation studies are still needed (C07); accuracy in unselected real-world populations is uncharacterised.
- **Between-assay CIs vary in width**: p-tau205 and p-tau212 have notably wider CIs than p-tau217/p-tau181, indicating fewer contributing studies (inferred from the CI widths reported in the abstract).
- **Abstract-only compilation**: heterogeneity statistics, publication-bias assessment, subgroup analyses, per-reference-standard results, and figures/tables are Not available from provided input (no full text).

## Data and code availability
- No primary data were collected and no individual-level dataset was released (Europe PMC `hasData = "N"`); underlying cohorts appear only as citations within the 113 included studies.
