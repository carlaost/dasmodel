## Overview

This is a PRISMA-DTA systematic review and meta-analysis of plasma phosphorylated-tau (p-tau) blood biomarkers for the diagnosis of biologically defined Alzheimer's disease (AD). Across databases searched from 1984 to December 2024, 6,429 records were identified, 312 assessed for eligibility, and 113 studies (29,625 unique individuals) included. Using bivariate random-effects meta-analysis, the authors pooled sensitivity, specificity, diagnostic odds ratio (DOR), and AUROC for five individual p-tau assays (p-tau217, p-tau181, p-tau205, p-tau212, p-tau231) against amyloid-PET, tau-PET, CSF, and neuropathological reference standards. Plasma p-tau217 was the highest-performing biomarker (AUROC 91.1%, DOR 50.7). Certainty of evidence was graded with GRADE and risk of bias with QUADAS-2; approximately 90% of studies were high risk of bias, chiefly for not using predefined or externally derived diagnostic thresholds. The authors conclude p-tau217 is highly accurate for AD pathology but that prospective real-world implementation studies are needed.
