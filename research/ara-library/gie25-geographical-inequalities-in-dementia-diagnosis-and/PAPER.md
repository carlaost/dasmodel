Methods Five databases were searched in June 2024, including studies conducted in any country, published from 2010 onwards, and in English or German. ## Overview

This systematic review examines evidence on geographical inequalities in dementia diagnosis and care access. The abstract reports that five databases were searched in June 2024 for English- or German-language studies from 2010 onward, with dual-reviewer screening, third-reviewer discrepancy resolution, dual-researcher data extraction, and narrative synthesis. From 1321 screened studies and 49 full texts, 32 studies were included.

The abstract-supported findings are that most included studies were conducted in the US, followed by the UK; geographical inequalities are most often evidenced through regional variation in service availability and suitability; rural residents with dementia often experience challenges in timely diagnosis and access to health and social care; no research addressed geographical inequalities in residential care access; and innovative rural diagnosis-rate models were reported from Canada and Australia.
