# Problem Specification

> Grounding: abstract-only. Observations use only `metadata.md` / `metadata.json`; unsupported details are marked unavailable.

## Observations

### O01: Place of residence can disadvantage dementia service access
- **Statement**: People with dementia can be disadvantaged in access to health and social care services for diagnosis and care depending on where they live, including rural, suburban, urban, postcode, and country-level geography.
- **Evidence**: metadata.md abstract, Background.
- **Implication**: Geography is treated as a determinant of dementia diagnosis and care access, not only as a descriptive location variable.

### O02: The review addresses a missing comprehensive synthesis
- **Statement**: The review was motivated by the absence of an existing comprehensive synthesis of evidence on geographical inequalities in dementia diagnosis and care access.
- **Evidence**: metadata.md abstract, Background.
- **Implication**: The contribution is primarily evidence synthesis rather than a new intervention trial or model.

### O03: The systematic review included 32 studies from a screened corpus
- **Statement**: The review screened 1321 studies, read 49 full texts, and included 32 studies in the final review.
- **Evidence**: metadata.md abstract, Results.
- **Implication**: The review's conclusions are based on a moderate-sized included literature after substantial screening.

### O04: Existing evidence is concentrated geographically
- **Statement**: Most included studies were conducted in the US, followed by the UK.
- **Evidence**: metadata.md abstract, Results.
- **Implication**: Generalizability to other countries is uncertain from the abstract alone.

### O05: Rural service access and timely diagnosis are recurring challenges
- **Statement**: People with dementia residing in rural areas often experience challenges in receiving a timely diagnosis and accessing health and social care.
- **Evidence**: metadata.md abstract, Results.
- **Implication**: Rurality is a central axis of geographical inequality for both diagnosis and care access.

## Key Insight
- **Insight**: Dementia diagnosis and care inequalities are geographically patterned, with rurality and regional service availability/suitability acting as key access barriers.
- **Derived from**: O01, O03, O05
- **Enables**: A policy and research agenda focused on rural service access, residential care evidence gaps, rarer dementia forms, and place-sensitive national strategies.
