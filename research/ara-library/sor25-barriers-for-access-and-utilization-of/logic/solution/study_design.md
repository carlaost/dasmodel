# Systematic Review Methodology (Study Design)

This is the "method" of the work: how the PRISMA-guided systematic review was designed and conducted. (Mirrors §Methods and §Results "Study selection and quality assessment.")

## Standards followed
- Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses (PRISMA) guidelines (ref 33).
- PICO framework used to define inclusion criteria (Population, Intervention/Outcome, Comparison, plus Geographical Area and Timeframe added as review-specific PICO extensions — Table 2).

## Search strategy
- **Primary database**: PubMed/MEDLINE.
- **Secondary databases**: Embase, PsycINFO (EBSCOhost), Health Technology Assessment Database, Web of Science (Clarivate).
- **Timeframe**: 10-year window, 2013–2023.
- **PICO-based search string** (Table 2):
  - Population (P): "Alzheimer Disease" OR "Dementia"
  - Intervention (I)/Outcome (O): "Barrier*" OR "Access" OR "Healthcare" OR "Health Care Utilization"
  - Comparison (C): Not applicable
  - Geographical Area (S): "Europ*" [MeSH]
  - Timeframe (T): "2013/01/01"[PDAT]:"2023/12/31"[PDAT]
- Additional relevant papers manually added from reference lists of collected studies and reviews (reference mining; yielded 4 additional studies from refs 36–38 — see `logic/related_work.md` RW04).

## Eligibility criteria (Table 1)
- **Inclusion — Population**: European settings AND (people living with dementia/Alzheimer's disease with a formal diagnosis) AND/OR (formal and informal caregivers) AND/OR (healthcare professionals and other stakeholders involved in dementia care).
- **Inclusion — Intervention**: Assessment or search of barriers to dementia care.
- **Inclusion — Other criteria**: Written in English AND original research AND published in or after 2013.
- **Exclusion — Population**: Studies focused on neurological diseases other than dementia/Alzheimer's disease.
- **Exclusion — Intervention**: Other study types not meeting specified criteria (e.g., randomized controlled trials, case-control studies, editorials, letters, conference papers).
- **Exclusion — Other criteria**: Full text not available.
- Grey literature was not considered, nor were conference papers, dissertations, letters, or editorials.

## Screening process
1. Run the search string on PubMed/MEDLINE; adapt and run on the four secondary databases.
2. Remove duplicates using Rayyan AI, yielding a database of unique studies plus reference-mined additions.
3. Five reviewers independently screen titles/abstracts against eligibility criteria using Rayyan (which supports independent tagging, inclusion/exclusion criteria application, and blind screening).
4. Where abstracts provide insufficient information, undertake full-text review.
5. The same five reviewers independently evaluate full texts to determine final inclusion; conflicts resolved by the senior reviewer (RP).
6. Pivotal information from included studies extracted and reported in an Excel sheet.

## Quality assessment
- Tool: Mixed Methods Appraisal Tool (MMAT), revised version (ref 35).
- MMAT evaluates study quality based on the specific design of each study (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed-methods), scoring 0–100%.
- Studies scoring below the 50% threshold are not automatically excluded but are examined more carefully for how quality might influence overall findings, ensuring the review captures a full range of evidence while remaining transparent about lower-quality studies' interpretation.
- In mixed-methods studies, the overall quality score is determined by the lowest-scoring component (cannot surpass the weakest component).
- Reviewers evaluated each paper independently for an objective quality assessment.

## Data extraction and synthesis approach
- Per-study fields extracted (Table 3): First author/year/citation number; nation(s) of study; study design; methods (interview, focus group, survey, or combinations); population (PLWD, caregivers, HCWs, or combinations); sample size/composition; setting(s) of care; identified barriers (categorical tags); MMAT quality score.
- Synthesis approach: **narrative synthesis** (not meta-analysis), given the heterogeneity of qualitative, quantitative, and mixed-methods designs and the non-continuous, categorical nature of "barrier" findings.
- Barriers narratively categorised into five/six thematic domains (organisational; informational/educational; cultural/stigma-related; logistical; financial), each mapped onto the countries/regions of the underlying studies (Northern, Western, Southern, Eastern Europe) and, where reported, onto specific minority/migrant populations.

## No registered protocol
- No PROSPERO registration number or other protocol pre-registration identifier appears anywhere in the paper's full text — not specified in paper.
