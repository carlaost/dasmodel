# Concepts

## PICO framework
- **Notation**: P (Population), I (Intervention), O (Outcome), C (Comparison), S/T added as Geographical Area / Timeframe in this review's adaptation
- **Definition**: A structured framework used to define eligibility criteria for the systematic review's search string. Here: Population = "Alzheimer Disease" OR "Dementia"; Intervention/Outcome = "Barrier*" OR "Access" OR "Healthcare" OR "Health Care Utilization"; Comparison = Not applicable; Geographical Area = "Europ*" [MeSH]; Timeframe = 2013/01/01–2023/12/31.
- **Boundary conditions**: No comparison arm was specified (Comparison = "Not applicable"), since this is a barriers-identification review, not an intervention-effectiveness study.
- **Related concepts**: PRISMA, Eligibility criteria

## PRISMA (Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analyses)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: A reporting guideline for systematic reviews and meta-analyses of healthcare interventions, used here to structure and report the identification → screening → eligibility → inclusion pipeline (Fig. 1).
- **Boundary conditions**: Applied to structure the flow of records from initial database identification (1748 + 4) through final inclusion (29); it governs reporting transparency, not the review's substantive eligibility criteria (which are set by the PICO framework and Table 1).
- **Related concepts**: PICO framework, Eligibility criteria

## Eligibility criteria (inclusion/exclusion)
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The review's explicit inclusion criteria (Table 1): European settings; people living with dementia/Alzheimer's disease with a formal diagnosis, and/or formal/informal caregivers, and/or healthcare professionals/stakeholders; assessment of barriers to dementia care; written in English; original research; published in or after 2013. Exclusion criteria: non-dementia neurological diseases; non-original-research study types (RCTs, case-control, editorials, letters, conference papers); full text not available.
- **Boundary conditions**: Grey literature, conference papers, dissertations, letters, and editorials were not considered under any circumstance.
- **Related concepts**: PICO framework, MMAT

## Rayyan AI
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: An AI-assisted systematic-review screening tool (ref 34) used by the five reviewers (MS, CF, MM, FE, IS) to independently tag articles, apply inclusion/exclusion criteria, remove duplicates, and perform blind screening of titles and abstracts.
- **Boundary conditions**: Used for duplicate removal and title/abstract screening; full-text review to determine final eligibility was performed independently by the same five reviewers, with conflicts resolved by the senior reviewer (RP) — Rayyan did not adjudicate full-text inclusion decisions.
- **Related concepts**: PRISMA, MMAT

## Mixed Methods Appraisal Tool (MMAT)
- **Notation**: Score range 0–100%
- **Definition**: A quality-appraisal tool (revised 2018 version, ref 35) used to evaluate the methodological rigor of included studies according to their specific design (qualitative, quantitative, or mixed-methods), covering well-defined study design, transparent data collection, and thorough reporting.
- **Boundary conditions**: Studies scoring below the 50% threshold are not automatically excluded but are examined more carefully for how their quality might affect overall findings; in mixed-methods studies, the overall score cannot exceed the score of the weakest individual component.
- **Related concepts**: Eligibility criteria, Risk of bias

## Barrier-category taxonomy
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The review's narrative-synthesis categorisation of identified barriers into five/six named thematic domains: (1) organisational barriers — coordination, communication, and stability issues within organisations hindering service delivery; (2) information and educational barriers — lack of adequate information, knowledge, and skills in managing dementia care; (3) cultural and stigma-related barriers — social prejudices, misconceptions, and lack of cultural understanding reducing accessibility/efficacy of care; (4) logistical barriers — practical challenges such as waiting times, continuity, and difficulties in service use; (5) financial barriers — economic constraints and complex administrative processes limiting access to services.
- **Boundary conditions**: A single study can be tagged with multiple barrier categories simultaneously (per Table 3, most studies list 1–5 barrier types); categories are not mutually exclusive.
- **Related concepts**: Narrative synthesis, MMAT

## Healthcare workers (HCWs)
- **Notation**: HCWs
- **Definition**: Healthcare professionals and other stakeholders involved in dementia care (e.g., primary care physicians, nurses, care-home staff), distinguished in the review's population taxonomy from people living with dementia (PLWD) and caregivers.
- **Boundary conditions**: HCWs were the sole population focus in 9 of 29 studies and appear in combination with other groups in 9 additional studies (6 with caregivers, 3 with PLWD+caregivers).
- **Related concepts**: Formal/informal caregivers, Barrier-category taxonomy

## Formal vs. informal caregivers
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: Formal caregivers are paid/professional care providers operating within organised care systems; informal caregivers are typically family members or others providing unpaid care, who bear a substantial share of the overall dementia-care burden alongside formal support systems.
- **Boundary conditions**: The review's inclusion criteria admit both formal and informal caregivers as an eligible population (Table 1); most included studies do not further stratify "caregiver" samples by formal/informal status in the reported sample breakdowns (Table 3).
- **Related concepts**: Healthcare workers, People living with dementia

## PWLD / people living with dementia (PLWD)
- **Notation**: PWLD (as abbreviated in Table 3) / PLWD (as abbreviated in body text)
- **Definition**: Individuals formally diagnosed with dementia or Alzheimer's disease, a required population category under the review's eligibility criteria (with or without co-enrolled caregivers/HCWs).
- **Boundary conditions**: PLWD were the sole population focus in only 1 of 29 studies; they most often appear jointly with caregivers (5 studies) or with caregivers and HCWs (3 studies).
- **Related concepts**: Formal/informal caregivers, Healthcare workers
