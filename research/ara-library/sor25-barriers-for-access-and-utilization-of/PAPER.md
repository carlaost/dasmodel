Support for people with dementia can be provided by formal support systems although most of the care process relies upon informal care givers. Despite the availability of formal support systems and healthcare workers, the utilization of dementia care services remains suboptimal. Factors such as non-compliance, lack of awareness, and poor care coordination contribute to this issue. Understanding these barriers is crucial for improving service utilization and alleviating the economic burden on families and national health systems. This systematic review analysed the literature, published from 2013 to 2023, on barriers in Alzheimer and other dementia healthcare system, conducted on people living with a dementia, their caregivers, or healthcare workers in dementia care settings in Europe, following PRISMA guidelines. Searches in PubMed, Embase, PsycINFO, Health Technology Assessment Database, and Web of Science used terms related to Alzheimer's, dementia, and access barriers. # Barriers for Access and Utilization of Dementia Care Services in Europe: A Systematic Review

## Overview

This is a **PRISMA-guided systematic review** (not an empirical/computational study) that searched five databases (PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, PsycINFO, Health Technology Assessment Database, Web of Science) for literature published 2013–2023 on barriers to accessing and utilising dementia/Alzheimer's care services in Europe, among people living with dementia (PLWD), their formal/informal caregivers, and healthcare workers (HCWs). From 1748 database-identified plus 4 reference-mined records (1202 unique after deduplication), 73 full-text articles were reviewed and **29 studies** were ultimately included. Quality was assessed with the Mixed Methods Appraisal Tool (MMAT); screening was AI-assisted via Rayyan. The review narratively synthesises identified barriers into six thematic domains — organisational, informational/educational, cultural/stigma-related, financial, and logistical — and discusses minority/migrant-specific barriers and European policy context. It has no released code, dataset, or registered clinical trial (per `sources.yaml`: `code: []`, `data: []`, `clinical_trials: []`; "No datasets were generated or analysed during the current study").
