# Study Design (Review Structure)

This is a narrative review (not a systematic review or meta-analysis); no PRISMA-style search
protocol, inclusion/exclusion criteria, or quantitative pooling method is reported. The review's
organizing structure is as follows (paper.pdf, section headings):

## 1. Overview of spatial transcriptomics technologies (paper.pdf p.2–3)
Introduces img-ST and seq-ST as the two broad ST families and surveys representative platforms
(MERFISH, seqFISH, ISS/padlock/STARmap for img-ST; Visium/Slide-seq/Visium HD/Stereo-seq/DBiT-seq/
FISSEQ/GeoMx for seq-ST), framed around Fig. 1 (encoding/decoding schemes) and Fig. 2 (comparative
resolution).

## 2. Microglia regional and functional heterogeneity (paper.pdf p.5)
Establishes baseline non-spatial microglial heterogeneity findings (density gradients across brain
regions; DAM/ARM/MGnD activation-state nomenclature) as the biological substrate that spatial
methods are then applied to.

## 3. Microglia in the amyloid plaque niche (paper.pdf p.6–8)
The review's technical core: synthesizes ST findings on the spatial organization of the amyloid
plaque niche, organized around Fig. 3A — PIGs, distance-graded cell-type enrichment (microglia →
astrocytes/oligodendrocytes/OPCs → neurons), microglia-astrocyte receptor-ligand crosstalk, and
IFN-I signaling as a mechanistic node linking microglia and neurons. A dedicated subsection
("Microglia and vulnerable neurons," p.8) covers spatial p-tau/neuron co-profiling.

## 4. Microglia in aging brains (paper.pdf p.8–9)
Parallel synthesis for the aging (non-AD) brain, organized around Fig. 3B — white-matter/
demyelination-associated microglia, foamy microglia, activation scores, the Spatial Ageing Clock,
and proximity effects (microglia pro-aging; NSCs pro-rejuvenating; T cells pro-aging via IFNγ).

## 5. AD in human samples (paper.pdf p.9–10)
A shorter section acknowledging that most spatially resolved cellular-interaction findings above
derive from mouse models, and surveying the smaller set of human-tissue ST studies (PIGs in human
brain; dorsolateral prefrontal cortex and middle temporal gyrus mapping; genetic vs. sporadic AD
comparisons; immunized-patient microglial mechanisms).

## 6. Conclusions / future directions (paper.pdf p.10)
Identifies remaining challenges (resolution, multi-omic integration, mechanistic elucidation of
cell-cell interactions in situ) and proposed next steps: 3D deep-tissue ST imaging, Raman-scattering
metabolic imaging, spatial epigenomics, and spatial proteomics as complementary/corroborating
modalities — including the explicit caveat about mRNA-protein dissociation that motivates pairing ST
with proteomic validation.
