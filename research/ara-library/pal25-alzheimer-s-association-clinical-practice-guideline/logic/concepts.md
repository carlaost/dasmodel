# Concepts

## Blood-based biomarker (BBM) test
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: For this guideline, a BBM test is the combination of a blood-based analyte and the technology used to measure it (e.g., a specific immunoassay or mass spectrometry method). Assays measuring the same analyte (e.g., p-tau217) but using different technology or antibodies can achieve different performance and must be evaluated as separate tests.
- **Boundary conditions**: This iteration considers plasma p-tau and Aβ analytes only (p-tau217, %p-tau217, p-tau181, p-tau231, Aβ42/40); excludes GFAP and NfL; excludes cross-protein ratios (e.g., p-tau217/Aβ42) unless the ratio uses a same-protein reference (e.g., Aβ42/40). Individual biomarkers only, not multi-biomarker combinations.
- **Related concepts**: Reference standard, Sensitivity/Specificity, Youden's index

## Triaging test
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: A test in which a negative result rules out AD pathology with high probability, whereas a positive result should be confirmed using another method, such as CSF AD biomarkers or amyloid PET.
- **Boundary conditions**: Acceptable triaging accuracy defined by the panel as ≥90% sensitivity and ≥75% specificity against a reference standard.
- **Related concepts**: Confirmatory test, Sensitivity/Specificity, Pretest probability

## Confirmatory test
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: A test in which a negative result rules out AD pathology and a positive result confirms AD pathology with high probability, used without additional biomarker assessment.
- **Boundary conditions**: Acceptable confirmatory accuracy defined by the panel as ≥90% sensitivity and ≥90% specificity against a reference standard; serves as a substitute for CSF analysis or amyloid PET.
- **Related concepts**: Triaging test, Reference standard

## Reference standard
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The established test(s) against which BBM diagnostic accuracy is assessed for the presence/absence of AD pathology: CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid positron emission tomography (PET), or neuropathological assessment of AD.
- **Boundary conditions**: Applies to determining AD pathology (amyloid) status; not a clinical-diagnosis reference.
- **Related concepts**: BBM test, Sensitivity/Specificity

## Sensitivity (Sn) and Specificity (Sp)
- **Notation**: Sn, Sp
- **Definition**: Sensitivity is the proportion of true AD-pathology-positive individuals correctly identified as positive (TP/(TP+FN)); specificity is the proportion of true negatives correctly identified as negative (TN/(TN+FP)). Raw TP, TN, FP, FN counts were sought at the Youden index cutoff to compute pooled Sn and Sp.
- **Boundary conditions**: Pooled via meta-analysis per BBM test; Sn weighted more heavily for triaging certainty, Sp more heavily for confirmatory certainty.
- **Related concepts**: Youden's index, PPV/NPV, Triaging/Confirmatory test

## Youden's index
- **Notation**: J = Sn + Sp − 1
- **Definition**: A single-cutoff criterion that maximizes the sum of sensitivity and specificity; used as the primary cutoff for the main meta-analysis of diagnostic test accuracy.
- **Boundary conditions**: The panel notes Youden's index cutoff is not always an optimal Sn/Sp combination, prompting supplementary fixed-Sn/fixed-Sp analyses.
- **Related concepts**: Sensitivity/Specificity, Two-cutoff approach, ROC curve

## Positive/Negative predictive value (PPV/NPV)
- **Notation**: PPV, NPV
- **Definition**: PPV is the probability that a positive test reflects true AD pathology; NPV is the probability that a negative test reflects true absence. Unlike Sn/Sp, both depend on the pretest probability (prevalence) of AD pathology in the tested population.
- **Boundary conditions**: The panel did not report PPV/NPV for individual tests because they are highly dependent on pretest probability; illustrative values are given at 20%, 50%, and 80% pretest probability.
- **Related concepts**: Pretest probability, Sensitivity/Specificity

## Pretest probability
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: The probability that a given patient has AD pathology before the BBM test is performed; varies by clinical presentation, age, and known risk factors, and is more accurately estimated in specialist than non-specialist settings.
- **Boundary conditions**: Central to interpreting PPV/NPV and to the good practice statement; differs between primary and specialized care, motivating separate CPG processes.
- **Related concepts**: PPV/NPV, Triaging/Confirmatory test

## GRADE approach and Evidence-to-Decision (EtD) framework
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: GRADE (Grading of Recommendations, Assessment, Development, and Evaluation) is a system for rating certainty of evidence (assessing risk of bias, indirectness, inconsistency, imprecision, publication bias) and for moving from evidence to recommendations. The EtD framework translates evidence summaries into recommendations by weighing certainty, benefits/harms balance, values/preferences, resources/cost, acceptability, feasibility, and equity.
- **Boundary conditions**: Applied here to diagnostic test accuracy; recommendations labeled "strong" ("the panel recommends") or "conditional" ("the panel suggests").
- **Related concepts**: Certainty of evidence, Strong vs conditional recommendation

## Two-cutoff approach
- **Notation**: —
- **Definition**: A testing paradigm using two cut-points: values above the upper cutoff rule in AD pathology with high certainty, values below the lower cutoff rule it out, and intermediate values ("indeterminate zone") require further testing with PET or CSF.
- **Boundary conditions**: Not assessed in this iteration due to limited peer-reviewed evidence; a poorly performing assay could create a very large indeterminate zone. Deferred to future guideline updates.
- **Related concepts**: Youden's index (single cutoff), Sensitivity/Specificity
