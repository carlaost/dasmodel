# Recommendations, Thresholds, and EtD Judgments

The core deliverable of the CPG: two conditional recommendations, one good practice statement, the accuracy thresholds, and the EtD factor judgments. (See Table 2 for verbatim wording; §3.6–3.14 for EtD judgments.)

## Recommendation 1 — Triaging test (conditional, low certainty)
> In patients with objective cognitive impairment presenting for specialized memory-care, the panel **suggests using a high-sensitivity BBM test as a triaging test** in the diagnostic workup of AD.

- **Acceptable accuracy**: at least **90% sensitivity and 75% specificity** against a reference standard (CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid PET, or AD neuropathology).
- **Clinical logic**: A negative result rules out AD pathology with high probability; a positive result should be confirmed by CSF or amyloid PET.
- **Strength/certainty**: Conditional recommendation ("the panel suggests"); Low certainty evidence (certainty based on tests meeting acceptable accuracy).

## Recommendation 2 — Confirmatory test (conditional, low certainty)
> In patients with objective cognitive impairment presenting for specialized memory-care, the panel **suggests using a high-sensitivity and high-specificity BBM test as a confirmatory test** in the diagnostic workup of AD.

- **Acceptable accuracy**: at least **90% sensitivity and 90% specificity** against a reference standard (CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid PET, or AD neuropathology).
- **Clinical logic**: A negative result rules out AD pathology; a positive result confirms it with high probability, substituting for CSF/amyloid PET.
- **Strength/certainty**: Conditional recommendation; Low certainty evidence.

## Good practice statement
> A BBM test should **not** be obtained before a comprehensive clinical evaluation by a healthcare professional, and test results should always be interpreted within the clinical context. Clinicians should consider the pretest probability of AD pathology for each patient when deciding whether to use a BBM test.

## Illustrative predictive values (why pretest probability matters)
Predictive values were not reported per test (they depend on pretest probability). Illustratively:

| Triage (Sn 90%, Sp 75%) — PPV | 47% | 78% | 94% |
| Triage (Sn 90%, Sp 75%) — NPV | 97% | 88% | 65% |
| Confirmatory (Sn 90%, Sp 90%) — PPV | 69% | 90% | 97% |

## Clinical scenarios where a BBM test may not be appropriate (or needs extra caution)
- Low utility per shared decision-making (patient would not act on the result).
- Obvious modifiable/temporary conditions likely accounting for impairment.
- Limited life expectancy (unclear significance of AD pathology).
- History of brain-affecting conditions altering BBM levels in poorly studied ways (e.g., neurocysticercosis, HIV, prior chemotherapy/radiation, chronic traumatic encephalopathy).
- Medical conditions affecting BBM levels (e.g., acute brain injury, severe chronic kidney disease, ALS).
- Medications affecting BBM levels (e.g., neprilysin inhibitors, drugs disrupting the blood–brain barrier).

## EtD factor judgments (§3.6–3.14)
- **Desirable effects**: large — minimally invasive vs PET/CSF; less discomfort/anxiety; streamlined, earlier diagnosis; fewer repeated consultations/referrals.
- **Undesirable effects**: small when used appropriately by trained personnel; larger otherwise — over-testing/system burden, false positives (harmful if therapy started erroneously), false negatives (delays), platform variability, over-reliance leading to underdiagnosis of non-AD conditions, DTC-test confusion.
- **Balance of effects**: probably favors BBM use over CSF/PET/no-testing when a threshold-meeting test is used by a trained specialist.
- **Values and preferences**: possibly important uncertainty/variability (personal, emotional decision); underscores shared decision-making.
- **Resources required**: moderate savings; BBMs often 70%–90% lower cost than PET (varies by country/setting; US savings offset by inconsistent reimbursement).
- **Cost-effectiveness**: no judgment possible (insufficient, regionally variable data).
- **Equity**: probably increased (more affordable, less invasive, easier to implement — especially in low/middle-income countries).
- **Acceptability**: probably acceptable to collaborative parties.
- **Feasibility**: varies with sample-handling protocols, commercial availability, and reimbursement; more approved tests could improve feasibility via competition.

## Emerging paradigms flagged for future updates
- **Two-cutoff testing** (rule-in/rule-out with an indeterminate zone requiring PET/CSF) — deferred pending more peer-reviewed evidence and consideration of indeterminate-zone size.
- **Biomarker combinations/ratios** (e.g., p-tau217/Aβ42) — deferred to subsequent phases.
- Distilled, easy-to-use clinical tools are under development by the Alzheimer's Association.
