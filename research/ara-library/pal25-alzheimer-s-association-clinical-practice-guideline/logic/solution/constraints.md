# Constraints, Assumptions, and Limitations

## Scope boundaries (what the guideline does and does not cover)
- **Population**: Adults with **objective** cognitive impairment (MCI or dementia) presenting to specialized memory-care where AD is the suspected etiology. Objective impairment requires evidence of deficits on cognitive assessment and/or medical examination.
- **Excluded populations/uses**: Cognitively unimpaired individuals; subjective cognitive decline without objective deficits; screening; non-specialty/primary-care settings; nursing homes and long-term care. Primary-care use is deferred to a future, separate CPG process.
- **Analytes covered**: plasma p-tau217, %p-tau217 (p-tau217/non-p-tau217 ×100), p-tau181, p-tau231, and Aβ42/40 only. GFAP and NfL were deliberately excluded.
- **Ratios/combinations**: Only individual biomarkers (including same-protein ratios like Aβ42/40). Cross-protein ratios (e.g., p-tau217/Aβ42) and multi-biomarker combinations were excluded from this first set of meta-analyses; combinations are deferred to later phases.
- **Cutoffs**: Only single-cutoff (Youden-index) performance was evaluated. Two-cutoff approaches were not assessed due to limited peer-reviewed evidence.
- **Test-level recommendations**: The guideline is **brand-agnostic** and deliberately makes no recommendation for or against any specific named test.

## Assumptions
- A1: Real-world clinician-acceptable accuracy can be anchored to thresholds (triage Sn ≥90%/Sp ≥75%; confirmatory Sn ≥90%/Sp ≥90%), with CEOi expert opinion (ref 15) as the starting point.
- A2: Sensitivity is weighted more heavily for certainty in triaging; specificity more heavily for confirmatory testing.
- A3 (a priori decision): Data combining cognitively impaired and unimpaired populations are not used, to minimize indirectness and avoid inflated performance from bimodal amyloid distributions.
- A4: A BBM test = analyte × measurement technology; same-analyte assays with different technology/antibodies are separate tests.
- A5: The reference standard for AD pathology is CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid PET, or neuropathology.

## Known limitations
- **Low/very-low certainty**: Certainty for tests meeting thresholds is low to very low; a future study could move pooled estimates enough to change a test's threshold status. Most tests rated down for risk of bias (often no prespecified thresholds; unclear blinding between index and reference tests).
- **Single-batch analyses**: Most informing studies used single-batch plasma analyses that do not reflect rolling real-world clinical processing; assay imprecision/bias (coefficient of variation) can affect fixed-cutoff interpretation, especially for small-fold-change markers like Aβ42/40 — recommendations for such markers warrant extra caution.
- **84 studies excluded**: Otherwise-eligible studies were excluded for pooling impaired and unimpaired populations; some studies lacked sufficient TP/TN/FP/FN data — gaps that limit the evidence base.
- **Cost-effectiveness undetermined**: The panel could not judge cost-effectiveness due to insufficient, regionally variable data.
- **Regulatory data not captured**: Some regulatory-approved tests may be absent from the review because their supporting data were not peer-reviewed or publicly available.
- **Evidence recency**: Literature search ended November 3, 2024; studies published since were not included but will be considered in future updates.
- **Prevalence variability**: Brain-amyloid prevalence in cognitively impaired populations varies across studied populations, and many clinical populations have limited gold-standard testing.
- **Not a substitute for clinical judgment**: Recommendations do not restrict access, do not replace comprehensive clinical evaluation, and do not encompass all diagnostic/therapeutic considerations.
