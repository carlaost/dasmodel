# Guideline Development Methodology (Study Design)

This is the "method" of the work: how a GRADE-based Clinical Practice Guideline was developed. (Mirrors §2 Methods.)

## Standards followed
- Developed per the **AGREE II Reporting Checklist** (ref 20) and the **Guidelines International Network–McMaster Guideline Development Checklist** (ref 21).
- Process (funding, panel formation, COI management, internal/external review, organizational approval) governed by Alzheimer's Association policies.

## Panel composition
- 11 panel members: clinical neurologists (S.Pal., M.S.-C., S.S., D.G.), geriatricians (H.W., H.O.), nurse practitioner (L.A.), physician assistant (M.P.), and subject-matter experts, some with clinical expertise (C.T., H.Z., T.K.).
- Geographic distribution: six North America, four Europe, two dual-region affiliations.
- Three guideline methodologists (L.A.K., S.Pah., M.P.T.) oversaw methodology; a medical librarian (M.B.M.) co-developed the search strategy; statisticians performed data analysis.
- One co-chair was replaced when an employment change introduced an unmitigable potential conflict of interest (Oskar Hansson was the previous chair; acknowledged).

## Clinical questions (scope)
Two questions were prioritized for this first iteration:
1. Should a BBM test be incorporated as a **triaging** test to determine presence/absence of AD pathology in cognitively impaired patients (MCI or dementia) in specialized care?
2. Should a BBM test serve as a **substitute for CSF analysis or amyloid PET** (a **confirmatory** test) in the same population?

Input was gathered at the 2024 Alzheimer's Association International Conference (AAIC). The guideline is explicitly not a comprehensive clinical pathway, and does not address screening, non-specialty settings, or cognitively unimpaired/subjective-only individuals.

## Why specialized vs primary care were separated (four reasons)
1. Different prevalence of AD pathology → different NPV/PPV for the same test.
2. Pretest probability is more accurately determined in specialist than non-specialist settings, and it must inform acceptable Sn/Sp thresholds.
3. Limited non-specialist experience interpreting/disclosing AD biomarker results; more comorbidity that may affect BBM results.
4. Practical barriers (time constraints, limited workflow integration) in non-specialized settings.

## Evidence review (linked systematic review, ref 19)
- Databases searched: **PubMed, Medline, Embase, Cochrane Library**, from **2019 through November 3, 2024**.
- Methodologists did study selection, data extraction, risk-of-bias, and certainty assessments; statisticians analyzed data.
- Raw data sought: TP, TN, FP, FN, Sn, Sp at the **Youden-index cutoff**, from (1) published studies, (2) author-provided data when missing, and (3) Sn/Sp derived from ROC curves at Youden's index using **WebPlotDigitizer** (v4.6, ref 24) when neither published nor author-provided.
- Meta-analyses per BBM test computed pooled Sn and Sp (main analysis: single Youden cutoff), with four sensitivity analyses (see experiments E02).

## Defining a "BBM test" and reference standards
- A BBM test = analyte × measurement technology. The panel prioritized amyloid-indicating p-tau and Aβ analytes, excluded GFAP/NfL, analyzed single analytes (or same-protein ratios like Aβ42/40), and selected p-tau species with the strongest evidence base (informed by head-to-head and Round Robin data, ref 23).
- Reference standards: CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid PET, or neuropathology.
- **Brand-agnostic, blinded, performance-based** approach: the panel was blinded to test names until recommendations were drafted, to protect against bias and obsolescence.

## Certainty assessment (GRADE)
- Domains: risk of bias, indirectness, inconsistency, imprecision, publication bias (reported in Evidence Profiles).
- Certainty for Sn assessed separately from Sp; Sn weighted more for triaging, Sp more for confirmatory.

## From evidence to recommendations (EtD)
- The GRADE EtD framework (ref 27) translated evidence into recommendations, weighing certainty, benefit/harm balance, values/preferences, resources/cost, acceptability, feasibility, and equity (completed form: Table S1).
- "The panel recommends" = strong; "the panel suggests" = conditional (Table 1).
- Thresholds anchored to clinician-acceptable accuracy using CEOi expert opinion (ref 15) as a starting point.
- Health economists summarized cost/cost-effectiveness evidence; publicly available price sources compared test prices.
- ESAG (patients with early-stage AD), collaborative parties, and a public comment period (May 12–23) informed final recommendations.

## Update process and external review
- Draft recommendations were posted for public comment; in-scope, evidence-supported comments were considered.
- The guideline and its systematic review are maintained as living documents via the **MAGICapp** platform (https://app.magicapp.org/#/guideline/nyO1Yj).
- De-identified comments to be posted on the Alzheimer's Association website.
