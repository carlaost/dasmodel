## Recommendations

| Clinical question | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Should a BBM test be incorporated as a **triaging test** (a negative result rules out AD pathology with high probability; a positive result should be confirmed by CSF AD biomarkers or amyloid PET) in the diagnostic workup of individuals with cognitive impairment (MCI or dementia) presenting for specialized memory care? | In patients with objective cognitive impairment presenting for specialized memory-care, the panel **suggests using a high-sensitivity BBM test as a triaging test** in the diagnostic workup of AD. **(Conditional recommendation, Low certainty evidence.)** Acceptable diagnostic accuracy for triaging was defined as **≥90% sensitivity and ≥75% specificity** against a reference test (CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid PET, or AD neuropathology). |
| Should a BBM test serve as a **substitute for CSF analysis or amyloid PET** as a **confirmatory test** (a negative test rules out and a positive test confirms AD pathology with high probability) in the diagnostic workup of patients with cognitive impairment (MCI or dementia) presenting for specialized memory care? | In patients with objective cognitive impairment presenting for specialized memory-care, the panel **suggests using a high-sensitivity and high-specificity BBM test as a confirmatory test** in the diagnostic workup of AD. **(Conditional recommendation, Low certainty evidence.)** Acceptable diagnostic accuracy for confirmatory testing was defined as **≥90% sensitivity and ≥90% specificity** against a reference test (CSF AD biomarkers, amyloid PET, or AD neuropathology). |

Certainty of evidence in both cases is based on tests meeting the acceptable diagnostic-accuracy thresholds. A systematic review of relevant studies is maintained on the MAGICapp platform (https://app.magicapp.org/#/guideline/nyO1Yj) and will be updated with future systematic reviews.

## Good practice statement

A BBM test should not be obtained before a comprehensive clinical evaluation by a healthcare professional, and test results should always be interpreted within the clinical context. The panel urges clinicians to consider the pretest probability of AD pathology for each patient when deciding whether to use a BBM test.

## Remarks

The associated systematic review summarizes the performance of different BBM tests and will be regularly updated; many assays are still in development, optimization, and validation, and reported performance may evolve. The first set of meta-analyses excluded biomarker combinations/ratios involving different protein types (e.g., p-tau217/Aβ42) unless the ratio included a reference measure from the same protein/peptide (e.g., Aβ42/40). Most eligible literature provided sensitivity and specificity for a single cutoff (e.g., Youden's index); a limited number used a two-cutoff approach (values above the upper cutoff confirm and below the lower cutoff rule out AD pathology with high certainty) — the panel will consider this approach as more peer-reviewed publications become available.

**Scenarios where a BBM test may not be appropriate** (low utility per shared decision-making, e.g., when knowing AD-pathology status would not inform care given patient preferences).

**Scenarios where a BBM test may not be appropriate or should be interpreted with extra caution:**
1. Obvious modifiable/temporary conditions likely to account for the cognitive impairment.
2. Limited life expectancy (clinical significance/prognosis of AD pathology not well-defined).
3. History of conditions that can affect the brain and may impact a given BBM in under-studied ways (e.g., neurocysticercosis, HIV, prior chemotherapy or radiation, chronic traumatic encephalopathy).
4. Medical conditions that may affect BBM levels (e.g., acute brain injury, severe chronic kidney disease, ALS).
5. Certain medications that may impact BBM levels (e.g., neprilysin inhibitors, drugs that disrupt the blood–brain barrier).

*Abbreviations: AD, Alzheimer's disease; ALS, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis; BBM, blood-based biomarker; CSF, cerebrospinal fluid; HIV, human immunodeficiency virus.*
