# Study Design

> Grounding: abstract-only. This file captures only design details stated in the metadata/abstract.

## Design

- Prospective cohort analysis.
- Cohort: Atherosclerosis Risk in Communities (ARIC) study.
- Follow-up interval: 1987-2020.
- Analysis period: August 2023 to December 2024.
- Field centers: Jackson, Mississippi; Forsyth County, North Carolina; Minneapolis suburbs, Minnesota; Washington County, Maryland.

## Participants

- Eligible participants are described as Black and White participants with complete exposure and covariate data.
- Study baseline was set by age at risk factor measurement.
- Age-window analyses were performed for 45-54 years, 55-64 years, and 65-74 years.

## Exposures

- Hypertension: systolic BP >=130 mm Hg, diastolic BP >=80 mm Hg, or use of medication for BP.
- Diabetes: fasting glucose >=126 mg/dL, nonfasting glucose >=200 mg/dL, self-reported physician diagnosis, or use of any diabetes medication.
- Current smoking: self-reported.
- Exposure summary used for attributable-fraction reporting: at least one vascular risk factor.

## Outcome

- Incident dementia.
- Attributable fractions were estimated by age 80 years and separately after 80 years.

## Subgroups

- APOE e4 genotype.
- Self-reported race.
- Sex.
