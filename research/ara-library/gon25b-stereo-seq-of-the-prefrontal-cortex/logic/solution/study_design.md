# Study Design

> Grounding: abstract-only. This captures only study-design elements explicitly present in the abstract.

## Objective

Generate and analyze a subcellular-resolution spatial transcriptome atlas of the human prefrontal cortex in aging and Alzheimer's disease.

## Inputs

- **Cases**: Six male AD cases at varying neuropathological stages.
- **Controls**: Six age-matched male controls.
- **Tissue**: Human prefrontal cortex.
- **Assay**: Stereo-seq.
- **Additional sample metadata**: Not available from provided input.

## Analysis Components

### Atlas Construction
- **Purpose**: Create a subcellular-resolution spatial transcriptome atlas of human PFC.
- **Supported claims**: C01
- **Missing details**: Tissue handling, sequencing chemistry, spatial binning, cell segmentation, quality control, and data release are not available from provided input.

### Layer and Laminar Analysis
- **Purpose**: Identify transcriptional alterations across PFC layers and disruptions in laminar structure.
- **Supported claims**: C02
- **Missing details**: Layer annotation method, statistical contrasts, and laminar disruption metrics are not available from provided input.

### Interaction Analysis
- **Purpose**: Detect AD-related shifts in layer-to-layer and cell-cell interactions, including stress-response interactions connected to amyloid-beta clearance.
- **Supported claims**: C02, C03
- **Missing details**: Interaction inference algorithm, cell-type definitions, ligand-receptor resources, and significance criteria are not available from provided input.

### Cell-Type-Specific Co-Expression and Regulatory Analysis
- **Purpose**: Identify neuronal modules linked to neuroprotection, protein dephosphorylation, and amyloid-beta regulation, then nominate regulatory transcription factors.
- **Supported claims**: C04
- **Missing details**: Co-expression algorithm, module membership, regulatory inference method, and validation data are not available from provided input.

## Outputs Reported in Abstract

- Subcellular-resolution spatial transcriptome atlas of human PFC.
- AD-associated transcriptional alterations across PFC layers.
- Disrupted laminar structure.
- AD-related shifts in layer-to-layer and cell-cell interactions.
- Upregulated genes in stressed neurons and nearby glial cells.
- Diminished stress-response interactions that promote amyloid-beta clearance.
- Three neuronal modules linked to neuroprotection, protein dephosphorylation, and amyloid-beta regulation.
- ZNF460 identified as a transcription factor regulating those modules.
