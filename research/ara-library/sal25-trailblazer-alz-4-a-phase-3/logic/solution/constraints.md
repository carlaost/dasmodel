# Constraints — TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 4

## Boundary conditions
- Applies to early symptomatic AD (MCI or mild dementia), age 50–85, amyloid-positive by florbetapir PET, MMSE 20–30, CDR-GS 0.5/1.0.
- Amyloid outcomes are florbetapir-PET-derived Centiloids; clearance defined as < 24.1 CL. Findings are specific to this tracer/threshold.
- Comparator regimen is aducanumab per US ADUHELM label; donanemab per the trial's dosing schedule with cessation criteria.
- US-only sites (31); trial population predominantly White (donanemab arm 100% White).

## Assumptions
- A1: < 24.1 CL is a valid operational definition of amyloid plaque clearance (refs 17, 18).
- A2: The low–medium tau subpopulation, defined by flortaucipir PET, is clinically meaningful (refs 13,14,19,20).
- A3: Central blinded imaging reads and identical MRI schedules mitigate open-label bias for imaging/ARIA endpoints.
- A4: Plasma biomarkers were log-transformed for analysis (exploratory).

## Known limitations (stated by the authors, §4)
- **Open-label design**: treatment allocation was known to participants and investigators, which may have affected safety reporting (mitigated by identical MRI schedules and central blinded reads).
- **No cognitive/functional outcomes**: because of the open-label design, cognitive and functional measures were not performed, so the clinical significance of greater/faster amyloid clearance is unclear.
- **Amyloid PET cannot capture all mechanistic differences**: mechanistic differences (e.g., effects on soluble oligomers vs insoluble fibrils) may not be detectable by amyloid PET, and no methodology currently exists to measure soluble oligomers and insoluble fibrils.
- **Limited participant diversity**: diversity of trial participants and sites remains a challenge; need for improved reach, retention, and understanding of screen-failure variables (ref 23).
- **Uncertain clinical relevance of the comparison**: aducanumab is no longer available (manufacturer discontinued development in January 2024), although it was expected to be standard of care at trial design.
- **Reduced enrollment / power**: 148 randomized vs ~200 planned (power was estimated at ≥98% under the planned design and assumed clearance rates).

## Reproducibility constraints
- No public individual participant data; IPD available on request via Vivli (see `data/dataset.md`), gated by an independent review committee and a signed data-sharing agreement.
- No code/software repository released; analyses performed in SAS v9.4; further methodological detail on ClinicalTrials.gov/study/NCT05108922.
