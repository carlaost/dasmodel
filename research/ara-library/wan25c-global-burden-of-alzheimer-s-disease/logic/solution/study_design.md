# Study Design

> Grounding: abstract-only. This file captures only the design elements stated in the abstract.

## Objective

Assess trends, burden, and inequalities of Alzheimer's disease and other dementias at global, regional, and national levels from 1990 to 2021.

## Data Source

- Global Burden of Disease and Risk Factors Study 2021 (GBD 2021).
- Disease-burden data for AD and other dementias during 1990-2021 were extracted from GBD 2021.
- Extraction details: Not available from provided input.

## Outcomes

- Age-standardized prevalence.
- Age-standardized mortality.
- Age-standardized disability-adjusted life-years (DALYs).
- DALY risk-factor ranking.

## Trend Indicator

- Average annual percentage changes (AAPCs) were estimated for age-standardized prevalence, mortality, and DALYs.
- The abstract says AAPCs were used "as an indicator to evaluate the healthcare system."
- Model form and software: Not available from provided input.

## Inequality / Stratification Dimensions

- Global, regional, and national level.
- Age group, including the population aged 65-69.
- Sociodemographic index grouping, including high-middle SDI and low-middle SDI.
- Regional/country examples named in the abstract: East Asia (e.g., China) and South Asia (e.g., India).

## Risk-Factor Analysis

- The abstract reports high fasting plasma glucose as the highest-ranked risk factor for DALYs during 1990-2021.
