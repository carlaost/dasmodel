# Heuristics — Recommended Sample-Handling Protocol

These are the practical sample-handling recommendations the authors derived from the
pre-analytical experiments (§3.6, §4 Discussion) to be sent to ADAPT stage-3 trial sites.

## H01: Pre-centrifugation hold time
- **Rationale**: p-tau217 was robust to pre-centrifugation delay; median relative change stayed within ±10% (ALZpath Experiment 1 showed a small significant reduction between 1 and 3 h at RT that normalized by 3 h).
- **Sensitivity**: low
- **Bounds**: Blood can be stored up to 3 hours at room temperature or 24 hours refrigerated (2–8°C) before centrifugation.
- **Code ref**: Not specified
- **Source**: §4 Discussion (recommended protocol); §3.6, Figure 2A/2B

## H02: Post-centrifugation plasma storage
- **Rationale**: A transient subtle reduction (≈5%) in Lumipulse p-tau217 was seen when post-centrifugation samples were kept in the fridge for 4–24 h (Experiment 3, P = 0.0047), mitigable by storing at −20°C; overall change stayed within ±10%.
- **Sensitivity**: low
- **Bounds**: Plasma can be stored up to 2 weeks frozen at −20°C (or refrigerated at 2–8°C if necessary).
- **Code ref**: Not specified
- **Source**: §3.6, §4 Discussion; Figure 2C; Table S10/S12

## H03: Freeze–thaw cycles
- **Rationale**: Freeze–thaw cycles did not yield statistically significant differences (Experiment 4); median change within ±10%.
- **Sensitivity**: low
- **Bounds**: Up to four freeze–thaw cycles prior to p-tau217 measurement are acceptable.
- **Code ref**: Not specified
- **Source**: §3.6, Figure 2D

## H04: Sub-zero transport without dry ice
- **Rationale**: Lumipulse p-tau217 from −20°C Bio-Freeze transport correlated with −80°C storage at rho = 0.998 with no systematic bias (Experiment / §3.8).
- **Sensitivity**: low
- **Bounds**: Plasma can be transported at sub-zero temperature using a temperature-controlled transport system (e.g., Bio-Freeze, −20 to 0°C) without the need for dry ice.
- **Code ref**: Not specified
- **Source**: §3.8, §4 Discussion; Figure S5

## H05: Assess serum creatinine when interpreting p-tau217
- **Rationale**: ≥ CKD-3a (eGFR < 60) was associated with elevation of p-tau217 predominantly into the intermediate zone; renal function can raise borderline values.
- **Sensitivity**: medium (near cut-points)
- **Bounds**: Serum creatinine should be assessed for patients whom p-tau217 is used as an adjunct to diagnosis; use extra caution interpreting p-tau217 in ≤ CKD-3a (eGFR < 60) patients.
- **Code ref**: Not specified
- **Source**: §3.5, §4 Discussion; refs 47,48
