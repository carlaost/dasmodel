# Constraints, Assumptions, and Limitations

## Boundary conditions
- Cut-points (Lumipulse 0.153/0.422 pg/mL) are derived for and intended for **specialist memory-service** interpretation of Lumipulse (Fujirebio) plasma p-tau217 in individuals with cognitive impairment — not primary care, not population screening.
- Cut-points are assay-specific (Lumipulse) and reference-definition-specific (CSF Aβ42/Aβ40 ≤ 0.065, "amyloid only"). ALZpath cut-points (0.220/0.486 pg/mL) differ.
- The clinical assay implemented uses plasma p-tau217 alone (not a p-tau217/Aβ42 ratio), because the literature at study design supported single-biomarker use and the ratio is harder to apply in routine due to Aβ42 pre-analytical susceptibility.

## Assumptions
- CSF Lumipulse Aβ42/Aβ40 ≤ 0.065 is a valid AD-status reference for derivation; amyloid PET visual read is a valid independent reference for validation.
- A ±10% relative change from baseline is the clinically significant threshold for pre-analytical effects (following prior publications).
- CN-CKD cohort aged < 60 has < 20% background amyloid prevalence, so elevated p-tau217 there is attributable to renal function rather than AD pathology.
- eGFR via race-free CKD-EPI 2021 creatinine equation.

## Known limitations (stated by the authors, §4)
- **L1 — Symptomatic-only cohorts**: Both derivation (CSF) and validation (amyloid PET) cohorts comprised symptomatic memory-service patients; transferability to primary care, population screening, or other intended uses is unvalidated.
- **L2 — Ethnicity not systematically recorded**: Black, Asian, and other ethnicities comprised only 11% (CSF cohort) and 25% (amyloid PET cohort); inter-ethnic differences in p-tau217 are reported elsewhere with mixed results; no clear indication that ethnicity-specific cut-points are needed, but larger diverse samples are required.
- **L3 — Limited comorbidity/staging data**: Partial availability of cognitive/functional staging (MMSE/ACE-III) and comorbidity data across cohorts.
- **L4 — BMI effect inconclusive**: No significant BMI effect found, but the CSF derivation cohort had median BMI 25.8 available for only 59% of participants, and BMI data were unavailable for the amyloid PET cohort; cannot refute prior reports of low p-tau217 with high BMI.
- **L5 — Serum creatinine effect inconclusive**: No significant creatinine effect on p-tau217, possibly due to median-normal-range values (73–74 µmol/L) and small sample with data available (24% and 60%). Addressed separately via the ADPKD/CKD cohort.
- **L6 — CKD caution**: p-tau217 was not frequently elevated to AD-diagnostic levels in CKD, but ≥ CKD-3a (eGFR < 60) is associated with elevation predominantly into the intermediate zone; serum creatinine should be assessed and extra caution used interpreting p-tau217 in ≤ CKD-3a patients.
- **L7 — Amyloid PET vs CSF discordance**: Larger indeterminate proportion in the PET-defined cohort (34.2%) partly reflects known amyloid-PET-visual-read vs quantitative/CSF discordance (5–14% of cases) and differing amyloid/tau burden between cohorts; amyloid burden degree in the CSF cohort was unknown.
- **L8 — Aβ42 not tested**: Plasma Aβ42 (for a p-tau217/Aβ42 ratio, later FDA-relevant) was not tested in stage 1.
- **L9 — Cut-point temporal/prospective stability unconfirmed**: Long-term and prospective stability of cut-points (over 3 years, against CSF and amyloid PET) is deferred to ADAPT stages 2/3.
