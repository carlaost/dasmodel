### Statistical results (from §3.6)
- Experiment 1 (A): Lumipulse not significant; ALZpath significant (Friedman P = 0.007), post hoc difference between 1 h and 3 h; normalization by 3 h.
- Experiment 2 (B): no significant differences (P > 0.05) for either assay.
- Experiment 3 (C): Lumipulse significant (P = 0.0047), post hoc between 4 h and 24 h at 2–8°C vs 2-week −20°C storage; transient ≈5% reduction when refrigerated 4–24 h; ALZpath robust.
- Experiment 4 (D): no significant differences for either assay.

## Trend summary
Across all four experiments and both assays, box medians cluster within the ±10% bounds; plasma p-tau217 is robust to pre-analytical handling. The only statistically significant (but clinically small, ≤10%) effects are ALZpath in Experiment 1 (pre-centrifugation delay at RT) and Lumipulse in Experiment 3 (post-centrifugation refrigeration), the latter mitigable by −20°C storage. Individual-level outliers (e.g., one ALZpath point at ≈2.0 in Experiment 1) exist but do not shift group medians.
