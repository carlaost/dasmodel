## Trend summary
County crude ADRD incidence spans ≈386 to ≈1148 per 100,000. The highest-incidence class (909–1148, dark blue) concentrates in the Pee Dee region (e.g., Dillon, Marion, Florence, Williamsburg, Sumter, Clarendon, Lee, Darlington, Kershaw) plus a few scattered counties (e.g., Allendale, Hampton, Greenwood). The lowest class (386–680) appears in several Midlands/Low Country counties (e.g., Richland, Saluda, Calhoun, Berkeley, York). The map is the county-level basis for the region-level pattern in Figure 3 (Pee Dee highest). Exact per-county values are not printed on the map.
