# Results Summary

Directional narrative of findings; exact values in `evidence/`. Region abbreviations: LC = Low Country, Mid = Midlands, PD = Pee Dee, Up = Upstate.

## Cohort (Table 1, Figure 1)
- N = 18,955 analyzed (from 377,143 registry records 1988–2021; restricted to 2021 diagnoses and ages 50–110).
- Predominantly AD (~79%), aged 75+ (75–84 ≈ 38%, ≥85 ≈ 35%), female (~59%), White (~66%). Mixed dementia ~2%.
- Regional counts do not sum to the total because 3,910 records had missing county/zip.

## Crude ADRD incidence (Figures 2, 3; Table 2)
- Region ordering: PD (896) > Up (840) > LC (764) > Mid (684) per 100,000.
- County map (Figure 2) places the highest-incidence class (909–1148) predominantly in Pee Dee counties.
- All six pairwise crude-incidence comparisons are statistically significant (p < 0.05). → C01.

## ADRD-specific incidence by PHR (Figures 4–7; Table 2)
- **AD**: highest in PD (727); PD significantly higher than each other region. LC vs Up not significant (p = 0.1402). → C02.
- **VaD**: highest in Up (59); Mid vs Up significant (IRR 0.642, p < 0.0001); LC vs Up significant (p = 0.0011). Several VaD contrasts non-significant. → C03.
- **Mixed**: highest in Up (24); Mid vs Up significant (IRR 0.450, p < 0.0001); LC vs Mid significant (p = 0.0282). → C03.
- **Other**: highest and tied in PD and Up (102 each); several significant contrasts (e.g., Mid vs Up p = 0.0009; Mid vs PD p = 0.0028). → C03.

## Age-specific incidence (Table 3)
- AD incidence rises steeply with age within every PHR (e.g., PD AD: 69.534 <65 → 388.184 65–74 → 1913.741 75–84 → 7955.116 ≥85).
- 65–74, 75–84, ≥85: AD highest in PD; VaD and Mixed highest in Up.
- <65: AD, Mixed, Other highest in Up; VaD highest in PD; Mid lowest for Mixed/Other, LC lowest for AD/VaD.
- **Discrepancy** (reproduced): the Discussion says AD/VaD/Mixed were highest in PD among <65, contradicting Table 3 (Up highest for AD and Mixed). The 75–84 "Midlands lowest across all types" is inexact for AD and Other (LC is lower). See table3.md, claims.md C05.

## Missing data & sensitivity (in-text; Supplementary Table 1 / Figure 1 online)
- Missingness associated with age-group (p < 0.0001, V = 0.15) and ADRD diagnosis (p < 0.0001, V = 0.12); weakly with sex (p = 0.020, V = 0.018): not completely at random.
- Proportional redistribution: complete-case analysis underestimates incidence by ~20.6%, consistently across all PHRs.
- Extreme-case reassignment: only Mid and PD change; other PHRs stable → estimates robust. → C06.

## Interpretation (Discussion / Conclusion)
- Higher Pee Dee ADRD/AD incidence is consistent with rurality, high social vulnerability, lower educational attainment, and cardiovascular risk-factor burden (stroke belt).
- Higher Upstate VaD is attributed partly to a dense cluster of healthcare facilities enabling diagnosis, plus vascular risk factors.
- Explicitly associational, not causal (observational registry study). → C07.
