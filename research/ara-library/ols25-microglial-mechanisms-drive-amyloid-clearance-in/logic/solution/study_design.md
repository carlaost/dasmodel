# Study Design

> Grounding: abstract-only. This file captures only design elements explicitly stated in the abstract.

## Objective

Explore the effects of active and passive Aβ immunization in the AD brain and identify microglial/transcriptional mechanisms associated with Aβ removal.

## Cohorts and Comparators

- Actively immunized patients with AD.
- Nonimmunized patients with AD.
- Neurologically healthy controls.
- Lecanemab-treated context for deeper analysis of transcriptional pathways involved in Aβ removal.
- Exact cohort counts, demographics, clinical stages, postmortem intervals, and matching criteria: Not available from provided input.

## Data Modalities

- Spatial transcriptomics.
- High-resolution spatial transcriptomics.
- Single-cell RNA sequencing.
- Antibody response and Aβ removal/clearance measures are mentioned as correlation targets, but measurement methods are not available from provided input.

## Analysis Targets

- Distinct microglial states associated with Aβ clearance.
- Spatially distinct microglial responses varying by brain region.
- Microglial TREM2 and APOE upregulation across immunization approaches.
- Positive correlation of TREM2/APOE upregulation with antibody responses and Aβ removal.
- Complement signaling in brain myeloid cells as a contributor to Aβ clearance after immunization.
