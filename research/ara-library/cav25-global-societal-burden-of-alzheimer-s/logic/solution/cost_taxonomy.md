## Hierarchy

```
Societal cost
├── Direct cost
│   ├── Direct medical
│   │   ├── Healthcare
│   │   │   ├── Medication
│   │   │   ├── ER (emergency department visits)
│   │   │   ├── Inpatient
│   │   │   ├── Outpatient*
│   │   │   ├── Diagnostics
│   │   │   └── Consults**
│   │   ├── Dentist
│   │   ├── Hospice
│   │   ├── Rehabilitation
│   │   ├── Mental Health Services
│   │   └── Caregiver healthcare†† (caregiver-specific medications, hospital nights, ER visits, outpatient visits — for the caregiver only)
│   └── Direct non-medical
│       ├── Community/Social care
│       │   ├── Daycare
│       │   ├── Institutional care‡‡ (residential care + nursing care)
│       │   ├── Domiciliary care
│       │   ├── Modifications† (to diet, home, car, or related items)
│       │   ├── Surveillance
│       │   ├── Home help
│       │   ├── Financial aid
│       │   ├── Meals§ (pre-prepared meals; meal/grocery delivery)
│       │   ├── Caregiver respite
│       │   └── Transport‡ (associated with medical visits and with reduced mobility)
│       ├── Police call outs
│       └── Financial delinquency
└── Indirect cost
    ├── Loss of productivity & leisure time (Caregiver)
    │   ├── Assistance with BADL (basic activities of daily living)
    │   └── Assistance with iADL (instrumental activities of daily living)
    ├── Patient supervision
    ├── Loss of productivity (Patient)
    └── Patient & caregiver
        ├── Public fiscal losses
        └── Loss of QoL costs
```

## Visual Description (for the evidence-layer record)
- **Components**: A top-down tree diagram with a single root box ("Societal cost") splitting into two color-coded top-level branches ("Direct cost" in dark blue-grey, "Indirect cost" in dark green), each further split into sub-branches rendered as nested colored boxes of decreasing shade intensity to indicate hierarchy depth.
- **Connections**: Pure containment/hierarchy (no cross-links or feedback loops) — every leaf box belongs to exactly one parent category.
- **Annotations**: Footnote markers (*, **, †, ‡, §, ††, ‡‡) attached to specific leaf boxes point to explanatory footnotes below the figure (e.g., "Outpatient*" → "General practitioner, geriatrician, psychiatrist, neurologist, physiotherapist, occupational therapist, social worker, Psychologist"; "Consults**" → "Neurologists, geriatricians, psychiatrists, psycho-geriatricians, neuro-psychiatrists, and specialists in memory clinics").
- **What it conveys**: This diagram is the review's unifying cost-definition framework (referenced directly in problem.md's Key Insight) — it operationalizes "societal cost" as an exhaustive partition into direct-medical, direct-non-medical, and indirect components, explicitly including the two categories (police call-outs / financial delinquency under direct non-medical; public fiscal losses / loss-of-QoL costs under indirect) that motivate this review's distinct contribution (problem.md G2).
