# Constraints

## Scope Boundaries (from Methods / PICOS, Table 1)
- **Population**: Adult (≥18 years) patients with MCI due to AD (only if mild dementia due to AD is also presented in the study, when the MCI etiology is unknown), or adult patients with AD/dementia with an explicitly defined severity (mild, moderate, severe, or moderately-severe to severe). Populations with mixed dementia are included only if AD-specific subgroup results are reported, or if the AD proportion is ≥80% of the aggregate sample.
- **Intervention/Comparator**: Not applicable — this is a cost-burden review, not an interventional or comparative-effectiveness study.
- **Outcomes**: Direct medical/non-medical costs, indirect costs, overall societal costs, caregiver profiles and hours spent caregiving, and non-medical costs not typically reported by AD cost-of-illness studies (financial delinquencies, quality-of-life loss costs). Studies with cost data not extractable (e.g., only presented in figures) or not reported across discrete disease severities were excluded.
- **Study designs included**: Cost-of-illness studies; real-world studies (prospective, retrospective, cross-sectional); systematic reviews and meta-analyses.
- **Study designs excluded**: Pre-clinical studies, expert opinions, letters, pilot studies, protocols, clinical trials, economic evaluations.
- **Language**: English only.
- **Timeframe**: January 1, 2013 – June 12, 2024 for SLRs/meta-analyses; individual primary studies published before 2013 admitted only if identified via an eligible SLR and reporting cost by discrete disease severity.
- **Geography**: No geographic restriction (None specified for inclusion or exclusion).

## Assumptions (methodological choices, stated or directly implied)
- Disease severity across five different underlying staging instruments (MMSE, CDR-SB, FAST, GDS, DS) can be validly harmonized into four common buckets (MCI/very mild, mild, moderate, severe) for cross-study comparison, "regardless of the dementia staging scales used by different studies."
- No adjustment of cost year or currency to a common baseline was performed; costs are reported as originally published, trading standardization for fidelity to source data.
- Where a cost was reported as a range within a severity category, the median value was used as the single representative estimate.
- A single primary reviewer performed study screening and data extraction, with a second reviewer performing selective (not full independent dual) quality-assurance checks, and a third reviewer resolving any disagreements.
- No ethics approval was required or sought, since the review analyzes only previously published literature and involved no new human or animal data collection by the authors.

## Known Limitations (from Discussion, "Strengths, Limitations, and Future Direction")
- **L1 — Non-uniform severity definitions**: "Definitions of disease severity were not uniform amongst all publications. For example, various studies used diverse scales to assess AD or dementia severity, such as the MMSE, CDR-sum of boxes, FAST, and Dependence scales... Notably, even studies using the same instrument to classify disease severity (e.g., CDR-SB) applied different scoring criteria for disease severity, adding additional variability." The authors state they "believe... overall conclusions of this would not reasonably change if scales were standardized, given the consistency with which overall results were observed," but this is an unverified assumption rather than a demonstrated robustness check.
- **L2 — Unstandardized cost years and currencies**: "Cost years and currencies were presented in this review as reported from all publications and were not inflated to a common cost year or currency. This limits the ability to compare how costs have evolved over time, across geographies, and across care settings." The authors justify this by prioritizing provision of "raw data from studies... important... so that information to inform future health economic evaluations can be directly derived from this review."
- **L3 — Inclusion of studies with limited scope or sample size**: "This review also included studies that only reported one cost category or had small sample sizes. As such, some individual study results should be interpreted with caution."
- **L4 — Sparse institutional-care evidence**: Only one of the 81 included studies was set specifically in institutional care alone; "Studies focusing on institutional care were not available to show patterns in direct costs with advancing disease," limiting the granularity of institutional-care findings relative to community care.
- **L5 — Sparse patient-specific indirect-cost evidence**: "Few studies reported on patient-specific indirect costs (i.e., patient-specific losses in productivity); in fact, only one presented data stratified by AD severity," meaning most indirect-cost figures in this review reflect caregiver burden only, not combined patient+caregiver productivity loss.
- **L6 — Single-reviewer screening/extraction with limited independent replication**: Study selection and data extraction were performed by a single primary reviewer, with only selective (not full dual-independent) checking by a second reviewer, which the review itself does not characterize as a limitation but which constrains the reliability guarantees typical of a full systematic review.

## Boundary Conditions on Specific Findings
- The 126% MCI-to-mild cost increase (C03) and the four-fold mild-to-severe increase in Iran (C04) are each drawn from a single study and should not be read as typical/central-tendency estimates.
- The intangible-cost trends (C10, patient costs rising vs. caregiver costs falling with severity) come from one US patient-caregiver dyad survey (Monfared et al. 2024 [59]) and have not been independently replicated within this review's corpus.
- The financial-delinquency and public-fiscal-loss figures (C05, C06) are drawn almost entirely from single national sources (UK Alzheimer's Society 2024 [20]; Martins et al. 2022, Germany [68]) and their generalizability to other regions is not established within this review.
