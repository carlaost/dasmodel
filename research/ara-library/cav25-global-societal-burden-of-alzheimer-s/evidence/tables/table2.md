# Table 2: Definitions of indirect costing methods valuating the time spent caring for a patient with AD by an informal caregiver as reported by included studies
- **Source**: Table 2, p.1803
- **Caption**: "Definitions of indirect costing methods valuating the time spent caring for a patient with AD by an informal caregiver as reported by included studies"
- **Screenshot**: table2.png
- **Extraction type**: raw_table

| Costing method | Definition |
|---|---|
| Opportunity cost [24] | Assigns the market value of lost production time for working caregivers and the value of lost leisure time for non-working caregivers |
| Replacement cost [67] | Assigns the cost of informal care at the level of remuneration that is required to hire a professional caregiver, no matter whether a caregiver is working for pay or not |
| Friction cost [25] | Cost to employers due the time spent to replace ill workers and the corresponding expenses required to train new replacement employees |
| Human capital cost [43] | Assigns the cost of informal care based on the cost of absenteeism (i.e., loss of earnings and loss of productivity) |

**Note (verbatim footnote)**: AD Alzheimer's disease
