## Trend summary
- **Community care (Panel A)**: Mean caregiver age rises from 60 (MCI) to the mid-to-high 60s (Mild/Moderate/Severe) and stays roughly flat thereafter. Mean % of caregivers who are the patient's spouse rises from 44% (MCI) to a peak of 65% (Mild), then eases to 55–57% (Moderate/Severe). Mean % child caregiver falls from a peak of 42% (MCI) to 28–37% at later stages (a roughly inverse pattern to % spouse). Mean % female caregiver declines modestly and monotonically from 69% (MCI) to 60% (Severe). Mean % living with patient is highest at Severe (79%) but non-monotonic overall (68→75→69→79). Mean % working for pay declines monotonically and substantially, from 44% (MCI) to 26% (Severe) — caregivers are progressively less likely to also hold paid employment as disease severity increases.
- **Mixed care (Panel B)**: Mean caregiver age declines slightly and monotonically from 63 (MCI) to 58 (Severe) — the opposite direction from Panel A's age trend. Mean % spouse declines sharply and monotonically from 64% (MCI) to 32% (Severe), while mean % child caregiver rises sharply and monotonically from 18% (MCI) to 50% (Severe) — a clear crossover pattern (spouse-caregiving becomes relatively less common, child-caregiving more common, as severity increases in this care-setting grouping). Mean % female caregiver rises from 58% (MCI) to 71–72% (Mild/Moderate/Severe). Mean % living with patient rises from 68% (MCI) to a plateau of 84% (Moderate/Severe). Mean % working for pay is lowest at Mild (7%, the minimum value in the entire figure) and highest at Severe (26%), a non-monotonic (dip-then-rise) pattern unlike Panel A's monotonic decline.
- Across both panels, caregivers are consistently described by the surrounding text (Results, "Caregiver Burden and Indirect Costs," p.1812–1813) as "predominantly female... spouse or child of the patient, and near retirement age" (see exploration_tree.yaml N11) — the exact percentages in this figure substantiate that qualitative summary but the two care-setting panels show materially different spouse-vs-child and employment trends by severity, a distinction the surrounding prose does not fully spell out.
