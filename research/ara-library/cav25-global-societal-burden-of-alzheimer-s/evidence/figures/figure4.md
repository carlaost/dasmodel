## Notable individually-labeled bars (exact values, quoted directly from main text, not digitized from this figure)
- **Aajami et al. 2019 (Iran)**: the longest single bar in the figure (visually the largest total extent, reaching furthest right of any study, well past +275%), corresponding to the text's exact statement of "a four-fold jump from mild AD to severe AD" (≈+300%) — see C04.
- **Jetsonen et al. 2021 (Finland)**: the largest "MCI to Mild" (grey) segment of any study in the figure, consistent with the text's exact "126%" MCI-to-mild increase — see C03.
- Several studies (e.g., Yu 2021 China; isolated Gustavsson 2010a and Wübker 2015 bars) show a small bar extending left of 0%, i.e., a **negative** percent change (cost decrease) for at least one severity transition — the only clearly negative-going bars in the figure.

## Trend summary
- The overwhelming majority of plotted bars extend rightward (positive percent change), visually confirming C01's "societal costs typically increase ... with progression from mild ... to more severe stages" — increasing cost with severity is the dominant pattern across nearly all 34 plotted study-bars.
- Bar lengths vary widely within and across regions — some Europe & UK bars (e.g., Wübker 2015, several Gustavsson 2010a entries) show near-zero or modest (<25%) change, while others (Jetsonen 2021 Finland, Darba 2015 Spain, Wimo 2013 Germany, Michaud 2017 US) show changes exceeding 125–175% — consistent with C04's statement that cost-increase magnitude "varies substantially by region."
- The Aajami et al. 2019 (Iran) bar is a clear visual outlier, extending furthest of any bar in the figure, consistent with its identification in the text as the single largest relative increase reviewed.
- No study contributes all three segment types (MCI-to-Mild, Mild-to-Moderate, Moderate-to-Severe) at once at a length that would suggest a single dominant transition; most bars are composed predominantly of "Mild to Moderate" (light blue) and/or "Moderate to Severe" (dark navy) segments, reflecting the sparser MCI-stage cost reporting noted in problem.md G3.
