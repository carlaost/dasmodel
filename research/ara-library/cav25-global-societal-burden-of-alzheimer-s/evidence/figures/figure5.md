## Trend summary
- "Informal caregiving" is visually the single largest component at every severity stage, and its share is visibly larger at Moderate than at any other stage — directly consistent with the main text's exact statement (quoted in C02) that informal care "comprised > 50% of total costs" specifically at the moderate stage, and with claims.md C02's grounding.
- The MCI bar is visually distinct from the other three: it has far fewer visible sub-component colors (dominated by one very large "Medication, patient" (magenta) band plus "Long term care"), reflecting that fewer of the n=6 studies contributed granular MCI-stage breakdowns.
- Mild, Moderate, and Severe bars all show a similar dense multi-color "direct cost" base (many thin bands for ER visits, outpatient visits, medication, hospitalization, transportation, etc., split separately for patient vs. caregiver), consistent with direct costs remaining "at least one-third" of total societal cost across stages (C09) — though C09's exact 31–52% figure is drawn from a different n=8 subset (Fig. S2, not provided in this input) and is not read directly off this figure.
- No segment in any bar visually approaches zero-width extinction across severities; every one of the 24 legend categories appears to contribute at least a thin band at most severity stages, though several (Lab tests, Diagnostics, Consumables) are visually near-negligible throughout.
