## Trend summary
- The five "Number of overall studies" (blue) values in the Study design facet sum to exactly 81 (20+35+4+2+20=81), matching the review's total included-study count (n=81) — confirming that the blue segment represents the actual per-design-type study count, while the black/grey/light-grey segments stacked above it are a disaggregation of that same count by population type (AD-specific vs. Mixed dementia vs. broader Dementia), not an additive total. Each design category's population-type breakdown sums back to its own blue value (e.g., Retrospective: 16+3+1=20; SLR: 10+3+7=20), an internal self-consistency check within the source figure.
- Prospective studies (n=35) are the single largest study-design group, followed by Retrospective (n=20) and SLR (n=20) tied, then Simulation (n=4) and Patient advocacy reports (n=2).
- By care setting, Community care (n=33) and Mixed (n=32) settings dominate; only 1 study was institutional-care-only, and 15 did not specify care setting.
- By region, Europe+UK (n=37) is the most represented region, more than double any other region (Global n=15, North America n=15, Asia n=14).
