# Constraints, Assumptions and Limitations

## Boundary conditions
- **Population**: Single-centre, real-world memory-clinic cohort (Cognitive Centre, Ghent University Hospital), predominantly Belgian background; consecutively enrolled with available plasma up to data extraction (n = 187). Findings apply to cognitively symptomatic patients referred for evaluation — not to screening in cognitively unimpaired populations.
- **Reference standard**: AD vs non-AD defined by CSF biomarkers (n = 180) or, in a minority, amyloid-PET (n = 7), using reference-lab cut-offs (Aβ42/Aβ40 ≤ 0.070, Aβ42 ≤ 722 pg/mL, p-tau181 ≥ 50 pg/mL, total tau ≥ 403 pg/mL).
- **Cut-offs are cohort-derived**: Optimal single cut-offs (Youden) and 95% sensitivity/specificity thresholds were derived within this same cohort (no separate derivation/validation split), so performance estimates may be optimistic.
- **Assay-specific values**: Absolute p-tau217 concentrations differ between Elecsys (ECLIA) and Lumipulse (CLEIA); cut-offs are not transferable across assays without harmonization.

## Assumptions
- CSF biomarkers (± amyloid-PET) are a valid gold standard for AD pathology (A1 in problem.md).
- Plasma-based APOE-ε4 proteotyping adequately substitutes for APOE-ε4 genotype.
- MoCA is an adequate longitudinal cognitive outcome (acknowledged as limited).
- Samples below LLOQ are interpreted at the LLOQ value.

## Known limitations (stated by authors)
- **Single-centre design**; no cross-validation in an independent cohort.
- **Limited analytical evaluation**: interference testing, multi-lot and multi-operator precision, and upper-limit-of-quantification assessment were NOT performed (beyond the scope of this clinical-performance study).
- **Longitudinal subset is modest** (n = 69 AD with follow-up, median 2.1 years) and relies solely on MoCA, which has limited sensitivity to subtle change and possible floor effects — longitudinal findings are exploratory (decline trend p = 0.07, not significant).
- **Renal-function null may be underpowered**: only 21 patients had eGFR < 60 and none < 30 (advanced CKD), where stronger p-tau217 effects have been reported.
- **Discordant CSF profiles** (n = 16) were excluded from primary analyses and analyzed only exploratorily.
- **Supplementary analyses** (imprecision by concentration, ND/PSY subgroups, discordant-case sensitivity analysis, linearity) reside in supplementary material not included in the provided PDF.
