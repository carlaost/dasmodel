# Study Design

## Type
Retrospective, single-centre diagnostic-accuracy / method-comparison study nested in an ongoing
prospective biobank (CCUG-Biobank, initiated 2021). Observational — no interventional arm, no trial
registration.

## Population and sampling
- **Source**: Cognitive Centre Ghent University (CCUG)-Biobank, Ghent University Hospital. Collects blood + CSF, plus clinical, neuropsychological, and imaging data from patients referred for cognitive complaints.
- **Inclusion**: Consecutively enrolled participants with available plasma up to the data-extraction date, n = 187. Predominantly Belgian background.
- **Reference-standard classification**: AD pathology determined by CSF biomarkers (n = 180) or amyloid-PET (n = 7).
  - **AD**: n = 103
  - **non-AD cognitive disorders**: n = 84, comprising non-AD neurological disorders (ND, n = 54; other neurodegenerative + vascular aetiologies) and psychiatric/psychological causes (PSY, n = 30).
  - **AD prevalence** in cohort: 55.1%.
- **Excluded from primary comparisons**: 16 participants with discordant CSF AT(N) profiles (15 A+T−N−, 1 other), analyzed separately (see C11 / E09).

## Reference standard cut-offs (CSF, Lumipulse G1200)
- Aβ42/Aβ40 ratio ≤ 0.070
- Aβ42 ≤ 722 pg/mL
- p-tau181 ≥ 50 pg/mL
- total tau ≥ 403 pg/mL
(validated reference-laboratory standards, ref [35]; CSF measured blinded.)

## Ethics
- Approved by the local ethics committee of Ghent University Hospital (ONZ-2024-0422).
- Conducted per the World Medical Association Declaration of Helsinki.
- Written informed consent from all participants (or legally authorised representative when decision-making capacity lacking).

## Endpoints / analyses
1. Diagnostic accuracy (AUC, single cut-off) of Elecsys p-tau217 vs Lumipulse p-tau217 vs Elecsys p-tau181.
2. Two-cut-off (grey-zone) classification per biomarker.
3. Added value of APOE-ε4 carrier status (logistic regression).
4. Added value of Aβ42 / Aβ42-40 (Fujirebio subset).
5. Method comparison of the two p-tau217 assays (Passing–Bablok, Bland–Altman).
6. Analytical performance (precision, linearity, LLOQ).
7. Influence of clinical cofactors on Elecsys p-tau217 (linear regression).
8. Exploratory longitudinal association with MoCA decline (mixed-effects).
9. Discordant-CSF subgroup + sensitivity analysis.

## Power
Authors state the sample (AD = 103, non-AD = 84) gives ~90–99% power to detect a medium-to-large
effect (Cohen's d = 0.5–0.8) in plasma p-tau levels at a two-sided α = 0.05.
