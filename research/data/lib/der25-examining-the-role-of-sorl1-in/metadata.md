## Abstract

Disruption of endolysosomal trafficking is a key pathogenic driver in Alzheimer's disease (AD). Among the genes linked to the endosomal pathway in AD, SORL1 is recognized as one of the most significant risk factors. SORL1 encodes an endocytic sorting receptor that regulates endosomal trafficking and processing of crucial AD‐related molecules, including pathogenic forms of amyloid‐β (e.g., Aβ42) and amyloid precursor protein (APP). Using complementary cell‐based models, we examined the impact of a protein‐truncating variant of SORL1 on endolysosomal trafficking and APP processing.
