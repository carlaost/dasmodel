## Abstract

Plasma phosphorylated tau at threonine 217 (p‐tau217) has emerged as one of the most promising blood‐based biomarkers for Alzheimer's disease (AD). While rare, heterophilic antibodies (HAb) are a persistent potential confounding factor in immunoassays.
