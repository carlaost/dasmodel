## Abstract

Alzheimer's disease (AD) cognitive decline is correlated with tau neuropathology, which can follow a typical spatiotemporal pattern spreading from the medial temporal lobe to the lateral temporal, parietal, frontal, and occipital lobes, at varying levels of severity. However, atypical patterns have also been observed in postmortem studies. Recently, more studies have used hypothesis‐driven partitioning informed by postmortem findings or data‐driven clustering with in‐vivo imaging data to identify AD subtypes with distinct clinical characteristics. However, major challenges remain: (i) AD subtypes are poorly defined, and are difficult to compare, especially at the individual‐level, (ii) Distinct pathological tau and gray matter atrophy patterns characterize AD subtypes, but the unique spatiotemporal relationship is unknown. Thus, the present study proposes a framework to aid interpretation of subtype replicability by utilizing hypothesis‐driven postmortem data to guide data‐driven clustering.
