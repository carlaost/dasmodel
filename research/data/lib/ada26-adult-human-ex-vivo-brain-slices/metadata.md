## Abstract

Glial cells are critical modulators of brain function in health, aging, and disease. This study establishes a robust ex vivo platform for cell-type-specific interrogation of glial responses and multicellular crosstalk, based on adult human organotypic brain slice cultures from neurosurgical resections. Cultures preserve tissue architecture and mature cell identities over weeks, elicit stimulus-specific transcriptional programs to diverse stressors, and resolve coordinated glial responses to TNFα (validated in postmortem human brains), including pro-/anti-inflammatory loops among microglia, astrocytes, and OPCs.
