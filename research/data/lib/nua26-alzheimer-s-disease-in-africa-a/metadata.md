## Abstract

The global burden of Alzheimer's disease (AD) is set to increase in the next three decades particularly because of the global increase in life expectancy. The state of AD in Africa is a cause for concern because the continent's rapid population growth and demographic transition will be major contributors to AD's increased global burden. This scoping review aims to investigate the following: the extent of AD research in Africa, the major contributors, key research trends, areas that require further investigation. By addressing these questions, this review will help map out existing literature and highlight areas needing further investigation.
