## Abstract

Early proper diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease remains one of the biggest challenges in medical imaging. This study introduces a new hybrid neural network that blends Transformer architecture with Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN) to effectively integrate multimodal MRI and PET brain images. The model leverages the power of CNN's capturing local spatial information and the Transformer's ability to capture long-range dependencies, allowing for better fusion of structural and functional information. Established tests Alzheimer's imaging findings reveal that the hybrid method that significantly enhances feature representation and classification accuracy relative to benchmark single-modality and unimodal models. The results show that the hybrid architecture offers a promising avenue to the production computerized, preclinical diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease.
