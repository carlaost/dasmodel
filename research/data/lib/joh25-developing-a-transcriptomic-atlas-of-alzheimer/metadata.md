## Abstract

Alzheimer's disease (AD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder marked by cognitive decline, ultimately leading to dementia and death. While the amyloid hypothesis has historically guided AD research, emerging evidence suggests alternative mechanisms, including vascular dysfunction. Angiogenesis, the formation of new blood vessels, is increasingly implicated in early AD pathology, yet transcriptomic insights remain limited. To address this gap, we generated the first single‐cell transcriptomic profile of AD progression in a mouse model, aiming to identify novel molecular mechanisms and potential therapeutic targets.
