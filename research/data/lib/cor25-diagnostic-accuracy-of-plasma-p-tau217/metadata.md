# Diagnostic accuracy of plasma p-tau217 and Aβ42/40 ratio across the Alzheimer’s disease continuum

## Abstract

Introduction: Alzheimer's disease and Mild Cognitive Impairment are characterized by cognitive decline. Early diagnosis is crucial, and blood-based biomarkers offer a promising alternative. Methods: We searched MEDLINE (via PubMed), Embase, Web of Science, and Cochrane Library up to March 2025, following Preferred Reporting Items for Systematic Reviews and Meta-Analysis for diagnostic test accuracy (PRISMA-DTA) 2018 protocol. We included studies evaluating the diagnostic accuracy of plasma p-tau217 and Aβ42/40 for Alzheimer’s disease, using CSF or amyloid PET as comparison. Risk of bias was assessed using QUADAS-2. Statistical analysis was performed in STATA 14.2 using metandi and midas. Results: The diagnostic odds ratio was 52, and the area under the summary receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve was 0.94, suggesting strong overall diagnostic performance. Considerable heterogeneity was observed, with an inconsistency index (I²) of 98 percent. Sensitivity and specificity estimates varied between studies, and a strong threshold effect was identified, indicating that differences in diagnostic thresholds likely contributed to the observed variation. Conclusion: Plasma p-tau217 and Aβ42/40 ratio demonstrate strong potential for improving early diagnosis. These biomarkers could significantly impact timely interventions and help slow disease progression.
