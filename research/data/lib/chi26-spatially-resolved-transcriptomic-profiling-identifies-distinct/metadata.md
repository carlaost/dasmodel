## Abstract

Alzheimer's disease (AD) pathology frequently coexists with cerebral amyloid angiopathy (CAA), in which amyloid beta (Aβ) deposits in cerebral blood vessels. Although anti‐Aβ immunotherapies can reduce parenchymal plaques, they often exacerbate vascular pathology. This study investigates compartment‐specific microenvironmental responses to parenchymal versus vascular amyloid deposition.
