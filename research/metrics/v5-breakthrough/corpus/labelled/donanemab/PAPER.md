# Donanemab in Early Symptomatic Alzheimer Disease (TRAILBLAZER-ALZ 2)

## One-line summary
A phase 3, 18-month, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial (n=1736) showing that
donanemab, an antibody that clears brain amyloid plaque, significantly slowed clinical progression
of early symptomatic Alzheimer disease on both the iADRS and CDR-SB, with 23 of 24 prespecified
gated outcomes statistically significant.

## Why it is a breakthrough (positive label)
Donanemab is one of only a handful of disease-modifying anti-amyloid therapies to show a
statistically significant slowing of Alzheimer decline in a fully powered phase 3 trial. It
confirmed and scaled up (n=1736 vs the phase 2 n=257) the phase 2 iADRS signal, and its CDR-SB
treatment effect was numerically larger than the concurrently reported lecanemab CLARITY-AD result.
The trial supported the FDA approval pathway for anti-amyloid therapy and validated a
tau-PET-stratified enrollment strategy (low/medium vs high tau).

## Nature of the artifact
Primary empirical study: a pivotal phase 3 randomized clinical trial with a prespecified,
alpha-controlled hierarchy of 24 gated outcomes. Load-bearing quantities are the trial's own
measured effect sizes and safety event rates, reported verbatim in gro/quantities.yaml. Comparative
deltas (gro/delta_ledger.yaml) are drawn against two named prior anti-amyloid trials.
