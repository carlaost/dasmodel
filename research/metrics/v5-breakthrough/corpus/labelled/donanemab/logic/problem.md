# Problem

## The therapeutic problem
"There are limited efficacious treatments for Alzheimer disease" (Abstract, Importance). The trial
set out "to assess efficacy and adverse events of donanemab, an antibody designed to clear brain
amyloid plaque" (Abstract, Objective) in people with early symptomatic Alzheimer disease.

## Population and disease stage
Enrollment targeted "early symptomatic Alzheimer disease (mild cognitive impairment/mild dementia)
with amyloid and low/medium or high tau pathology based on positron emission tomography imaging"
(Abstract, Design). This is a biomarker-confirmed, tau-PET-stratified population - a design choice
that lets the trial test whether baseline tau burden modifies treatment benefit.

## What a positive result must show
A disease-modifying anti-amyloid antibody must slow the clinical (cognitive + functional) decline
of the disease relative to placebo, on a validated composite scale, with control of type I error
across a large prespecified outcome family. The primary outcome was "change in integrated
Alzheimer Disease Rating Scale (iADRS) score from baseline to 76 weeks (range, 0-144; lower scores
indicate greater impairment)"; a key secondary was "change in the sum of boxes of the Clinical
Dementia Rating Scale (CDR-SB) score (range, 0-18; higher scores indicate greater impairment)"
(Abstract, Main outcomes).

## Statistical framing
"There were 24 gated outcomes (primary, secondary, and exploratory)." "Statistical testing
allocated alpha of .04 to testing low/medium tau population outcomes, with the remainder (.01) for
combined population outcomes" (Abstract, Main outcomes). The prespecified low/medium-tau population
is the primary analysis population; the combined population is a secondary target.

## Design summary
"Multicenter (277 medical research centers/hospitals in 8 countries), randomized, double-blind,
placebo-controlled, 18-month phase 3 trial that enrolled 1736 participants ... from June 2020 to
November 2021 (last patient visit for primary outcome in April 2023)." Participants "were
randomized in a 1:1 ratio to receive donanemab (n = 860) or placebo (n = 876) intravenously every 4
weeks for 72 weeks," and "Participants in the donanemab group were switched to receive placebo in a
blinded manner if dose completion criteria were met" (Abstract, Design/Interventions).
