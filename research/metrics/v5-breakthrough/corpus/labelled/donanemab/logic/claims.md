# Claims (load-bearing)

All quotes are verbatim from the JAMA structured abstract (evidence/abstract.txt). Q-IDs point at
gro/quantities.yaml.

## C01 - Primary efficacy (iADRS), low/medium tau population
Donanemab significantly slowed decline on the primary outcome in the primary (low/medium tau)
population: LSM iADRS change "-6.02 (95% CI, -7.01 to -5.03) in the donanemab group and -9.27
(95% CI, -10.23 to -8.31) in the placebo group (difference, 3.25 [95% CI, 1.88-4.62]; P < .001)".
Grounds: Q01, Q02, Q03.

## C02 - Primary efficacy (iADRS), combined population
Effect held in the combined low/medium + high tau population: "-10.2 (95% CI, -11.22 to -9.16) with
donanemab and -13.1 (95% CI, -14.10 to -12.13) with placebo (difference, 2.92 [95% CI, 1.51-4.33];
P < .001)". Grounds: Q04, Q05, Q06.

## C03 - Secondary efficacy (CDR-SB), low/medium tau population
Donanemab slowed decline on CDR-SB: "1.20 (95% CI, 1.00-1.41) with donanemab and 1.88 (95% CI,
1.68-2.08) with placebo (difference, -0.67 [95% CI, -0.95 to -0.40]; P < .001)". Grounds: Q07, Q08, Q09.

## C04 - Secondary efficacy (CDR-SB), combined population
"1.72 (95% CI, 1.53-1.91) with donanemab and 2.42 (95% CI, 2.24-2.60) with placebo (difference,
-0.7 [95% CI, -0.95 to -0.45]; P < .001)". Grounds: Q10, Q11, Q12.

## C05 - Breadth/robustness of the outcome hierarchy
"Of the 24 gated outcomes, 23 were statistically significant." Grounds: Q13, Q14.

## C06 - Safety: ARIA-E
"Amyloid-related imaging abnormalities of edema or effusion occurred in 205 participants (24.0%; 52
symptomatic) in the donanemab group and 18 (2.1%; 0 symptomatic during study) in the placebo
group". Grounds: Q15, Q16, Q17.

## C07 - Safety: infusion-related reactions
"infusion-related reactions occurred in 74 participants (8.7%) with donanemab and 4 (0.5%) with
placebo". Grounds: Q18, Q19.

## C08 - Safety: treatment-related deaths
"Three deaths in the donanemab group and 1 in the placebo group were considered treatment related".
Grounds: Q20, Q21.

## C09 - Overall conclusion
"Among participants with early symptomatic Alzheimer disease and amyloid and tau pathology,
donanemab significantly slowed clinical progression at 76 weeks in those with low/medium tau and in
the combined low/medium and high tau pathology population" (Conclusions).

## C10 - Population / retention characterization
"Among 1736 randomized participants (mean age, 73.0 years; 996 [57.4%] women; 1182 [68.1%] with
low/medium tau pathology and 552 [31.8%] with high tau pathology), 1320 (76%) completed the trial."
Grounds: Q22, Q23, Q24, Q25, Q26, Q27.
