Measuring Cerebral Atrophy and White Matter Hyperintensity in AD. Abstract: whether baseline MRI predicts cognitive decline rate in AD; 84 AD patients, bicaudate ratio + Scheltens WMH, ~6mo intervals. Findings: greater atrophy -> faster decline (beta -0.316, P.04); WMH severity -> steeper decline (P.03); three-way interaction: highest WMH + greatest atrophy = most precipitous decline (synergy). Contribution: both structural measures have prognostic utility in early AD; supports cerebrovascular contribution to AD syndrome.
