The Amyloid Beta Ion Channel Hypothesis of Alzheimer's Disease (review). Abstract: reviews hypothesis that Abeta causes AD toxicity by forming aberrant ion channels in neuronal membranes disrupting homeostasis. Findings: Abeta increases intracellular calcium; forms cation-selective channels in artificial membranes; zinc/metals block channel activity; intracellular Abeta accumulates before plaques. Contribution: synthesizes evidence into an integrative Abeta ion-channel framework; identifies gaps (validated mostly in artificial systems, lacks whole-cell patch-clamp in AD neurons).
