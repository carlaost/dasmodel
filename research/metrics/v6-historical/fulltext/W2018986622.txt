Abstract: Proposes that joint actions of common gene variants within pathways play a major role in predisposing to complex diseases. Tested polymorphisms in the axon guidance pathway relative to Parkinson disease. Findings: models predicted PD susceptibility OR=90.8 (p=4.64e-38); survival free of PD HR=19.0 (p=5.43e-48); age at onset R2=0.68 (p=1.68e-51); validated in a second dataset (OR>100) and by gene expression (39% of pathway genes differentially expressed). Contribution: claims a paradigm shift from single-gene to pathway studies of complex diseases; joint pathway effects yield larger effect sizes; proposes applicability to AD, diabetes, cancers.
