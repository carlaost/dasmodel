Abstract: APP, through amyloid beta production, causes an imbalance of mitochondrial fission/fusion that results in mitochondrial fragmentation and abnormal distribution, contributing to mitochondrial and neuronal dysfunction. Findings: DLP1 and OPA1 decreased, Fis1 increased in APP-overexpressing cells; ~40% (WT APP) and >80% (Swedish mutant) showed fragmented mitochondria with perinuclear accumulation; elevated ROS, decreased membrane potential, reduced ATP; OPA1 rescued fragmentation/function but not distribution; DLP1 corrected distribution not fragmentation; confirmed in primary hippocampal neurons. Contribution: identifies abnormal mitochondrial dynamics as a mechanistic link between amyloid-beta pathology and neuronal dysfunction in AD; potential therapeutic target.
