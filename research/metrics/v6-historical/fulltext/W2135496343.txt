Abstract: To identify predictors of AD versus frontotemporal lobar degeneration pathology in primary progressive aphasia (PPA), and whether AD pathology is atypically distributed to fit the aphasic phenotype. Findings: all six agrammatic PPA cases had FTLD (five tauopathy); 7/11 logopenic cases had AD; lower memory scores increased AD probability with exceptions; ApoE4 did not predict AD pathology in PPA; PPA/AD showed entorhinal NFT typical of amnestic AD; neocortical NFTs more numerous in left hemisphere but asymmetry low/inconsistent. Contribution: challenges the assumption that AD pathology in PPA necessarily causes the aphasic presentation; some cases involve concurrent processes initiating language dysfunction.
