Problem Behavior of Dementia Patients Predicts Low-Grade Hypercoagulability in Spousal Caregivers. Abstract: 108 spousal AD caregivers; whether dementia problem behaviors + caregiver negative reactions predict blood clotting abnormalities. Findings: greater problem-behavior frequency (dR2=0.046,p.014) and negative reactions (dR2=0.044,p.017) independently predicted increased procoagulant activity controlling for CV risk; problem behaviors predicted D-dimer (p.010); memory/depressive behaviors stronger than disruptive. Contribution: empirical link between specific caregiving behavioral stressors and thrombogenic changes; implications for caregiver cardiovascular health.
